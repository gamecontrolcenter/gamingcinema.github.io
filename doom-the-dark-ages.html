About the Game

Doom: The Dark Ages is id Software's prequel to Doom (2016) and Doom Eternal, set in a medieval techno-fantasy era of the Doom universe. The Slayer fights for the Maykrs in armies of demonic invaders across war-torn realms, wielding a redesigned Shield Saw, the Mace, and reimagined firearms. The walkthrough is a single full-game playthrough on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary — pure cinematic carnage at PS5 Pro fidelity.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Village of Khalim

    The campaign opens with the Doom Slayer awakened from stasis on Argent D'Nur — the alien planet of the Night Sentinels — by the Maykrs, the angel-tier beings who control the Slayer's actions through the neural-control device called the Tether. The Slayer's first mission is the defense of the Village of Khalim, a Night Sentinel border settlement under active demonic invasion from Hell's expanding war.

    The opening hour is structured as a sustained tutorial-paced introduction to the campaign's medieval-techno aesthetic and the franchise's reworked combat fundamentals. The Slayer's new combat repertoire is anchored by the Shield Saw — a saw-bladed shield that replaces DOOM Eternal's shoulder-mounted Flame Belch as the campaign's primary defensive tool, supporting both parry mechanics and projectile defense. The chapter introduces parrying as the campaign's central combat verb, with green-flashing enemy projectiles signaling parry windows that turn defense into offensive opportunity.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's tonal pivot. Where DOOM Eternal was a high-mobility platforming-shooter hybrid, The Dark Ages is mechanically slower, with the Slayer rendered as an unstoppable tank rather than an acrobatic predator. The chapter ends with the Village of Khalim's beachhead secured, the demonic invasion's tactical objective destroyed, and the campaign's first sustained encounter against a Titan-class demon staged as the chapter's climactic boss fight.

  2. 2

    2. Hebeth

    The campaign's second chapter takes the Slayer into Hebeth's Weapons Facility, a Night Sentinel manufacturing complex that has been compromised by demonic corruption from within. The chapter is structured as a multi-floor research-lab infiltration, with the campaign's first sustained interior environments and the introduction of additional weaponry — the Combat Shotgun with its parry-trigger reload, the Pulverizer with its grindable bone-fed ammunition, and the early versions of the campaign's signature Skullcrusher (a chaingun that fires skull fragments).

    The chapter introduces the campaign's central environmental storytelling. Hebeth's facility logs reveal that the Maykrs' control over Night Sentinel R&D extends far beyond what King Novik's court openly acknowledges, with the early seeds of the campaign's central political conspiracy embedded in lab transcripts and recovered audio.

    The chapter ends with the demonic corruption traced to its source in the facility's research basement, the Slayer recovering the Shield Saw's first major upgrade, and an Aggadon Champion confrontation staged as the chapter's climactic boss.

  3. 3

    3. Barrier Core

    The campaign's most distinctive early-chapter design. The entire chapter is staged inside the cockpit of the Atlan — a massive Night Sentinel mech roughly the height of a skyscraper, deployed against demon Titans of similar scale. The chapter is structured as a sustained giant-mech combat sequence, with the Atlan's repertoire (missiles, shoulder-mounted artillery, melee combat) introduced through tutorial-paced encounters.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's scale shift. DOOM has never staged combat at this scale — the Atlan punches Titan-class demons through fortress walls, with city-block-sized impacts and destructible environmental geography. The mech missions become one of the campaign's most acclaimed design innovations.

    The chapter ends with the Barrier Core's central reactor secured, the demon Titan threatening the region defeated, and the Slayer dismounting from the Atlan to continue on foot.

  4. 4

    4. Sentinel Barracks

    The campaign's first chapter to introduce optional combat challenges. The Slayer arrives at a fortified Night Sentinel barracks complex under siege by a coordinated demonic assault force. The chapter is structured as a sustained defensive combat sequence across multiple barracks chambers, with the campaign's first sustained encounter with Battle Knights — heavily armored demon lieutenants that demand parry-and-counter mastery rather than direct-fire combat.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's challenge system. Each challenge — killing forty demons, executing three-hit melee combos, finding hidden gold caches — unlocks weapon skins, ruby currency, and gold upgrades. The system encourages replays and provides the campaign's primary post-completion engagement loop.

    The chapter ends with the barracks secured, the demonic assault repulsed, and the path to the campaign's central political setting opened.

  5. 5

    5. The Holy City of Aratum

    The campaign's political center. Aratum is the capital city of the Night Sentinels and the seat of King Novik, the Sentinels' ceremonial monarch. The chapter is structured as a sustained urban-defense sequence, with the demon invasion penetrating the city's outer walls and the Slayer working alongside Night Sentinel forces to push them back. The chapter weaves the campaign's largest sustained combat scenarios with the introduction of the Doomslayer's parry-counter system's full repertoire.

    The chapter introduces Commander Thira, the Night Sentinels' field commander and the campaign's primary supporting protagonist. Thira's introduction is staged through a sustained command-tent sequence in which she briefs the Slayer on the demonic forces' strategic objective: the Heart of Argent, an artifact of immense power housed within Aratum's central temple complex.

    The chapter dwells on Aratum's architectural aesthetic. The medieval-techno fusion of Night Sentinel design — gothic cathedrals laced with energy conduits, banners hung over plasma turret emplacements — has become one of the campaign's most acclaimed visual achievements. The chapter ends with the city's outer defenses secured but the demonic invasion's broader objective unrevealed.

  6. 6

    6. Siege — Part 1

    The campaign's first major spectacle chapter. Prince Ahzrak, the leader of Hell and the campaign's primary antagonist, launches a coordinated assault on Aratum with the explicit goal of seizing the Heart of Argent. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage defensive sequence — the Slayer mans a turret position to engage an armored Titan, falls back to the hangar to rescue King Novik, and conducts sustained urban combat across the city's collapsing infrastructure.

    The chapter introduces Prince Ahzrak in person for the first time. Ahzrak is rendered as a tall, regal demon prince with deliberately elegant combat animations, contrasting sharply with the brute-force tone of the franchise's prior demon antagonists. His brief on-screen appearance is staged as a personal challenge to the Slayer, with Ahzrak vowing to acquire the Heart of Argent regardless of the Slayer's intervention.

    The chapter ends with King Novik secured, the immediate demonic assault repulsed, and the broader siege now committed to its second phase.

  7. 7

    7. Siege — Part 2

    The siege's second phase. The demonic forces, repulsed at the city's outer walls, breach through Aratum's underground tunnel network and emerge inside the city. The chapter is structured as a sustained interior combat sequence through the city's barracks complex, with the Slayer fighting to reach the Forest Gate that connects Aratum to the surrounding wilderness.

    The chapter weaves combat with the campaign's most sustained Battle Knight encounters and the introduction of the Aggadon Champion — a recurring mid-tier boss class with sustained parry-and-counter mechanics. The chapter's climactic boss confrontation against an Aggadon Champion in the barracks' central armory is the campaign's first major mechanical test.

    The chapter ends with the Forest Gate opened, the path to the Mine Entrance beyond it cleared, and the campaign's central thread now committed to the broader regional defense.

  8. 8

    8. Abyssal Forest

    The campaign's first sustained natural-biome chapter. The Slayer enters the Abyssal Forest, a corrupted woodland where the demonic invasion has been operating from outside the Night Sentinels' surveillance. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence, with the campaign's most acclaimed map design — a multi-tier forest with hidden secrets, optional combat arenas, and the campaign's first sustained collectible density.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's environmental atmosphere. The Abyssal Forest is rendered in deep gold and purple tones, with twisted trees, demonic shrines, and the campaign's most acclaimed soundtrack cues. The combat density is moderate; the chapter's focus is exploration and the discovery of side content.

    The chapter ends with the forest's central demonic shrine destroyed, the Ancestral Heart quest objective revealed, and the path to the Ancestral Forge opened.

  9. 9

    9. Ancestral Forge

    The campaign's mid-game upgrade chapter. The Slayer activates the Ancestral Heart — a massive Night Sentinel forge powered by the same energy that fuels the Heart of Argent — and acquires his first Shield Rune, a permanent upgrade to the campaign's defensive repertoire. The chapter is structured as a multi-tier platform-traversal sequence with sustained combat against Hell's incoming reinforcements.

    The chapter introduces the Shield Rune progression system. Each Shield Rune the Slayer acquires across the campaign expands the Shield Saw's repertoire — bash attacks, projectile-reflect modes, parry-trigger explosions, and the campaign's signature Charged Lock-On that allows the Slayer to fire concentrated energy attacks at distant enemies.

    The chapter ends with the first Shield Rune acquired, the Ancestral Forge's secrets accessible for return visits, and the campaign's mid-act objective — the assault on Ahzrak's Fortress — fully staged.

  10. 10

    10. The Forsaken Plains

    The campaign's largest open-zone chapter. The Forsaken Plains is a sweeping war-torn plateau between the Sentinel territories and Hell's encroaching forces. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence across one of the campaign's most expansive maps, with multiple optional combat arenas, hidden mini-bosses, and the campaign's most extensive collectible network.

    The chapter dwells on the Slayer's growing tactical autonomy. The Maykrs' Tether control — established in the opening chapter as the Slayer's defining constraint — visibly weakens as the Slayer's growing combat experience strains its operational parameters. The chapter's audio logs reveal that the Maykrs themselves are growing concerned about the Slayer's behavioral drift.

    The chapter ends with the Forsaken Plains secured, the path to Ahzrak's Fortress opened, and the campaign's mid-third-act assault committed.

  11. 11

    11. Hellbreaker

    The campaign's second Atlan mech chapter. The Slayer pilots the giant mech across the Forsaken Plains toward Ahzrak's Fortress, with the chapter's combat staged at the same massive scale as Chapter 3. The chapter weaves Atlan combat with sustained on-foot infiltration sequences inside the fortress's interior chambers.

    The chapter's central objective is the destruction of the Hell Cannons that protect the fortress's primary gate. The mech combat is the campaign's most sustained and one of its most acclaimed sequences. The chapter ends with the fortress breached, the Hell Cannons disabled, and the Slayer's first sustained on-foot infiltration into Hell's territory imminent.

  12. 12

    12. Sentinel Command Station

    A perspective-shift chapter that returns to Aratum's territory after the Slayer's initial Hell incursion. The Sentinel Command Station has come under unexpected attack from a cosmic-scale entity — a paranatural creature that has emerged from outside the demon invasion's expected vectors. The chapter is structured as a sustained defensive sequence inside the station's multi-level interior.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's first cosmic-scale enemy class. The cosmic creature attacks through demonic portals, tentacle weak spots, and zone-denial AOE attacks that demand the Slayer's full repertoire to manage. The chapter dwells on the campaign's escalating cosmology — the demon war is not the only threat to Argent D'Nur, and the broader paranatural framework of the DOOM universe is now visibly active.

    The chapter ends with the cosmic creature defeated, the Sentinel Command Station secured, and the campaign's mid-third-act broader threats fully revealed.

  13. 13

    13. From Beyond

    The campaign's lockdown-escape chapter. The Slayer awakens inside a Maykr-controlled facility, with the Tether's control visibly attempting to reassert dominance over his actions. The chapter is structured as a sustained interior infiltration with the campaign's first sustained encounter against Maykr-controlled enforcers.

    The chapter's central objective is to find a way out of the lockdown and reach Serrat, the Slayer's cyber-dragon companion now revealed for the first time. The chapter weaves combat with environmental traversal across the facility's interior, with the Hangar Key Card acquisition as the chapter's central progression objective.

    The chapter ends with Serrat liberated, the dragon-mount traversal system fully unlocked, and the campaign's broader aerial-combat repertoire now active. The chapter's quiet revelation is that the Slayer has been a Maykr captive for longer than the campaign's opening framing implied.

  14. 14

    14. Spire of Nerathul

    The campaign's central pivot chapter. The Slayer pilots Serrat to the Spire of Nerathul — a massive Old One artifact located in a contested cosmic zone — to drain the water levels and deactivate the Stasis Emitters that have been suppressing the spire's core. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage puzzle-combat sequence with sustained dragon-combat aerial set-pieces.

    The chapter's central objective is the acquisition of the BFC (Big Cosmic something — the campaign's parallel to the franchise's iconic BFG) and the release of The Old One — an ancient being who has been imprisoned beneath the spire across centuries. The Old One's release becomes the campaign's mid-third-act pivot.

    The chapter ends with the BFC acquired, The Old One released, and the path to Thira's location in the Cosmic Realm now clear.

  15. 15

    15. City of Ry'uul

    A multi-island exploration chapter set across the cosmic-realm city of Ry'uul. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence across several interconnected island districts, with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental storytelling and the introduction of new enemy variants drawn from the cosmic-realm's distinct enemy bestiary.

    The chapter's central objective is the gathering of Relic Fragments scattered across the city's districts, which the Slayer must assemble to reach the Temple of Lomarith in a later chapter. The chapter dwells on the cosmic realm's aesthetic — a fusion of medieval Sentinel architecture with cosmic-scale geometries that the campaign has been building toward since the Sentinel Command Station.

    The chapter ends with the Relic Fragments collected, the path to the Temple of Lomarith mapped, and the campaign's broader cosmic-realm questline fully committed.

  16. 16

    16. The Kar'Thul Marshes

    A sustained natural-biome chapter set in the Kar'Thul Marshes — a corrupted wetland region between the cosmic realm and the demonic territories. The chapter is structured as a multi-zone exploration sequence with the campaign's hardest sustained combat encounters and the introduction of new enemy variants adapted to the marsh's waterlogged terrain.

    The chapter weaves combat with sustained environmental puzzles, including water-level manipulation and the campaign's most distinctive parry-and-traversal combinations. The chapter ends with the marshes traversed, the path to the Temple of Lomarith now directly accessible, and the campaign's third-act assault on Hell's leadership now positioned.

  17. 17

    17. Temple of Lomarith

    The campaign's mid-third-act ancient-temple chapter. The Temple of Lomarith is a pre-Sentinel cosmic-realm structure that contains key intelligence on Prince Ahzrak's actual objective. The chapter is structured as a sustained interior exploration with the campaign's most acclaimed puzzle sequences and the introduction of the Witch — the campaign's secondary antagonist who has been operating alongside Ahzrak in the shadows.

    The chapter's central reveal is the Heart of Argent's true power source: Commander Thira carries the soul of Wraith, an ancient being whose fragmented essence is the Heart's actual operational core. Thira's role in the campaign is reframed — she is not merely Aratum's field commander but the campaign's central macguffin.

    The chapter ends with the Temple of Lomarith's intelligence recovered, the Witch's identity confirmed, and the campaign's final assault on Hell's command structure committed.

  18. 18

    18. Belly of the Beast

    The campaign's most distinctive single-chapter design. The Slayer is swallowed by a massive ancient cosmic beast — an enemy class the campaign has staged across previous chapters at smaller scale — and the entire chapter takes place inside the creature's body. The chapter is structured as a sustained interior-organic exploration, with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental design and the introduction of biological combat scenarios distinct from anything in the franchise's prior installments.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's tonal commitment. Where prior chapters have weighed combat against environmental texture, "Belly of the Beast" foregrounds the campaign's most experimental design choices — destructible biological structures, ammunition synthesized from the creature's interior fluids, and the chapter's climactic escape sequence staged as the Slayer carves his way out from inside.

    The chapter ends with the cosmic beast destroyed from within, the Slayer ejected back into the campaign's geography, and the path to the campaign's emotional pivot chapter — Harbor of Souls — now clear.

  19. 19

    19. Harbor of Souls

    The campaign's most thematically loaded chapter. The Slayer descends into a representation of the afterlife — a cosmic-realm zone between life and death — in pursuit of essential knowledge before the final assault. The chapter is structured as a sustained narrative-led exploration with reduced combat density and the campaign's most extensive lore reveals.

    The chapter's central reveal is the broader campaign cosmology. The Slayer is not merely a Maykr-controlled super-weapon. He is an ancient being whose history predates the current war, whose memory has been suppressed by the Maykrs' Tether across multiple resurrections, and whose true origin connects to the broader paranatural framework of the DOOM universe. The chapter dwells on this reveal in extended cutscene sequences.

    The chapter ends with the Slayer's broader history restored to his memory, the Tether's control permanently broken, and the campaign's final assault now committed with the protagonist fully autonomous.

  20. 20

    20. Resurrection

    The campaign's third-act pivot. The Slayer, now fully free from Maykr control, returns to Argent D'Nur to find the Night Sentinels in catastrophic retreat. Ahzrak, having captured Commander Thira and extracted the Wraith's soul from her, has weaponized the Heart of Argent's full power for a final assault on the Sentinels. Kreed Maykr, revealed as a traitor working in coordination with Ahzrak, has accelerated the betrayal.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained defensive combat sequence across Aratum's collapsing infrastructure, with the Slayer pushing back the demonic assault to repel the immediate threat. The chapter weaves combat with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental destruction sequences, with much of Aratum's signature architecture visibly collapsing across the chapter's runtime.

    The chapter's mid-section boss fight is the confrontation with Kreed Maykr, the traitor whose betrayal accelerated Hell's advance. The fight is the campaign's most personal antagonist encounter, with Kreed wielding Maykr technology against the Slayer he once controlled. The chapter ends with Kreed defeated, the immediate Aratum defense secured, and the path to Ahzrak's final stand opened.

  21. 21

    21. Final Battle

    The campaign's penultimate chapter. The Slayer pilots the Atlan mech for the third and final time, breaching Ahzrak's central fortress for the campaign's climactic boss confrontation. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage assault — mech combat against the fortress's defensive infrastructure, on-foot infiltration through the interior, and the climactic first phase of the Ahzrak boss fight.

    The Ahzrak fight is the campaign's mechanical apex. The Prince of Hell's combat repertoire combines every enemy archetype the campaign has staged across the prior twenty chapters — Battle Knight melee, Titan-scale projectile attacks, Witch-affiliated dark magic — into a sustained boss confrontation. Ahzrak resurrects multiple times during the fight, each time reframing his combat patterns and forcing the player to recalibrate.

    The chapter ends with Ahzrak temporarily defeated, Thira's soul released from the Heart of Argent, and Hell's immediate momentum crippled. The chapter explicitly refuses to call the campaign concluded — Ahzrak's broader resurrection remains active, and the campaign's final confrontation is staged for the closing chapter.

  22. 22

    22. Reckoning

    The campaign's climactic chapter. The Slayer enters the Hell dimension itself for the final confrontation with Prince Ahzrak and the Witch together. The chapter is structured as a sustained boss-rush sequence inside Hell's most distinctive arenas, with the combined Ahzrak-Witch fight serving as the campaign's mechanical and thematic apex.

    The combined boss confrontation is one of the franchise's most acclaimed encounters. Ahzrak and the Witch fight in coordinated patterns, with the Witch providing dark-magic support to Ahzrak's melee assaults while Ahzrak's resurrection mechanics force the Slayer to balance damage between the two antagonists. The fight ends with Ahzrak decapitated by the Slayer in a sequence staged with the franchise's signature brutal finisher animation.

    The chapter's epilogue is staged in the aftermath. Thira and her Sentinel forces — surrounded by an overwhelming demonic army — receive the Slayer's grim reaper-style intervention as he throws Ahzrak's decapitated head down from above into the demonic formation, breaking their advance. The war is won. The Maykrs publicly disavow Kreed's betrayal, but the damage has been done — Thira urges the surviving Night Sentinels to trust only in themselves going forward.

    The chapter's final scene establishes the campaign's connection to the broader franchise. The Slayer, now fully free from Maykr control, takes Kreed's ship and departs Argent D'Nur on his own personal crusade against Hell — the direct setup for DOOM (2016)'s opening hour. The credits roll over the Slayer's departure, with the campaign's central question — whether one being can truly end the eternal war — answered in the only way the franchise permits: through sustained, individual, brutal commitment.
