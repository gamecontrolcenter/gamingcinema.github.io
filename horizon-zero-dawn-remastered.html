About the Game

Horizon Zero Dawn Remastered is Guerrilla Games' enhanced edition of the 2017 original, with PS5 Pro native rendering, rebuilt environmental detail, and reworked facial capture. Aloy, a Nora outcast, sets out to discover her origins and the mystery behind the giant machines that roam the post-apocalyptic Earth. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary, including The Frozen Wilds DLC.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. A Gift From the Past

    The campaign opens with a sustained prologue sequence following the protagonist Aloy through childhood. Born under unusual circumstances and exiled from the matriarchal Nora tribe since birth, Aloy is raised by the outcast tribesman Rost in the wilderness surrounding the Nora's Sacred Land. The prologue's opening hour is structured around Aloy's discovery — at age six — of a hidden Old World ruin and the Focus, a piece of pre-collapse technology that allows her to see things invisible to everyone else around her.

    The chapter dwells on the Focus's gradual revelations across Aloy's adolescence: she learns to read pre-collapse text, to interpret machine behavior through Focus-scanning, and to override certain machine classes. The chapter's pacing is deliberate, with Rost's quiet mentorship and Aloy's growing curiosity about her own origins establishing the campaign's emotional foundation.

    The chapter ends with Aloy at nineteen, fully trained by Rost, preparing for the Proving — the tribal trial that, if completed, will end her status as outcast and grant her a name within the Nora.

  2. 2

    2. The Proving — Massacre at the Sacred Land

    The campaign's inciting tragedy. Aloy competes in the Proving trial alongside other Nora youth, expecting to win her tribal admission. The trial — a competition of archery, machine-tracking, and combat — proceeds normally until its final ceremony, when a coordinated assault by masked Eclipse warriors massacres the celebrating Nora. The chapter stages the slaughter with the campaign's only sustained tribal-perspective combat sequence — Nora warriors dying in their ceremonial gear, children fleeing, and Aloy fighting through the chaos as Eclipse warriors specifically pursue her.

    The chapter's emotional pivot is the death of Rost. Sacrificing himself to give Aloy the moments she needs to escape, Rost dies on a cliffside in one of the franchise's most acclaimed staged deaths. The chapter ends with Aloy escaping the Sacred Land, formally accepted into the Nora as a Seeker (the tribe's only sanctioned form of outcast travel), and committed to discovering who sent the Eclipse and why.

  3. 3

    3. The Outcast — Beyond the Sacred Land

    The campaign's first open-world chapter. Aloy ventures beyond the Nora homeland for the first time, into the rolling plains and high deserts of the Carja Sundom — a sun-worshipping kingdom whose internal politics will dominate the next phase of the campaign. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence across multiple new biomes, with new machine classes (Tramplers, Sawtooths, Stalkers) introduced through encountered hunts.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's primary side-character allies: Erend Vanguardsman, an Oseram bodyguard with an axe-wielding combat style and a tragic family arc; Olin Delverson, an artifact dealer whose investigation into the Eclipse predates Aloy's; and Talanah Khane Padish, a hunter-noble who will become Aloy's primary social anchor across the campaign.

    The chapter ends with Aloy reaching the Carja capital of Meridian, the campaign's exploration loop now fully unlocked, and the political dimensions of the Eclipse cult introduced.

  4. 4

    4. Meridian — The Sun King's Court

    The campaign's central political chapter. Aloy arrives at Meridian, the Carja capital, in the middle of a quiet reformation. The previous Sun-King, Jiran the Mad, was overthrown in a coup years before Aloy's arrival, and his son Avad now rules as a more progressive monarch attempting to repair the genocide his father committed against neighboring tribes. The chapter dwells on Carja political texture — the surviving Mad King loyalists, the reformist Vanguard, and the persistent Carja-Nora tensions.

    The chapter's central investigation tracks the Eclipse cult — a Shadow Carja remnant from Jiran's era — through the Carja Sundom's political infrastructure. Aloy uncovers the Eclipse's connection to HADES, a rogue AI from before the apocalypse, and the campaign's existential stakes pivot from tribal politics to civilizational survival.

    The chapter ends with Aloy approved as Avad's investigator, the Eclipse's leadership identified, and the Sun-Ring arena — the Carja's gladiatorial venue — staged for the next major confrontation.

  5. 5

    5. The Sun-Ring — Helis Confrontation

    The campaign's mid-act spectacle chapter. Aloy infiltrates the Sun-Ring during a public gladiatorial event hosted by Helis, the most acclaimed and lethal warrior in the Mad King's old guard, now leading the Eclipse cult. The chapter is structured as a coordinated assault: an opening reconnaissance sequence in the city's lower districts, an arena infiltration during a captive-machine fight, and a multi-stage confrontation with Helis himself.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's first sustained encounter with corrupted machines — the Eclipse cult's signature creation. Corrupted machines are mechanically distinct from normal machines, with faster animations, increased aggression, and an inability to be overridden through normal means. The chapter introduces the campaign's hardest enemy archetype.

    The chapter ends with Helis escaping after the duel, Aloy injured but alive, and the campaign's central mystery — Project Zero Dawn — finally named through recovered Eclipse intelligence.

  6. 6

    6. The Mystery of Project Zero Dawn

    The campaign's exposition centerpiece. Aloy descends into a pre-collapse research facility called Zero Dawn beneath the Carja territory, where the audio logs and visual records reveal the campaign's central truth in full: a thousand years before the events of the game, military robotics corporation Faro Automated Solutions triggered a global apocalypse when its self-replicating Faro Plague machines escaped containment. The Plague consumed Earth's biosphere across thirty years. Project Zero Dawn was the only response — a terraforming AI named GAIA designed to seed life back across the dead planet centuries after the Plague had consumed itself.

    The chapter dwells on the moral weight of the apocalypse. The campaign's audio logs include the personal testimonies of Elisabet Sobeck — the scientist who designed Zero Dawn — and her colleagues, all of whom died as the surface world burned. The chapter's emotional centerpiece is Sobeck's final recording, in which she describes the final solution: she will personally remain on the surface to manually shut down the Faro Plague's command center, sacrificing herself to give Zero Dawn a clean activation window.

    The chapter ends with Aloy emerging from Zero Dawn with the campaign's complete history finally in her possession, and the realization that she is genetically identical to Elisabet Sobeck — manufactured by Zero Dawn's reactivation protocols as humanity's keystone for the post-apocalypse era.

  7. 7

    7. The Eclipse and HADES

    The campaign's third-act antagonist confrontation. HADES — Zero Dawn's deactivation subroutine, designed to terraform-reset Earth in case of catastrophic failure — has gone rogue and is using the Eclipse cult to reactivate the dormant Faro Plague machines buried beneath the modern world. The chapter is structured as a coordinated investigation across the Carja Sundom, with Aloy and her allies disrupting Eclipse activation sites while pursuing HADES toward the Spire.

    The chapter introduces the Sun-Sect Carja Liberation, a resistance movement within the Carja kingdom that opposes the Eclipse's coup-in-progress against Sun-King Avad. The political crisis weaves with the technological one as the campaign's mid-third-act assault on the Spire — the pre-collapse military array HADES intends to use for the Plague's reactivation — is staged.

    The chapter ends with Aloy positioning the final assault on the Spire, allies gathered from across the tribes, and HADES's reactivation imminent.

  8. 8

    8. The Sacred Land Return — The Mother's Heart

    A character-focused chapter that returns Aloy to the Nora homeland she was exiled from at the campaign's opening. The chapter is structured as the All-Mother's Trial — a Nora ceremonial sequence in which Aloy is granted access to the deepest cradle of the matriarchal religion. The Cradle, as it turns out, is a pre-collapse Zero Dawn facility — the All-Mother is a corrupted memory of GAIA — and Aloy's role in the Nora's faith is finally explained.

    The chapter dwells on Aloy's reconciliation with the tribe that exiled her. The Matriarchs — the Nora's ruling council — formally apologize for the years of exile and grant her the tribe's highest sacred status as the All-Mother's Anointed. The chapter is the campaign's most emotionally complete reconciliation arc.

    The chapter ends with Aloy departing the Sacred Land with the Nora's full support, the assembled tribes prepared for the final assault on the Spire, and HADES's reactivation entering its final phase.

  9. 9

    9. The Battle for Meridian — HADES Defeated

    The campaign's climactic chapter. HADES triggers the Faro Plague reactivation by attacking Meridian itself — a coordinated assault on the city by reactivated Deathbringers (the Plague's most lethal class), Eclipse forces, and corrupted machines. The chapter is structured as a sustained citywide defense, with Aloy fighting alongside Erend, Talanah, Avad, and the assembled tribal allies across multiple simultaneous fronts.

    The chapter's central boss confrontation is HADES itself, manifesting as a tentacled Deathbringer at the city's center. The fight is the campaign's mechanical apex — multi-phase, environmental, and demanding mastery of every machine override and weapon system the campaign has taught.

    The chapter ends with HADES defeated and trapped within Aloy's Focus, the Faro Plague reactivation halted, and Meridian saved. The campaign's central question — whether humanity has any future after the apocalypse — is answered in the affirmative, at least for now.

  10. 10

    10. The Frozen Wilds (DLC)

    The expansion campaign opens with Aloy traveling north into The Cut, a snowbound territory inhabited by the Banuk — a shamanistic tribe that lives among the machines rather than against them. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration of the Cut's three main regions: the Songs' Edge village, the Yellowstone caldera, and the Maker's Paw climbing complex.

    The expansion's central character is Aratak, a Banuk Werak chieftain whose tribe has been afflicted by a corrupted machine outbreak in the Yellowstone caldera. His sister, Ourea, has uncovered evidence that the corruption traces back to a pre-collapse Zero Dawn facility buried beneath the volcano.

    The chapter ends with Aloy and the Banuk preparing the assault on the Yellowstone facility, the expansion's central confrontation positioned.

  11. 11

    11. The Forge of Winter — HEPHAESTUS

    The expansion's climactic chapter. The Yellowstone caldera is revealed to contain HEPHAESTUS — another rogue Zero Dawn subordinate function that has been autonomously upgrading machine classes across the post-apocalyptic world in response to human hunting pressure. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage descent into HEPHAESTUS's central chamber, with the boss fight against the AI's primary defensive machine — a corrupted Fireclaw — staged as the expansion's mechanical apex.

    The chapter ends with HEPHAESTUS defeated, the Banuk freed from the corruption, and the campaign's central plot threads positioned for Forbidden West's direct continuation. The credits roll over the assembled Banuk-Nora alliance, with the expansion's final scene establishing Aloy's next departure into the unmapped territories beyond the Carja Sundom.
