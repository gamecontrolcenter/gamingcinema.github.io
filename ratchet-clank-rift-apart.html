About the Game

Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart is Insomniac Games' PS5 launch-window title showcasing the SSD with instantaneous dimensional rift transitions. Ratchet and Clank confront Dr. Nefarious in a multiverse-spanning adventure that introduces Rivet, a Lombax resistance fighter from an alternate dimension. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary, with weapons preserved at maximum visual fidelity.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. The Galactic Parade — Corson V

    The campaign opens with Ratchet and Clank being honored at a galactic parade in Megalopolis, the central city of the planet Corson V. The opening sequence is structured as a sustained celebration set-piece, with the parade weaving through Megalopolis's skyline and the player controlling Ratchet in a sequence of comedic floats, fireworks, and demonstrations of the franchise's signature weapons. The chapter is the most spectacle-heavy opening in the franchise's history, with Insomniac's PS5-exclusive hardware showcased through dense environmental detail, real-time global illumination, and the campaign's signature near-instant level loading.

    The chapter's central narrative event is Clank's gift to Ratchet — the Dimensionator, a device built to open portals to alternate dimensions, designed specifically to help Ratchet find his lost Lombax homeworld. The Lombaxes, Ratchet's species, were exiled to a parallel dimension during a war centuries ago. Clank has spent the years between games secretly constructing the Dimensionator as the franchise's most personal gift.

    The chapter's pivot is the Nefarious assault. Dr. Nefarious — the franchise's recurring antagonist, presumed defeated in Into the Nexus — crashes the parade aboard a massive war machine and steals the Dimensionator before Ratchet can use it. The device is damaged in the resulting chase, opening uncontrolled dimensional rifts across the galaxy. Ratchet, Clank, and Nefarious are scattered into different dimensions as the chapter cuts to black.

  2. 2

    2. Crash on Sargasso — Meeting Rivet

    The campaign's central character introduction. Ratchet, having fallen through a dimensional rift, wakes up alone on the swampy frontier planet Sargasso, in a dimension where the original Ratchet's heroics never happened and Emperor Nefarious has conquered the galaxy. The chapter is structured as a sustained tutorial-paced exploration of Sargasso's wetlands, with the campaign's reworked combat fundamentals fully introduced — phantom dash mobility, the wall-running mechanic, and the rift-tether grapple that allows the player to dimensionally hop between combat positions.

    The chapter introduces Rivet, the campaign's secondary playable protagonist. A female Lombax from the Nefarious dimension, Rivet has spent her entire life resisting Emperor Nefarious's regime alongside a scattered network of rebel survivors. Her introduction is staged with deliberate parallels to Ratchet's — same species, same general loadout, radically different personality (more cynical, more battle-tested, more guarded). The chapter dwells on Ratchet and Rivet's first joint combat sequence against Goon-class enforcers, the chapter's primary antagonist faction.

    The chapter ends with Ratchet and Rivet's first conversation, the campaign's central premise (fix the Dimensionator, get everyone home) committed, and the journey toward Rivet's resistance base established.

  3. 3

    3. Megalopolis — Back to Nefarious City

    A perspective-pivot chapter that returns to Corson V — but in the Nefarious dimension, where Megalopolis has been renamed Nefarious City and is rendered as a dystopian neon-purple imperial capital. The chapter is structured as a sustained urban-infiltration sequence with Ratchet and Rivet attempting to recover Dimensionator fragments from the city's central plaza.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's signature dimensional rift combat mechanics. Mid-fight, rifts open spontaneously across the arena, allowing Ratchet and Rivet to tether-grapple between dimensional positions in real time. The mechanic transforms standard arena combat into the franchise's most acclaimed mobility-driven sequences, with the PS5's loading capabilities enabling instant transitions between dimensions during single combat encounters.

    The chapter ends with the first Dimensionator fragment recovered, Emperor Nefarious's military command structure observed at scale, and the campaign's central political stakes — that an entire dimension lives under Nefarious's regime — fully active.

  4. 4

    4. Savali — The Lombax Library

    The campaign's emotional anchor chapter. Savali is a desert planet that, in the Nefarious dimension, contains the Lombax Library — a hidden sanctuary where the displaced Lombax civilization preserved their cultural records before their exile. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration of the planet's golden-sand canyons and the underground library complex itself.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's emotional weight. Ratchet, who has spent his life believing his species was extinct, encounters firsthand evidence that the Lombaxes survived in another dimension and have left behind decades of cultural records. The library's archives include audio recordings from Lombax scientists, footage of the original Dimensionator's construction, and the campaign's most thematically loaded environmental detail.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's first sustained encounter with the Vullard, a peaceful trader species who have served as Savali's caretakers across the centuries. The chapter ends with a critical Dimensionator schematic recovered, Ratchet's understanding of his own heritage permanently deepened, and the campaign's middle act fully positioned.

  5. 5

    5. Blizar Prime — The Split-Dimension Mine

    The campaign's most mechanically distinctive single chapter. Blizar Prime is a mining planet that exists simultaneously in two dimensions, with the campaign's signature dimensional-shift puzzle mechanic allowing the player to switch between the two versions of the planet's geography in real time. The chapter is structured as a sustained vertical traversal sequence weaving puzzle-solving with sustained Goon combat.

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's mechanical innovation. The two versions of Blizar Prime are visually distinct (one is an active mining operation, the other is the post-collapse abandoned ruin of the same site), and the puzzles demand the player to switch between them at specific moments to access platforms, route lasers, and complete environmental sequences that exist only in one dimension at a time.

    The chapter ends with the second Dimensionator fragment recovered, the campaign's mid-third-act mining-rights political subplot resolved, and the path to the next dimension's exploration opened.

  6. 6

    6. Ardolis — Captain Quantum's Treasure

    A character-focused chapter set on the pirate planet Ardolis, where Rivet's intelligence network has identified a Dimensionator fragment hidden in Captain Quantum's Cove. The chapter is structured as a comedic pirate-themed exploration sequence with the franchise's signature humor at its highest density.

    The chapter introduces Captain Pierre — a robotic space pirate who has decided to become Rivet's reluctant ally. The chapter dwells on Pierre's relationship with his crew of equally robotic pirates, including a sustained subplot involving Pierre's missing identity papers. The chapter weaves combat against pirate enemies with sustained environmental puzzle sequences inside Captain Quantum's hidden treasure vault.

    The chapter ends with the third Dimensionator fragment recovered, the pirate crew formally allied with Rivet's resistance, and the campaign's third-act setup committed.

  7. 7

    7. Torren IV — Rivet's Past

    The campaign's most emotionally weighted chapter. Torren IV is a strip-mining planet that, twenty years before the campaign's events, was the site of a catastrophic Goon attack on a refugee settlement. The chapter is structured as a sustained narrative-driven exploration sequence, with combat encounters significantly reduced and the chapter's focus shifted to environmental storytelling.

    The chapter's central reveal is Kit's identity. Kit — a small war-robot Rivet has been searching for across the campaign — is revealed to be a defective Warbot prototype who participated in the Torren IV attack that destroyed Rivet's adoptive family. Kit's role in the massacre — staged as a brief, scarring memory sequence — is the campaign's emotional pivot. Rivet has spent the campaign hunting the robot responsible for her family's death; the robot, having reconstructed her ethics through years of guilt-driven solitude, has spent the same campaign seeking Rivet to apologize.

    The chapter ends with Rivet's acceptance of Kit's reformation, Kit joining the campaign's primary cast as Rivet's permanent companion, and the campaign's emotional question — whether the cycle of cruelty can ever be broken — answered in the affirmative.

  8. 8

    8. Cordelion — The Phantom Dimension

    The campaign's mid-third-act chapter. Cordelion is a planet partially overlapped with the Phantom Dimension — a between-dimensional space where dimensional anomalies have stabilized into a permanent geography. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration of phantom-shifted versions of standard environments, with combat encounters against phantom-class enemies that exist in multiple dimensional states simultaneously.

    The chapter weaves the campaign's most demanding sustained combat sequences with the franchise's signature environmental humor. Several of the chapter's combat arenas stage Clank — recovered earlier in the campaign and now operating as Kit's partner — through the franchise's signature Clank-puzzle minigames, with the Tachyon Field mechanic introduced for solving real-time spatial-displacement puzzles.

    The chapter ends with the final Dimensionator fragment recovered, the campaign's mechanical apex positioned, and Emperor Nefarious's location confirmed.

  9. 9

    9. Viceron — Emperor Nefarious's Capital

    The campaign's third-act spectacle chapter. Viceron is the central planet of the Nefarious Empire, with the Emperor's palace serving as the campaign's largest sustained interior environment. The chapter is structured as a sustained infiltration sequence, with Ratchet, Rivet, Clank, and Kit conducting a coordinated assault on the palace's central chamber.

    The chapter dwells on the Nefarious Empire's scale. The palace is rendered with the campaign's most architecturally ambitious design — massive throne rooms, neon-lit corridors, and the franchise's most distinctive Imperial aesthetic. The combat density escalates significantly, with the campaign's hardest sustained enemy encounters and the first sustained confrontation with Emperor Nefarious himself.

    The chapter ends with Emperor Nefarious's first encounter staged as a deliberate non-resolution — the Emperor escapes with the assembled Dimensionator, committed to using it to invade Ratchet's original dimension and conquer both realities. The campaign's climactic chapter is now staged.

  10. 10

    10. The Final Battle — Emperor Nefarious

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Emperor Nefarious, now in possession of the fully reassembled Dimensionator, attempts to open a permanent invasion portal between the two dimensions. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-stage boss confrontation across the palace's collapsing exterior, weaving Ratchet's signature weapon repertoire with Rivet's parallel loadout in coordinated combat sequences.

    The chapter's central mechanical conceit is the dimension-switching boss fight. Emperor Nefarious is fought across multiple dimensions simultaneously, with the boss's combat patterns shifting based on the dimension Ratchet and Rivet are currently occupying. The fight is the campaign's mechanical apex, demanding mastery of every weapon, every traversal ability, and every rift-tether interaction the campaign has taught.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's emotional resolution. The Dr. Nefarious of Ratchet's home dimension, recovered earlier in the chapter, is forced to confront the Emperor version of himself in a brief but thematically loaded sequence. The chapter ends with both Nefarious variants defeated, the Dimensionator destroyed, and the campaign's final character-resolution scenes committed.

  11. 11

    11. Epilogue — Home Across Dimensions

    The campaign's epilogue. Ratchet and Clank return to their original dimension, with Rivet and Kit choosing to remain in the Nefarious dimension to lead the post-Imperial reconstruction. The chapter is structured as a sustained quiet sequence with no combat, dedicated entirely to the campaign's character resolutions.

    The chapter dwells on Ratchet's growth. Where the franchise's prior installments left Ratchet's Lombax-heritage question open, Rift Apart explicitly answers it: the Lombaxes survived. They live in a parallel dimension. They can be visited but cannot be permanently returned to Ratchet's reality without reopening the dimensional crisis the campaign just resolved. The franchise's central character question — whether Ratchet must remain the last Lombax in his dimension — is answered with quiet acceptance rather than romantic reunion.

    The chapter's post-credits scenes establish the franchise's continuation, with Rivet and Kit's leadership of the new resistance government setting up potential future installments. The credits roll over the cross-dimensional friend group's final group photograph, with the campaign's central question — whether two heroes from two dimensions can find peace — answered through their mutual respect across the dimensional barrier.
