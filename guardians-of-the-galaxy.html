About the Game

Marvel's Guardians of the Galaxy is Eidos-Montréal's narrative-driven action-adventure following Peter Quill leading his team — Gamora, Drax, Rocket, and Groot — across the cosmic Marvel universe. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary. The cinematic playthrough preserves the team huddle dialogue trees and licensed 80s soundtrack callouts.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. The Quarantine Zone

    The campaign opens with Star-Lord — Peter Quill — leading the Guardians on a mission to capture a profitable alien creature inside the Nova Corps' Quarantine Zone, a restricted space anomaly drifting near the galactic core. The opening hour is structured as a tutorial-pacing introduction to the team: Gamora, Drax the Destroyer, Rocket Raccoon, and Groot each demonstrate their distinct combat abilities during a multi-stage chase through the Zone's wreckage fields.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's signature mechanic — the Huddle, in which Star-Lord can pause combat to deliver a motivational speech to the team, with player-selected dialogue options determining the buffs the team receives. The chapter dwells on the team's licensed soundtrack — the campaign's eighties-music selection becomes one of its most acclaimed identity choices.

    The chapter ends with the Guardians' chosen creature captured, the team's debt-driven motivations established, and the Nova Corps' bounty for unauthorized Quarantine Zone activity unfortunately accruing to their tab.

  2. 2

    2. Lady Hellbender's Court

    A character-focused chapter that takes the Guardians to Seknarf Nine, the planet ruled by Lady Hellbender — a monster-collecting noble who Star-Lord intends to sell the captured creature to in order to clear the Guardians' Nova Corps debt. The chapter is structured as a sustained negotiation sequence, with player choices determining which Guardian (Drax or Rocket) Star-Lord lies about as the "creature" he's selling.

    The chapter's emotional weight lands the campaign's central thesis: the Guardians are a found family bonded by repeated lies they tell each other, and the choices they make about which lies are loving versus which are betrayals will define the campaign's central conflict.

    The chapter ends with the negotiation completed (in various player-determined configurations), a violent Hellbender confrontation, and the Guardians fleeing Seknarf Nine without the payment they came for.

  3. 3

    3. Knowhere

    The campaign's central hub introduction. Knowhere — the severed Celestial head that serves as a freeport for the galaxy's outlaws — is rendered as one of the campaign's most visually distinctive environments. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration of the freeport's neighborhoods, with the team's network of contacts (the Collector, Cosmo the Spacedog, Mantis) established through small interaction beats.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's central antagonist faction: the Universal Church of Truth, a galaxy-spanning religious organization led by the Grand Unifier Raker. Raker's introduction is staged with deliberate menace — her sermons promise eternal peace, and her followers are visibly being absorbed into a planet-scale collective consciousness that the chapter renders in unsettling detail.

    The chapter ends with the Collector's tip pointing the Guardians toward an opportunity to make the credits they owe the Nova Corps — by stealing from the Universal Church's central archive.

  4. 4

    4. The Heart of the Universe

    A mid-act chapter set inside a Universal Church-occupied cathedral world. The Guardians infiltrate the Church's central archive in pursuit of a powerful artifact called the Promise. The chapter weaves combat against Church inquisitors with sustained environmental exploration of the Church's converted citizens — most of whom are willing converts now in the process of being absorbed by Raker's central consciousness.

    The chapter's emotional pivot is the discovery that the Promise is functionally a weapon — one capable of inducing the Church's mass-conversion frequency at planet-scale. The Guardians' theft is being interrupted by Raker herself, who reveals that the Promise's true power is not control but mass redemption: it can erase the trauma of any victim by erasing the personality that experienced it.

    The chapter ends with the Guardians escaping with the Promise, Raker's pursuit committed, and the campaign's central moral question fully posed.

  5. 5

    5. The Magus Encounter

    The campaign's mid-game perspective pivot. The Guardians, now in possession of the Promise, are intercepted by the Magus — a galactic-scale telepathic entity who occupies a dimension parallel to the Universal Church's cathedral worlds. The Magus is the campaign's true antagonist; Raker is his prophet. The chapter is structured as a sustained mind-prison sequence in which the Guardians are individually trapped in idealized fantasies of their own deepest desires.

    Each Guardian's fantasy sequence is the campaign's most emotionally weighted single chapter. Star-Lord dreams of his mother's resurrection on Earth; Gamora dreams of returning to her homeworld before Thanos arrived; Drax dreams of his wife and daughter restored; Rocket dreams of escape from the lab where he was made; Groot dreams of his original Planet X colony. The chapter dwells on the cost of each fantasy and the team's slow, painful rejection of the Magus's gift.

    The chapter ends with the team escaping the Magus's prison, the Promise's deeper origin understood, and the campaign's third act staged.

  6. 6

    6. The Promise — Drax's Story

    The campaign's emotional centerpiece. Drax the Destroyer finally faces the unresolved trauma of his family's death at Thanos's hand. The chapter is structured as a sustained character arc that takes the team to a memorial site on Drax's homeworld, where the player navigates Drax's grief through dialogue choices and the team's collective response.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central thesis. The Promise is not a weapon — it is the temptation to forget. Drax's refusal of the Promise is the chapter's emotional climax. The chapter ends with the team's commitment to destroying the artifact rather than using it, with Raker's Universal Church now in active pursuit across multiple worlds.

  7. 7

    7. The Universal Church Assault

    The campaign's third-act spectacle chapter. Raker's Universal Church mobilizes a full planetary fleet against the Guardians. The chapter is structured as a sustained ship-combat sequence aboard the Milano (the team's vessel), followed by a multi-stage boarding action against the Church's flagship.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's most demanding team-coordination sequences, with the Huddle mechanic deployed at increasing density. The chapter's emotional weight lands the campaign's central refusal: the team will not use the Promise to win, even when winning the conventional way is becoming impossible.

    The chapter ends with the Milano damaged but operational, several team members wounded, and the Magus's true cathedral world identified as the campaign's final destination.

  8. 8

    8. The Final Cathedral

    The campaign's climactic chapter. The Guardians descend into the Magus's cathedral world — a planet-scale Church construction where the Promise was originally created. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage assault on the Magus's central chamber, with sustained combat against Church inquisitors, the campaign's first sustained encounter with the Magus's mind-children, and a climactic boss fight against Raker herself.

    The Raker boss fight is the campaign's mechanical apex. Multiple phases force the team into coordinated attack-and-defense patterns, with Huddle deployment timing becoming critical. The fight ends with Raker defeated, the Promise destroyed, and the Magus's manifestation reduced to a single corporeal form.

    The chapter ends with the Magus's final reveal — he is not a god but a galactic-scale trauma victim himself, an entity created by mass-civilizational grief — and the campaign's emotional resolution staged for the next chapter.

  9. 9

    9. The Magus — The Final Confrontation

    The campaign's final boss fight. The Magus manifests on the cathedral world's central altar in his true form: a vast, scarred being whose face mirrors Star-Lord's own. The fight is staged as the team's most coordinated combat sequence, with each Guardian's distinct ability essential for breaking the Magus's regenerating defenses.

    The chapter's emotional weight lands the campaign's central question. The Magus offers the Guardians one final chance to accept the Promise's gift — collective forgetting, collective peace. The chapter's player choice (which the team responds to through Huddle dialogue) determines whether the campaign ends in triumphant victory, in mutual destruction, or in a moral compromise the campaign refuses to soften.

    The chapter ends with the Magus defeated, the Universal Church's broader operations dissolving, and the team beginning the long flight back to Knowhere.

  10. 10

    10. Epilogue — The Milano

    The campaign's epilogue is staged aboard the Milano as the Guardians return to Knowhere. The chapter is structured as a series of small character beats — Star-Lord and Gamora's relationship resolution, Drax and Rocket's quiet conversation, Groot's growth, and the team's collective recognition that they have, against all evidence, become a family.

    The chapter's final scene establishes the franchise's broader continuation, with a brief tease of Adam Warlock's arrival in the galaxy. The credits roll over the team's licensed soundtrack final cue. The campaign's central question — what the Guardians actually are — is answered in the only way the campaign permits: by their continued commitment to each other across every disaster they've authored together.
