About the Game

Ghost of Yotei is Sucker Punch's follow-up to Ghost of Tsushima, set centuries later in 1603 Hokkaido around the Mount Yotei region. Players take on the protagonist Atsu, a wandering swordswoman seeking vengeance against the Yotei Six who killed her family. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary. The cinematic playthrough preserves Sucker Punch's signature wind-guided traversal and standoff combat.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    Prologue — The Massacre at Mount Yōtei

    The campaign opens 300 years after the events of Ghost of Tsushima, in 1600s Ezo (the historical name for Hokkaidō). The opening sequence is structured as a sustained character-introduction chapter dwelling on the protagonist's foundational trauma. Atsu — the campaign's central protagonist — is a child living with her family at the base of Mount Yōtei when their settlement is attacked by the Yōtei Six, a violent gang led by Lord Saito that has been terrorizing the region.

    The chapter's central event is the massacre of Atsu's family. Atsu's father, mother, and brother are all killed in the assault. Atsu herself is left for dead, pinned to a tree by an arrow. The chapter dwells on the campaign's tonal commitment with significantly more sustained violence than its franchise predecessor, with the trauma of the opening sequence anchoring the campaign's central revenge motivation.

    The chapter ends with Atsu's survival, the campaign's central premise (Atsu's pursuit of the Yōtei Six across decades) committed, and the time-skip transition to Atsu's adult life as a wandering mercenary executing her long-planned revenge.

  2. 2

    1. The Lonewolf — Hunt for The Snake

    The campaign's first sustained chapter opens with Atsu as an adult, having spent the intervening years training in combat and tracking the surviving members of the Yōtei Six across Ezo's vast wilderness. The chapter is structured as a sustained introduction to the campaign's open-world exploration loop, with the Yōtei Grasslands — the starting region — providing the campaign's first sustained environmental investigation.

    The chapter's central event is the hunt for The Snake, the first of the Yōtei Six. The Snake is rendered as a drunken minor enforcer who has spent the intervening years coasting on his reputation, with his confrontation staged in a small tavern at the chapter's mid-section. The boss encounter is the campaign's first sustained mechanical test, with the franchise's signature stance-based katana combat fully introduced.

    The chapter ends with The Snake killed, Atsu writing his name on the sash of the Yōtei Six that serves as the campaign's central kill-tracking device, and the path to the campaign's first sustained side-investigation opened.

  3. 3

    2. The Lonewolf — Pursuing the Oni

    A sustained mid-game investigation chapter. Atsu's pursuit of The Oni — a brutally violent enforcer who has spent the intervening years running a mercenary operation in the campaign's northern mountains — provides the campaign's first sustained combat-focused questline. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-stage investigation weaving combat against the Oni's mercenary operation with sustained environmental investigation of the region's broader social infrastructure.

    The chapter dwells on Atsu's combat repertoire expansion. The pursuit of the Oni unlocks the campaign's signature Mercenary Attire equipment and the Oni's Flame combat ability, with the chapter's reward structure significantly expanding Atsu's tactical options. The chapter's climactic boss confrontation against the Oni stages the campaign's first sustained mid-tier antagonist encounter, with the antagonist's signature dual-handed combat repertoire demanding mastery of the campaign's parry-and-counter system.

    The chapter ends with the Oni killed, his name added to the sash, and the campaign's central revenge pursuit fully active.

  4. 4

    3. The Lonewolf — Hunting the Kitsune

    A sustained stealth-focused investigation chapter. The Kitsune — the campaign's stealth-specialist antagonist, a former noble who has spent the intervening years operating as an information broker and political manipulator — provides the campaign's first sustained stealth-focused questline. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-stage investigation weaving stealth infiltration with the franchise's signature investigation-and-evidence mechanics.

    The chapter dwells on Atsu's stealth-combat repertoire expansion. The pursuit of the Kitsune unlocks the campaign's signature Nine Tail Armor equipment, which significantly enhances Atsu's stealth detection avoidance and quickfire weapon ammunition regeneration. The chapter's climactic Kitsune confrontation is staged in the antagonist's private chambers, with the boss encounter weaving sustained stealth-positioning with the franchise's signature ranged combat.

    The chapter ends with the Kitsune killed, Atsu's stealth combat repertoire fully developed, and the campaign's third Yōtei Six member eliminated.

  5. 5

    4. The Lonewolf — The Saito Brothers' Cold Trail

    A sustained mid-act pursuit chapter. The Saito Brothers — The Dragon and The Spider, two of the Yōtei Six who operate together under Lord Saito's broader military command — represent the campaign's most demanding sustained antagonist faction. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-stage investigation across Ezo's central regions, with Atsu following the brothers' broader operational trail across multiple settlement zones.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's signature Way of the Odachi combat questline. The pursuit of the Saito brothers cannot be completed in the campaign's opening chapter — the brothers retreat to the campaign's final region, with their formal confrontation deferred to Chapter II. The chapter's central reward is the Odachi (great-sword) combat repertoire, which significantly expands Atsu's heavy-combat tactical options.

    The chapter ends with the brothers' immediate location confirmed in the campaign's final region, the Ghosts of the Past mission triggered as the chapter's closing event, and the campaign's transition to Chapter II committed.

  6. 6

    5. Ghosts of the Past — Chapter Transition

    A character-focused chapter staged at Atsu's family home in the Yōtei Grasslands. The chapter is structured as a sustained quiet sequence with no combat, dedicated entirely to Atsu's reflective character development at the site of her family's massacre.

    The chapter dwells on Atsu's emotional journey. The campaign's opening Yōtei Six revenge motivation has been complicated by the years of pursuit — Atsu has become known across Ezo as the Onryō (vengeful spirit), with the broader regional population fearing her arrival in their settlements. The chapter dwells on Atsu's quiet recognition that her revenge has reshaped her identity in ways she did not anticipate.

    The chapter ends with Atsu's commitment to complete her vengeance regardless of its cost, the campaign's transition to Chapter II formally committed, and the path to Oshima Coast — the campaign's final region — opened.

  7. 7

    6. The Onryō — The Dragon

    The campaign's mid-third-act spectacle chapter. The Dragon — the elder Saito brother — is rendered with significantly more sustained military infrastructure than the prior chapters' antagonists. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-stage assault on the Dragon's coastal stronghold in Oshima Coast, with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental design (the region's cherry-blossom-dense visual aesthetic) anchoring the chapter's spectacle weight.

    The chapter weaves combat against the Saito family's military forces with sustained environmental investigation of the broader coastal political situation. The Saito regime has been systematically destabilizing the region's smaller settlements, with the chapter's emerging social context positioning the campaign's central political crisis. The chapter's climactic boss confrontation against the Dragon stages the campaign's most demanding sustained one-on-one duel sequence.

    The chapter ends with the Dragon killed, the Saito family's broader military command structure significantly destabilized, and the path to the Spider's confrontation committed.

  8. 8

    7. The Onryō — The Spider

    The campaign's most acclaimed sustained antagonist-confrontation chapter. The Spider — the younger Saito brother — operates from a smaller, more isolated stronghold deep within the coastal forests. The chapter is structured as a sustained infiltration sequence weaving stealth combat with the franchise's most acclaimed environmental design.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central character arc. Atsu's pursuit of the Spider stages her most emotionally complex sustained antagonist encounter — the Spider's prior history with Atsu's family is significantly more personal than the prior Yōtei Six members', with the boss encounter weaving sustained combat with sustained emotional dialogue exchanges. The chapter's climactic confrontation is the campaign's most thematically loaded sustained one-on-one duel.

    The chapter ends with the Spider killed, the Saito family fully eliminated, and the campaign's final confrontation positioned. Lord Saito — the campaign's central antagonist — remains as the sole surviving Yōtei Six member.

  9. 9

    8. The Final Reckoning — Lord Saito

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Lord Saito — the central antagonist whose attack on Atsu's family launched the campaign's runtime — manifests for the campaign's final boss confrontation at the chapter's mid-section. The chapter is structured as a sustained assault on Saito's central fortress, with the campaign's most demanding sustained combat encounters and the franchise's most acclaimed final-boss-confrontation cinematography.

    The chapter's central event is the Saito duel, staged across multiple arena configurations with the antagonist's signature combat repertoire — the campaign's most sophisticated parry-and-counter patterns, the franchise's most acclaimed sustained boss mechanics, and the chapter's emotional weight anchored on Atsu's accumulated character development. The fight is the campaign's mechanical apex.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central thematic question. The campaign's runtime has positioned revenge as both Atsu's central motivation and her central character corruption — the fight's resolution offers Atsu (and the player) the choice between Saito's clean execution and a more morally complex alternative resolution.

    The chapter ends with Saito's death, the Yōtei Six fully eliminated, and the campaign's epilogue committed.

  10. 10

    9. Epilogue — The Onryō Departs

    The campaign's epilogue chapter is staged in the days following Lord Saito's death. Atsu, having completed the revenge that has defined her adult life, faces the campaign's central thematic question — what does she do now that her central motivation is permanently resolved? The chapter is structured as a sustained quiet exploration sequence across the regions she has traveled throughout the campaign.

    The chapter dwells on Atsu's character resolution. Her decision to depart Ezo entirely — to leave behind the Onryō identity that has defined her years of pursuit — is rendered with the franchise's signature qualified hope. The surviving settlements have begun their slow recovery from the Yōtei Six's regional destabilization. The cherry blossoms on Oshima Coast bloom for the first season without the Saito regime's military presence.

    The credits roll over Atsu's solitary departure across the Ezo countryside, with the campaign's central question — whether revenge can ever be a sustainable identity — answered through the franchise's signature commitment to the protagonist's accumulated character growth. The post-credits scenes seed the franchise's continued narrative potential, with the broader Yōtei region's recovery positioned for the franchise's possible continuation.
