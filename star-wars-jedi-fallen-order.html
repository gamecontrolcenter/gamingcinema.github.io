About the Game

Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order is Respawn Entertainment's 2019 Soulslike action-adventure set in the dark times after Order 66. Padawan survivor Cal Kestis attempts to rebuild the Jedi Order while hunted by Imperial Inquisitors. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary. The cinematic playthrough emphasizes the Metroidvania-style world design.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Bracca — Scrapyard Apprentice

    The campaign opens with Cal Kestis, a survivor of Order 66, working anonymously as a scrap-cutter on the Star Destroyer scrapyard of Bracca five years after the Empire's massacre of the Jedi. The opening sequence is structured as a slow-burn tonal piece: Cal lives in the workers' shanty town, hides his Force sensitivity, and works alongside his friend Prauf, an Abednedo scrapper. The chapter dwells on the texture of a post-Jedi galaxy — the visible Imperial oppression, the rationing, and the careful concealment Cal has constructed to survive.

    The chapter's pivot is a workplace accident that forces Cal to instinctively use the Force to save a fellow worker. The act is witnessed by Imperial agents, and the resulting investigation brings the Second Sister — a Jedi-trained Imperial Inquisitor — to Bracca personally. Prauf is murdered. Cal is chased across the scrapyard in the campaign's first sustained combat tutorial.

    The chapter ends with Cal extracted from Bracca by Cere Junda, a Jedi survivor turned anti-Imperial operative, and her pilot Greez Dritus aboard the Mantis. The campaign's central mission — to recover a Jedi holocron that contains the names of every Force-sensitive child the Order has ever identified — is introduced.

  2. 2

    2. Bogano — BD-1 and the Vault

    The campaign's tutorial planet. Bogano is a small, lush moon where the late Jedi Master Eno Cordova hid the holocron Cere has been hunting. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration of the moon's surface, with Cal acquiring the campaign's signature companion: BD-1, a small spherical droid Cordova left behind to guide a worthy Jedi to the vault.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's traversal fundamentals — wall-running, double-jumping, vine-swinging, and the BD-1 healing and scanning systems that will define the campaign's puzzle and exploration loop. The combat density is light; the chapter's focus is environmental learning.

    The chapter ends with Cal locating Cordova's holocron vault — sealed with a puzzle Cordova specifically designed so that only a Jedi who has visited multiple Zeffo-civilization tombs can solve it. The campaign's next planets — Zeffo, Kashyyyk, and Dathomir — are introduced as Cordova's required pilgrimage sites.

  3. 3

    3. Zeffo — The First Tomb

    The campaign's first sustained dungeon planet. Zeffo is the homeworld of the Zeffo civilization — an extinct precursor culture that practiced Force-based engineering centuries before the Jedi Order. The chapter is structured as a vertical exploration of Zeffo's surface and tomb complexes, with new traversal abilities (Force-push, slow-time effect) introduced through the Tombs themselves.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's first sustained encounter with Imperial Purge Troopers — anti-Jedi specialists equipped with electrostaffs and shield-piercing weapons. The Purge Troopers force Cal to integrate parry mechanics and Force-redirect attacks into his combat rotation.

    The chapter ends with Cal acquiring the first Zeffo tomb's puzzle piece and the campaign's exploration loop now fully open. The next destination — Kashyyyk — is unlocked.

  4. 4

    4. Kashyyyk — The Wookiee Resistance

    The campaign's mid-act political chapter. Cal arrives on the Wookiee homeworld Kashyyyk during the Imperial occupation, where Saw Gerrera's rebel cell is conducting a sustained insurgency against the Empire's slave-labor camps. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage rebellion assistance arc, with combat ranging from Wookiee village skirmishes to a multi-stage AT-AT walker assault on an Imperial refinery.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's political stakes. Saw Gerrera — a recurring figure across the broader Star Wars franchise — is rendered as a paranoid but effective extremist whose methods Cal finds alarming. The Imperial occupation of Kashyyyk is rendered with significantly more environmental detail than the franchise has typically afforded the Wookiee homeworld.

    The chapter ends with the Imperial refinery destroyed, Tarfful (the Wookiee chieftain) freed, and the second Zeffo puzzle piece recovered from the planet's interior.

  5. 5

    5. Zeffo (Return) — Trilla's Pursuit

    A return to Zeffo for the second tomb, with the campaign's mid-act pursuit dynamic now fully active. The Second Sister — revealed in audio logs as Trilla Suduri, a former Jedi Padawan turned Inquisitor — has been tracking Cal across the campaign's planets and now confronts him at the second Zeffo tomb. The chapter is structured as a sustained dungeon-and-duel sequence.

    The duel with the Second Sister is the campaign's first sustained Inquisitor confrontation. Trilla's combat style is the campaign's most demanding to date — dual-blade lightsaber stance, Force-redirected projectile attacks, and a multi-phase fight that demands mastery of the campaign's parry mechanics. The duel ends with Cal's narrow escape, Trilla's identity as a former Jedi exposed, and the campaign's central moral question — what the Empire has done to its former Jedi — fully introduced.

    The chapter ends with Cal's recovery on the Mantis, Cere visibly disturbed by Trilla's identity, and the campaign's mid-third-act setup for the next planet.

  6. 6

    6. Dathomir — The Nightsisters

    The campaign's darkest sustained chapter. Dathomir is the homeworld of the Nightsisters, a witch-coven of dark-side Force users whose order was nearly extinguished during the Clone Wars. Cal arrives in pursuit of the third Zeffo puzzle piece and immediately meets Merrin — the last surviving Nightsister, a teenage survivor who has spent the years since her order's destruction alone among the planet's ruins.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained negotiation arc. Merrin is hostile to Cal — the Jedi were complicit in the Nightsisters' fall — and the chapter requires Cal to navigate her grief through both combat and dialogue. The combat highlights include encounters with Undead Nightbrother warriors that Merrin's magic resurrects to test Cal's resolve.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's most thematically dense conversations — Merrin's grief, Cal's PTSD from Order 66, and Cere's own complicated history with the dark side. The chapter ends with Merrin granting Cal access to the third Zeffo puzzle piece, and Merrin herself joining the Mantis crew permanently as the campaign's most acclaimed companion.

  7. 7

    7. Ilum — The Kyber Crystal Pilgrimage

    The franchise's most cinematic single-planet chapter. Ilum — the ancient Jedi pilgrimage world where Padawans crafted their first lightsabers — has been hollowed out by the Empire and converted into a weapons-research facility. Cal arrives in pursuit of the second Zeffo tomb's central key, but the chapter pivots into the campaign's most emotionally complete sequence: Cal's pilgrimage to find a new kyber crystal of his own and to complete the lightsaber his master Jaro Tapal began.

    The chapter is structured as a single sustained walk through the Imperial-occupied ice caverns of Ilum, with Cal's PTSD from Order 66 surfacing through Force-vision flashbacks. The combat density is light; the chapter's central focus is the construction of Cal's new lightsaber and his emotional reconciliation with his master's death.

    The chapter ends with Cal's completed double-bladed lightsaber, his Force sensitivity stabilized, and the campaign's final destination — the Inquisitorius fortress on Nur — staged.

  8. 8

    8. The Subterrel Crypt — Final Holocron

    A medium-length chapter that returns Cal to the third and final Zeffo tomb, with Trilla's reinforced Inquisitor team now waiting at the chamber's entrance. The chapter weaves the campaign's hardest puzzles with sustained combat across the tomb's deepest level, culminating in a final dueling sequence against the Second Sister.

    The chapter's duel is the campaign's emotional centerpiece. Trilla is defeated, captured, and brought aboard the Mantis. The chapter's resolution stages the campaign's most acclaimed conversation — Cere confronting Trilla as her former Padawan, with the campaign's full backstory of betrayal and survival now finally articulated. Cal recovers the holocron with the list of every Force-sensitive child the Jedi Order knew of.

    The chapter ends with Cal in possession of the holocron, Trilla murdered by Darth Vader in the Mantis itself, and the campaign's final pursuit now committed.

  9. 9

    9. Nur — Confrontation with Vader

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Nur — the Inquisitorius fortress moon — is where Cal has decided to confront Darth Vader personally to recover Cere from his pursuit. The chapter is structured as a sustained infiltration of the Inquisitorius base, with combat against Purge Troopers, Stormtroopers, and the campaign's hardest sustained enemy encounters.

    The chapter's climactic encounter is the duel with Vader himself. The fight is mechanically distinct from every prior boss in the campaign — Vader cannot be defeated. The encounter is staged as a sustained survival sequence, with Cal forced to flee through the fortress's collapsing infrastructure while Vader pursues with overwhelming Force-based attacks. The sequence is one of the most acclaimed in modern Star Wars storytelling.

    The chapter ends with Cal and Cere narrowly escaping Nur, Vader's pursuit halted, and the campaign's central moral question — whether to use the holocron's list to recruit Force-sensitive children into a new Jedi order — staged for the final scene.

  10. 10

    10. The Holocron Decision — Bogano Return

    The campaign's epilogue. Cal returns to Bogano to face the final moral choice the campaign has been building toward: whether to use Eno Cordova's holocron to begin building a new Jedi order, with the catastrophic risk of the Empire identifying and hunting the children, or whether to destroy the holocron and protect them from ever being targeted.

    The chapter is structured as a single sustained conversation between Cal, Cere, Merrin, and Greez aboard the Mantis. The decision is Cal's. The campaign's resolution stages either choice with substantial emotional weight, and the credits roll over the Mantis departing Bogano's atmosphere.

    The chapter's post-credits scene establishes the campaign's sequel, with the surviving cast committed to a galaxy-spanning resistance arc that Survivor will pick up five years later.
