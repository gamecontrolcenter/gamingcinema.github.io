About the Game

Indiana Jones and the Great Circle is MachineGames' first-person action-adventure following Dr. Henry Jones Jr. in 1937, investigating a global conspiracy after a midnight artifact theft at Marshall College. The walkthrough captures the full campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary. The cinematic playthrough showcases the game's photoreal facial capture of Troy Baker as Indy and the period-accurate art direction.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Marshall College — The Mystery Begins

    The campaign opens in 1937, at Marshall College in Connecticut, where archaeology professor Indiana Jones is interrupted during a quiet evening lecture by an unusual burglary attempt at the campus museum. The opening sequence is structured as a sustained tutorial-paced introduction to the campaign's reworked combat fundamentals — Indy's signature whip-and-fist combat, the inventory-tile management system, and the franchise's signature improvised-weapon mechanics.

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's signature opening tone. The burglar — pursued through Marshall College's labyrinthine corridors and underground archives — has stolen a single artifact: an ancient cat mummy of significantly less obvious value than the museum's other holdings. The chapter's mid-section establishes Indy's investigation into the artifact's broader significance, with the campaign's central mystery — the Great Circle, a globe-spanning alignment of pre-modern archaeological sites — first introduced through Indy's research notes.

    The chapter ends with the burglar's escape, Indy's recovery of crucial intelligence linking the theft to a Vatican-based archaeological organization, and the campaign's first international destination committed.

  2. 2

    2. The Vatican — The Stolen Cat Mummy

    The campaign's first sustained open-zone chapter. The Vatican is rendered as the campaign's most acclaimed urban environment — a labyrinthine complex of churches, archives, and underground catacombs with the campaign's most extensive side-content density. The chapter is structured as a sustained investigation sequence weaving stealth-and-combat infiltration with the franchise's signature puzzle-and-traversal exploration.

    The chapter introduces Gina Lombardi, an Italian journalist with significantly more sustained personal investment in the Vatican investigation than her cover suggests. The chapter dwells on Gina and Indy's developing partnership, with sustained dialogue exchanges establishing her as the campaign's primary supporting protagonist. The chapter also introduces Emmerich Voss, the campaign's central antagonist — a former Nazi-affiliated archaeologist whose Great Circle research has been conducted with significantly more historical accuracy than the surrounding Nazi establishment can publicly admit.

    The chapter's central event is the recovery of the cat mummy in the Vatican's underground catacombs. The mummy's true significance — its relationship to the broader Great Circle alignment — is fully exposed through the chapter's environmental investigation. The chapter ends with the path to Gizeh opened, Voss's pursuit forces in active operation, and the campaign's central international investigation committed.

  3. 3

    3. Gizeh — The Idol of Ra

    The campaign's most architecturally ambitious mid-game chapter. Gizeh — the Egyptian site of the Great Pyramids — is rendered with the campaign's most acclaimed exterior environmental design. The chapter is structured as a sustained dual-environment exploration sequence weaving above-ground investigation in the modern Egyptian city of Cairo with sustained underground exploration of the tombs and chambers beneath the Pyramids.

    The chapter introduces several distinctive enemy variants — the Nazi expeditionary force's elite combat specialists, the local Egyptian gangs running their own parallel Great Circle investigations, and the campaign's first sustained encounter with the Order's enforcers, a paramilitary organization operating beneath the Nazi political infrastructure. The combat density escalates significantly, with the chapter's combat encounters demanding the full toolkit Indy has accumulated.

    The chapter's central event is the recovery of the Idol of Ra from a previously unexcavated chamber deep beneath the Great Pyramid. The chapter ends with the Idol secured, the next Great Circle waypoint identified, and the path to the Himalayas committed.

  4. 4

    4. The Himalayas — KMS Kummetz

    A vertical-traversal-focused chapter with the campaign's most distinctive single setting. KMS Kummetz is a Nazi battleship — improbably stationed high in the Himalayan mountains, beached and abandoned by an experimental Nazi engineering project that has been operating in secret across the previous decade. The chapter is structured as a sustained interior exploration of the wrecked battleship's multi-level architecture, with the franchise's signature climbing-and-traversal mechanics fully foregrounded.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's environmental absurdity. The decision to set a major mid-game chapter inside a beached battleship in the Himalayas is rendered with sustained earnestness — the engineering choices that put the battleship in the location, the Nazi survivors still operating from inside it, and the broader Great Circle significance of the location are all treated with the franchise's signature attention to historical detail despite the obvious physical impossibility.

    The chapter's central event is a critical Great Circle archive recovered from the battleship's lower decks. The chapter ends with the next Great Circle waypoint identified, the campaign's mid-third-act pivot committed, and the path to Shanghai opened.

  5. 5

    5. Shanghai — Into the Fire

    The campaign's most distinctive urban combat chapter. Shanghai is rendered as a sustained dense-urban environment with the campaign's hardest sustained combat encounters and the franchise's most acclaimed signature traversal sequences. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-district investigation weaving combat against the city's organized crime infrastructure with sustained environmental investigation of the local Great Circle excavation sites.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's most morally complex supporting characters. Local Shanghai informants, criminal-organization leaders, and surviving Chinese archaeologists provide significantly more historical context for the Great Circle's deeper meaning than the prior chapters' antagonist-focused investigations have produced. The chapter dwells on the campaign's recognition that the Great Circle is not merely a Nazi research target — it is a pre-modern global phenomenon whose original architects predated all currently surviving political infrastructures.

    The chapter ends with another Great Circle archive recovered, the campaign's third-act setup committed, and the path to Southeast Asia opened.

  6. 6

    6. Sukhothai — The Blessed Pearl

    The campaign's mid-third-act exploration chapter. Sukhothai is the ruins of an ancient Thai kingdom, rendered with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental detail and the franchise's signature jungle-and-temple exploration. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-temple exploration sequence with sustained environmental investigation of the Sukhothai kingdom's relationship to the broader Great Circle phenomenon.

    The chapter weaves combat with the franchise's most acclaimed sustained puzzle sequences. The Sukhothai temple complex contains the most architecturally ambitious individual puzzle in the campaign — a multi-chamber pearl-and-mirror sequence that demands the full toolkit Indy has accumulated. The chapter dwells on the campaign's environmental storytelling, with the surviving Sukhothai-era inscriptions providing significantly more historical context for the Great Circle's central architecture.

    The chapter's central event is the recovery of the Blessed Pearl, an artifact whose role in the Great Circle's central mechanism is the campaign's most thematically loaded reveal. The chapter ends with the campaign's final waypoint identified, and the path to Iraq committed.

  7. 7

    7. Iraq — Atonements

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Iraq — specifically a remote desert excavation site in the country's southern region — is the central node of the Great Circle, with the chapter staged as a sustained multi-stage assault on the Order's main archaeological compound. The chapter is structured as a sustained infiltration-and-combat sequence with the campaign's most demanding sustained combat encounters.

    The chapter's central confrontation is Emmerich Voss's final stand. Voss, having spent the campaign's runtime assembling the Great Circle's required artifacts, attempts to activate the alignment's central mechanism at the Iraq site. The chapter dwells on the campaign's central thematic reveal: the Great Circle is not a weapon. It is a temporal mechanism, a pre-modern technological infrastructure designed by an ancient civilization to suspend the broader passage of time across the planet.

    The chapter ends with Voss defeated, the Great Circle's activation prevented, and the campaign's final cinematic resolution committed. Indy and Gina escape the Iraq site as it collapses, with the franchise's signature post-mission departure rendered with the franchise's standard cinematic warmth.

  8. 8

    8. The Order of Giants — Rome DLC

    The campaign's expansion content. The Order of Giants is a post-launch DLC chapter set in Rome, accessible during the player's Vatican exploration via a meeting with Gina Lombardi. The chapter is structured as a sustained Rome-based investigation expanding the broader Great Circle mystery, with the franchise's signature urban exploration extending into the city's catacombs and ancient ruins.

    The chapter introduces the Order of Giants — a pre-modern religious organization with sustained historical connections to the Great Circle's original architects. The chapter weaves combat with sustained environmental investigation across Rome's most architecturally significant locations, with the franchise's signature traversal sequences foregrounded throughout the chapter's runtime.

    The chapter ends with the Order's full historical significance exposed, the broader Great Circle mythology significantly expanded, and the franchise's continued storytelling potential committed. The credits roll over Indy and Gina's quiet departure from Rome, with the campaign's central question — whether the past can ever be fully understood — answered with the franchise's signature qualified ambiguity.
