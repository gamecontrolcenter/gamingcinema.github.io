About the Game

Resident Evil 4 Remake reimagines Capcom's 2005 classic with the RE Engine, rebuilt combat systems, and modernized pacing. Leon S. Kennedy travels to a rural European village to rescue the President's daughter Ashley Graham from a parasitic cult known as Los Iluminados. The walkthrough captures the full campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling — Village, Castle, and Island acts rendered with high-fidelity lighting and crisp particle effects. No commentary, pure cinematic playthrough.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. The Village — Arrival in Rural Spain

    The campaign opens with Leon S. Kennedy — six years after the Raccoon City incident, now a U.S. government agent assigned to the Division of Security Operations — arriving in a remote village in rural Spain. His mission is the recovery of Ashley Graham, the U.S. President's daughter, who has been kidnapped by a religious cult operating in the region. The opening sequence is structured as a sustained tutorial-paced introduction to the campaign's remake-reworked combat fundamentals: the over-the-shoulder camera, the inventory-tile system, the knife durability mechanics, and the franchise's signature parry-and-counter system that the remake introduces as a defensive option.

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's iconic opening hour. Leon's escort drops him at the edge of the village; he descends into the village proper alone; the first villager he encounters violently attacks him with a meat cleaver. The chapter's first sustained combat sequence — the village square defense against the Ganados, the cult's infected followers — has become one of the most acclaimed combat introductions in modern gaming. The sequence culminates in the church bell that ends the assault with deliberate religious-horror staging.

    The chapter ends with Leon's recovery from the village square, the campaign's central mystery — what has transformed the villagers — fully active, and the path deeper into the surrounding farmlands committed.

  2. 2

    2. The Cabin — Luis Sera and the First Survival

    The campaign's first sustained dungeon-style sequence. Leon's exploration of the village's surrounding farmlands brings him to a fortified cabin where he encounters Luis Sera, a Spanish ex-cop who has been investigating the cult's activities for months. The chapter is structured as a sustained defensive combat sequence with Luis providing supporting fire as Ganados attack the cabin from multiple entry points.

    The chapter dwells on Luis's character. The remake significantly expands Luis's role from the original 2005 release, with sustained dialogue exchanges establishing his ethical investment in the cult's victims and his growing recognition that Leon is the only outside agent likely to survive long enough to expose the situation. The chapter introduces the campaign's signature Las Plagas parasitic infestation — the biological mechanism by which the cult controls the villagers.

    The chapter ends with Luis temporarily separating from Leon after the cabin's defense, the campaign's central villain mythology fully introduced, and the path to the village chief Bitores Mendez opened.

  3. 3

    3. The Lake — El Lago

    A medium-length chapter dedicated to one of the franchise's most acclaimed set-pieces. Bitores Mendez, the village chief, has been keeping a massive bioweapon hidden in the lake at the village's edge. The chapter is structured as a sustained boat-based exploration sequence with the franchise's first sustained encounter against Del Lago, the lake's prehistoric Plagas-mutated bioweapon.

    The chapter's boss confrontation is staged from Leon's small boat, with harpoon-based combat replacing the franchise's signature firearm rotation. The fight has become one of the franchise's most cinematic sustained sequences. The chapter ends with Del Lago defeated, Leon recovered, and the campaign's central infection now confirmed: Leon himself has been exposed to Las Plagas during the village combat encounters.

  4. 4

    4. Confrontation — Bitores Mendez

    The campaign's first sustained boss-confrontation chapter. Bitores Mendez, having received intelligence on Leon's presence, captures Leon and Luis at the village's central barn. The chapter is structured as a sustained combat sequence in the barn's loft, with the Mendez boss fight staged across multiple vertical levels and the chapter's signature aerial-grapple combat mechanics introduced.

    The Mendez fight is one of the franchise's most acclaimed sustained boss encounters. The village chief's two-phase transformation — first to his armored insectoid form, then to his unleashed Plagas-host configuration — demands mastery of the campaign's combat fundamentals at significantly higher intensity than the chapter's prior encounters. The fight ends with Mendez defeated, the village fully under Leon's control, and the path to the cult's central fortress — Castle Salazar — opened.

  5. 5

    5. The Castle — Ramón Salazar

    The campaign's most acclaimed mid-game chapter. Castle Salazar is rendered as the franchise's most architecturally ambitious environment — a Spanish medieval-Gothic fortress with sustained interior exploration, the campaign's most extensive puzzle sequences, and the introduction of the campaign's central antagonist Osmund Saddler through environmental detail and recorded audio messages.

    The chapter introduces Ramón Salazar, the eight-foot-tall castellan who has spent generations preparing his bloodline for the Los Iluminados cult's resurgence. Salazar's introduction is staged with deliberate camp menace — he speaks in formal Castilian Spanish, he is rendered as a small dwarf-like figure perched on an absurdly large throne, and his cult's veneration of him is foregrounded with intentional comedic absurdity.

    The chapter's central event is Ashley Graham's rescue. Salazar has held the president's daughter in the castle's upper chambers, and her recovery initiates the campaign's central escort-protection mechanic. Leon must keep Ashley alive across the remaining chapters, with the player commanding her to follow or wait at key combat encounters. The chapter ends with Leon and Ashley descending the castle's interior corridors.

  6. 6

    6. Castle Defenses — Verdugo and the Salazar Gauntlet

    The campaign's mid-act spectacle chapter. Salazar's loyalists have prepared an extensive gauntlet of enemy encounters across the castle's upper galleries, with the chapter's combat density escalating significantly. The chapter introduces several of the franchise's signature antagonist classes — the Garrador, a chained iron-clawed bioweapon that hunts by sound; the Iron Maiden, an upgraded Regenerator variant; and the Novistador, insectoid winged bioweapons.

    The chapter's central boss confrontation is the Verdugo — Salazar's pet bodyguard. The fight is staged in a flooded basement chamber with environmental traps (liquid nitrogen pipes that the player can rupture to freeze Verdugo) that demand mastery of the campaign's environmental-combat repertoire.

    The chapter ends with the Verdugo defeated, the path to Salazar's throne room opened, and Leon and Ashley positioned for the campaign's mid-act climactic confrontation.

  7. 7

    7. The Salazar Confrontation

    The campaign's climactic mid-act boss chapter. Ramón Salazar, having absorbed his castellan-ancestor Plagas as a final transformation, manifests as a massive tentacular bioweapon hung from the castle's central rotunda. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage vertical boss fight, with Salazar's signature combat patterns — long-reach tentacle slams, AOE swarm attacks, the campaign's first sustained boss-arena hazard mechanics — demanding the full toolkit Leon has accumulated.

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's tonal balance. The Salazar fight is openly absurd — the dwarf-like castellan now manifesting as a fifty-foot bioweapon — but the remake stages it with genuine threat density. The chapter ends with Salazar destroyed, the cult's middle-tier leadership eliminated, and the path to the campaign's final region — the island military facility — committed.

  8. 8

    8. The Mines — The Underground Tunnels

    A mid-third-act traversal chapter. The path from the castle to the island runs through a network of mining tunnels and underground caverns that the cult has been using to transport personnel and supplies. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence weaving combat with the franchise's signature environmental investigation.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's first sustained encounter with Krauser, a former U.S. Special Forces operative turned Los Iluminados enforcer. Krauser's introduction is staged through a series of cinematic ambushes and brief combat encounters across the mines, with the chapter dwelling on Leon's recognition that Krauser was once a colleague. The chapter ends with Krauser temporarily withdrawing, the cult's military-grade operational reach exposed, and the path to the island opened.

  9. 9

    9. The Island — Military Facility

    The campaign's third-act setting. The Island is the cult's central military training and operations facility, rendered as a brutalist concrete bunker complex set against rocky coastline. The chapter is structured as a sustained infiltration sequence with combat density escalating to the campaign's highest sustained level. The chapter introduces several enemy variants exclusive to the island — armored Soldados, the campaign's first sustained encounter with Regenerators, and the elite cult enforcers under Krauser's direct command.

    The chapter weaves combat with sustained environmental investigation of the island's infrastructure. Audio logs and documents reveal that the island has been operational for over a decade, that Los Iluminados has been receiving covert international funding from undisclosed parties, and that the cult's broader objective extends beyond the kidnapping of Ashley Graham.

    The chapter ends with Leon located in the island's central command compound, Ashley separated from him during the chapter's mid-section, and the campaign's final boss-confrontation sequences staged.

  10. 10

    10. The Krauser Duel

    The campaign's most acclaimed sustained boss confrontation. Krauser confronts Leon in a sustained one-on-one duel inside the island's central training arena. The chapter is structured as a sustained close-quarters combat sequence, with Krauser's signature combat repertoire (combat knife mastery, mutated arm transformation, military-trained combat instincts) demanding mastery of the campaign's parry-and-counter system.

    The Krauser fight is the franchise's most acclaimed sustained 1-on-1 boss encounter. The fight ranges across the training arena's multi-level architecture, with environmental traps, supporting cult enforcers, and Krauser's three-phase transformation across the encounter. The chapter dwells on Leon and Krauser's history as a brief mid-fight dialogue reveals their prior working relationship.

    The chapter ends with Krauser defeated, Leon's quiet recognition that his former colleague's death is the campaign's most personal loss, and the path to Saddler's central chamber opened.

  11. 11

    11. Saddler — The Cult Leader Confrontation

    The campaign's final boss-confrontation chapter. Osmund Saddler, the leader of Los Iluminados, has prepared his final ritual chamber at the island's central tower. The chapter is structured as a sustained ascent through the tower's multiple levels, with combat against the cult's elite Plagas-host enforcers and the chapter's climactic Saddler boss fight.

    The Saddler boss fight is the campaign's mechanical apex. The cult leader's full Plagas transformation manifests him as a massive multi-eyed organic structure, with the boss fight staged across an arena weaving sustained ranged combat with the campaign's first sustained encounter with Ada Wong as an ally. Ada provides supporting fire during the encounter, with the campaign's final co-op-style combat sequence anchored on her presence.

    The chapter ends with Saddler defeated, the Las Plagas infestation neutralized, and the campaign's final escape sequence committed.

  12. 12

    12. The Escape and Epilogue

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Leon and Ashley flee the collapsing island facility via the franchise's signature jet-ski escape sequence, with the chapter staged as a sustained high-speed traversal across the island's interior canal network. The chapter ends with Leon and Ashley's recovery by U.S. helicopter, the surviving cast extracted, and the franchise's signature epilogue staged.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's character resolutions. Luis Sera's death during the island chapter is acknowledged through a brief Leon monologue, Ashley Graham's formal return to her father's protection is staged with a quiet end-of-mission interaction, and Ada Wong's separate departure (with the Plagas sample she has been hunting throughout the campaign) is left deliberately ambiguous. The credits roll over Leon's signature post-mission flight back to the United States.

    The campaign's post-credits scenes establish the franchise's continuation, with The Mercenaries game mode and Separate Ways Ada Wong DLC explicitly seeded.
