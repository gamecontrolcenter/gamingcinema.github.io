About the Game

Echoes of the End is an Icelandic action-adventure from Myrkur Games following Ryn, a former soldier whose latent magical abilities draw her into a continent-spanning conspiracy alongside companion Abram. Combat blends sword strikes with elemental abilities — telekinesis, ranged blasts, environmental manipulation. The world design draws heavily on Icelandic landscape inspiration, with volcanic ridges and frost-bitten coastlines as the backdrop. The walkthrough captures the full game across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. The Awakening — Ryn's Journey Begins

    The campaign opens with Ryn — the protagonist, a young woman with latent magical abilities — awakening in the fantasy world of Aema, a Norse-influenced landscape designed by the Icelandic developer Myrkur Games as a fusion of medieval European and ancient Nordic aesthetics. The opening sequence is structured as a sustained tutorial-paced introduction to the campaign's reworked traversal fundamentals, with Ryn navigating early forest environments while the player learns the campaign's signature climbing-and-platforming mechanics.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's tonal commitment. Aema is rendered with significantly more environmental texture than typical fantasy-RPG opening environments — extensive ambient soundscapes, painterly visual design, and the campaign's signature attention to natural-world detail. The chapter introduces the campaign's central magic mechanic: the Shift, a telekinetic ability that allows Ryn to manipulate objects (and eventually enemies) across distance.

    The chapter ends with Ryn's first sustained combat encounter — a tutorial-paced introduction to the campaign's parry-and-counter combat fundamentals — and the path to the central village committed.

  2. 2

    2. The Companion — Meeting Abram

    The campaign's first sustained character-introduction chapter. Abram, an older companion-figure with significant prior experience with the campaign's central conflict, joins Ryn at the chapter's mid-section. The chapter is structured as a sustained partnership-building sequence weaving combat against the campaign's first sustained encounters with Glómori — the corrupted-creature antagonist class that has been infesting Aema's countryside.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central character dynamic. Abram and Ryn's partnership is rendered with significantly more conversational warmth than typical fantasy-game companion relationships — sustained casual dialogue, mutual problem-solving across early traversal puzzles, and the franchise's signature optional-conversation system that allows the player to invest in their developing relationship.

    The chapter ends with Ryn and Abram committing to a shared journey, the campaign's central antagonist faction (the corruption infrastructure causing the Glómori manifestations) introduced through environmental detail, and the path deeper into Aema's broader geography opened.

  3. 3

    3. The Durtar — Ancient Artifacts

    The campaign's first sustained worldbuilding chapter. The Durtar are an ancient pre-collapse civilization whose surviving artifacts — recovered across the chapter's exploration — provide the campaign's primary lore foundation. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence through ruined Durtar settlements with significantly increased combat density.

    The chapter weaves combat with the franchise's signature collectibles-and-conversation infrastructure. Journal entries recovered across Durtar sites provide the campaign's most thematically loaded historical context, with the player's accumulated reading material gradually exposing the broader mythological framework underlying the campaign's present-day conflicts.

    The chapter ends with Ryn's growing understanding of her own connection to the Durtar civilization, the campaign's central magical-inheritance subplot fully active, and the path to the campaign's first major boss encounter committed.

  4. 4

    4. The First Awakening — Powers Develop

    The campaign's first sustained character-development chapter. Ryn's latent magical abilities — initially limited to basic Shift telekinesis — manifest at significantly higher operational power. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence with the franchise's first sustained Double-Jump-enabled traversal puzzles, weaving the campaign's expanded movement repertoire with combat against significantly more demanding Glómori variants.

    The chapter dwells on Ryn's character arc. Her growing combat capability, her expanding understanding of her own magical heritage, and her deepening partnership with Abram all anchor the chapter's emotional weight. The chapter ends with Ryn's full Shift repertoire unlocked, the campaign's central magical-power-progression system fully active, and the path to the campaign's first sustained antagonist encounter committed.

  5. 5

    5. Zara — The Antagonist Appears

    The campaign's central antagonist-introduction chapter. Zara — a more powerful magic-user with personal connections to Ryn's family history — confronts Ryn and Abram in a sustained cutscene-and-combat sequence at the chapter's mid-section. The chapter is structured as a sustained character-confrontation chapter weaving exposition with combat across the chapter's runtime.

    The chapter dwells on Zara's character. She is rendered with significantly more emotional complexity than typical fantasy-RPG antagonists, with her grievances against Ryn's family rooted in personal trauma that the campaign treats with sustained earnestness. The chapter introduces the campaign's central antagonist motivation — Zara's pursuit of an ancient power source she believes will restore something that was taken from her.

    The chapter ends with Zara temporarily withdrawing, the campaign's central mid-act pursuit dynamic established, and the path to the campaign's volcanic-region exploration opened.

  6. 6

    6. The Volcanic Region — The Dash Ability

    The campaign's most visually distinctive single chapter. The volcanic region is rendered as the campaign's most thermally intense environment, with sustained traversal across lava-flow geography and the franchise's most acclaimed environmental hazards. The chapter introduces the campaign's signature Dash ability — a quick-movement traversal option that significantly expands Ryn's combat-and-traversal repertoire.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence with the campaign's first sustained encounter with the Eobard boss. Eobard — a fallen ruler whose throne the player can subsequently sit on after the encounter — is rendered with significantly more environmental gravitas than the campaign's prior antagonists. The fight is the campaign's first sustained mechanical apex, demanding mastery of Ryn's full combat repertoire across the encounter's multiple phases.

    The chapter ends with Eobard defeated, the volcanic region's central artifacts recovered, and the path to the campaign's mid-third-act pivot opened.

  7. 7

    7. The Separation — Abram Lost

    The campaign's emotional pivot chapter. Following a sustained combat encounter in the campaign's mid-third act, Ryn is separated from Abram by an environmental catastrophe. The chapter is structured as a sustained solo-exploration sequence with significantly reduced combat density, dedicated entirely to Ryn's character arc and her growing self-sufficiency.

    The chapter dwells on Ryn's character development. Her capacity to function independently of Abram's mentorship — a question the campaign's prior chapters have left deliberately open — is fully answered through the chapter's sustained solo investigation sequences. The chapter ends with Ryn's reunion with Abram, the partnership reframed as a relationship between equals rather than between mentor and student, and the path to the campaign's third-act revelations committed.

  8. 8

    8. The Brother Revealed

    The campaign's central reveal chapter. Ryn's brother — referenced through environmental detail across the prior chapters — is revealed as significantly more central to the campaign's underlying conflict than the player has been led to believe. The chapter is structured as a sustained dialogue-and-investigation sequence with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental storytelling.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central family history. Ryn's brother, her family's broader magical inheritance, and Zara's personal connection to all of the above are reframed across the chapter's narrative beats. The campaign's central political question — whether Zara's grievances are legitimate or whether her methods invalidate them — is rendered with significantly more moral complexity than the campaign's prior characterization implied.

    The chapter ends with the campaign's central mystery fully resolved, the path to Zara's final confrontation committed, and the campaign's third-act setup positioned.

  9. 9

    9. The Water Traversal — Boat Navigation

    The campaign's most acclaimed single traversal chapter. Ryn's pursuit of Zara across Aema's broader geography requires sustained boat-based navigation across the campaign's most expansive water-environment sequences. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence weaving boat-traversal combat with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental design.

    The chapter weaves combat against waterborne Glómori variants with sustained environmental investigation of Aema's surviving coastal settlements. The chapter's central event is the recovery of critical intelligence regarding Zara's central operational base, with the campaign's final assault positioned.

  10. 10

    10. The Final Confrontation — Zara

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Ryn confronts Zara in a sustained boss-fight sequence at the antagonist's central operational base. The chapter is structured as a multi-phase boss confrontation across multiple arena configurations, with Zara's signature combat patterns — magic-user mobility, Shift-style telekinetic counters, and the campaign's most demanding sustained combat encounter — demanding mastery of Ryn's full repertoire.

    The chapter's epilogue is staged in the immediate aftermath of Zara's defeat. Ryn, Abram, and Ryn's brother reunite in the final cutscene, with the campaign's central thematic question — whether family conflicts can ever be fully resolved when the underlying grievances are legitimate — answered through Ryn's accumulated character choices across the campaign's runtime.

    The credits roll over Aema's slow restoration to peace, with the campaign's central question — whether the cycle of magical inheritance can be broken — answered through the franchise's signature qualified hope. The post-launch Enhanced Edition content is explicitly seeded for the franchise's continuation.
