About the Game

Hogwarts Legacy is Avalanche Software's open-world action RPG set in the late 1800s wizarding world, a century before the Harry Potter book series. The player creates a fifth-year transfer student to Hogwarts who possesses rare Ancient Magic, drawn into a goblin rebellion led by Ranrok. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across three parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Welcome to Hogwarts

    The campaign opens in the late nineteenth century with the player's protagonist — a fifteen-year-old fifth-year transfer student — traveling to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry under unusual circumstances. The opening sequence is structured as a Portkey journey across the English countryside, intercepted mid-flight by a goblin-led attack that the campaign quickly establishes as part of a larger insurgency: Ranrok, a goblin warlord, has been conducting raids on wizarding interests across Britain for unclear reasons.

    The chapter's central tutorial sequence is the Gringotts vault. The Portkey wreckage forces the protagonist into the bank's underground depths alongside Professor Eleazar Fig, a Ministry-affiliated wizard who becomes the campaign's primary mentor figure. The Gringotts sequence introduces the basic spell casting mechanics (Lumos, Revelio, Accio, Incendio), the protagonist's growing awareness of an ancient magical sense the campaign calls Ancient Magic, and the first sustained boss encounter — a hostile dragon protecting one of Ranrok's stolen artifacts.

    The chapter ends with the protagonist and Fig escaping Gringotts with the Ancient Magic relic, the campaign's central mystery formally introduced, and the Portkey resuming its interrupted journey to Hogwarts.

  2. 2

    2. The Sorting and First Days

    A character-focused chapter that establishes the protagonist's house affiliation, the campaign's social structure, and the school's central spaces. The Sorting Hat sequence allows the player to choose between the four houses — Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, Slytherin — with each affiliation generating distinct dialogue and access to a unique House-specific quest line woven through the late campaign.

    The chapter's exploration loop opens the Hogwarts castle for the first time: the Grand Staircase, the Great Hall, the dormitories, and the seven major school subjects' classroom locations. The campaign dwells on the school's atmospheric texture — moving paintings, ghost residents (including the Bloody Baron, the Grey Lady, the Fat Friar, and Nearly-Headless Nick), and the day-night cycle that defines the campaign's quest-availability structure.

    The chapter introduces the protagonist's three primary companion characters: Sebastian Sallow (Slytherin), the ambitious and morally complex friend whose family arc anchors the campaign's darkest content; Poppy Sweeting (Hufflepuff), the magical-creature specialist; and Natty Onai (Gryffindor), the Senegalese-British transfer student with her own anti-poaching agenda. Each companion's first-meeting scene establishes their personality.

  3. 3

    3. Magical Classes and the Map Chamber

    A medium-length chapter dedicated to the campaign's spell-acquisition tutorial structure. The first classes — Charms with Professor Ronen, Defense Against the Dark Arts with Professor Hecat, Potions with Professor Sharp, Herbology with Professor Garlick, and Beasts with Professor Howin — each open distinct gameplay systems: spell casting, combat fundamentals, potion brewing, plant cultivation, and creature handling.

    The chapter's central narrative thread is the discovery of the Map Chamber beneath the castle. Professor Fig and the protagonist follow Ancient Magic resonances to a hidden architectural anomaly the school's regular staff cannot perceive. The chamber's central feature is a Pensieve — a memory-vessel — that contains the recordings of four previous wizards known as the Keepers, who hid a major Ancient Magic artifact in the Forbidden Forest centuries earlier. The Keepers' guardian trials structure the campaign's main quest progression.

    The chapter ends with the first Pensieve sequence — Professor Charles Rookwood's recorded message — establishing the campaign's Pensieve-trial loop that will recur four more times across the campaign.

  4. 4

    4. The Goblin Rebellion and Hogsmeade

    The campaign's first sustained open-world chapter. The protagonist accesses Hogsmeade, the wizarding village adjacent to the school, in the middle of a Ranrok-led raid. The chapter is structured as a hub introduction — Hogsmeade's shops, vendors, and quest hubs — wrapped around a sustained combat encounter against goblin raiders in the village square.

    The chapter introduces Ominis Gaunt, Sebastian's blind companion and one of the campaign's most morally complex supporting characters. Ominis's family heritage — direct descent from Salazar Slytherin — and his strong moral opposition to the Dark Arts make him a counterweight to Sebastian's increasingly transgressive arc. The chapter dwells on the tensions between Ominis and Sebastian in ways that anticipate the campaign's darkest companion content.

    The chapter ends with the Hogsmeade raid repulsed, the protagonist's broom flight unlocked, and the campaign's open-world map expanded to include the Highland surroundings.

  5. 5

    5. The First Keeper Trial — Percival Rackham

    The campaign's first sustained dungeon sequence. The protagonist, guided by Professor Fig and the Pensieve's recorded instructions, accesses Percival Rackham's trial in the wilderness south of Hogwarts. The trial is structured as a multi-chamber puzzle-and-combat sequence inside an ancient Keeper-built fortress, with the campaign's spell-puzzle system fully introduced.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's first sustained encounter with Inferi — reanimated corpses that have been a Dark Arts staple of the broader franchise. The Inferi force the player to integrate fire-based spells into their combat rotation, and the encounter establishes the campaign's late-game combat density.

    The chapter ends with the Rackham trial completed, the protagonist's growing mastery of Ancient Magic confirmed, and the second Keeper — Niamh Fitzgerald — named as the next objective.

  6. 6

    6. The Forbidden Forest — Poppy's Arc

    A character-focused chapter that opens the Forbidden Forest as a sustained exploration biome. The chapter's central thread is Poppy Sweeting's investigation into magical creature poaching, with the protagonist accompanying her on a series of rescue missions that take the campaign through some of its most visually distinctive environments.

    The chapter introduces the Vivariums — magical creature sanctuaries inside the Room of Requirement that the protagonist uses to house rescued creatures across the campaign. The vivarium loop introduces the campaign's most sustained collectible side activity, with creatures providing materials for the campaign's gear-upgrade system.

    The chapter ends with the protagonist and Poppy uncovering the poaching network's leadership, the Forbidden Forest opened to free exploration, and the campaign's third-act enemy faction — the Ashwinder Cult of Dark wizard partisans — introduced.

  7. 7

    7. The Second Keeper Trial — Niamh Fitzgerald

    The campaign's mid-game Keeper trial. The protagonist accesses Niamh Fitzgerald's chambers via a hidden Highland location, with the chapter's central sequence staged as a sustained gauntlet of magical puzzles and combat encounters. The trial introduces the Disillusionment Charm stealth mechanics that the campaign will rely on for several later infiltration sequences.

    The chapter's exploration loop opens the campaign's Highland mid-game region, including the Forbidden Forest's deeper layers and the Hogsmeade catacombs. The chapter dwells on the campaign's growing political complexity — Ranrok's goblin insurgency is now overtly coordinating with Ashwinder Cult forces, and Professor Fig's investigation has uncovered evidence that the Ministry of Magic is suppressing intelligence about the Repository the Keepers protected.

    The chapter ends with the second Keeper trial completed, the third Keeper — Charles Rookwood — named, and the campaign's mid-act political crisis fully active.

  8. 8

    8. Sebastian's Arc — The Family Trial

    The campaign's darkest sustained companion arc and one of its most acclaimed character sequences. Sebastian Sallow — the protagonist's Slytherin companion — has been struggling with his sister Anne's deteriorating health from an unidentified Dark curse, and the chapter is structured as a sustained moral arc in which Sebastian increasingly pushes the protagonist toward the Unforgivable Curses as the only path to saving her.

    The chapter's exploration loop opens the Sallow family crypt in the Highland countryside, with the protagonist navigating Dark Arts-protected chambers and confronting the curse's true source. The chapter introduces the three Unforgivable Curses as player-acquirable spells — Crucio, Imperio, and Avada Kedavra — with the campaign explicitly not blocking the player from using them at any point in the rest of the game.

    The chapter's emotional centerpiece is the Solomon Sallow confrontation — Sebastian's uncle, who has been resisting Sebastian's attempts to save Anne through the Dark Arts. The chapter ends with Solomon's death at Sebastian's hand and the protagonist's choice of whether to cover for him with Ominis. Either choice has campaign-spanning consequences.

  9. 9

    9. The Third Keeper Trial — San Bakar

    The campaign's most spectacle-heavy Keeper trial. San Bakar's chambers are accessible only via Hippogriff flight, and the chapter's central sequence is staged across a mountain-cliff fortress with extensive aerial combat. The trial introduces the campaign's most demanding combat scenarios — Inferi, Dark wizards, and the campaign's first sustained dragon encounter.

    The chapter's exploration loop opens the campaign's southern map regions, including Feldcroft village and the Cragcroft mountain passes. The chapter dwells on Natty Onai's anti-poaching investigation, with her family history in colonial-era Senegal foregrounded in ways the campaign uses to expand the wizarding world beyond its English center.

    The chapter ends with the third Keeper trial completed, the fourth Keeper — Charles Rookwood — finally named, and Ranrok's goblin army now openly mobilizing against Hogwarts.

  10. 10

    10. The High Keep — Rookwood Confrontation

    The campaign's third-act opening. Theophilus Rookwood, the Dark wizard leader of the Ashwinder Cult and the descendant of the original Keeper Charles Rookwood, has taken possession of the High Keep — a fortified mountain fortress that holds the key to the Repository. The chapter is structured as a sustained assault sequence, with the protagonist and Professor Fig leading a small force of allied wizards against Rookwood's Ashwinder defenders.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's most sustained Dark wizard duels, with Rookwood's lieutenants demanding mastery of the Unforgivable Curses or sustained spell-rotation builds. The chapter's central confrontation is with Rookwood himself, a multi-phase boss fight that demands the campaign's full spell repertoire.

    The chapter ends with Rookwood defeated, the Repository's location finally confirmed, and the campaign's fourth and final Keeper trial — Charles Rookwood's own recorded chamber — accessible.

  11. 11

    11. The Final Keeper Trial and the Repository

    The campaign's exposition climax. The fourth Keeper trial — staged in Charles Rookwood's original chambers beneath the Forbidden Forest — reveals the full history of the Ancient Magic Repository. The Keepers locked it away because Ancient Magic, while potentially transformative, carries with it the temptation to corrupt every wielder who has ever sought to control it. The chapter is structured as the campaign's most narrative-dense sequence, with sustained Pensieve exposition.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central moral question: whether to destroy the Repository (as the Keepers intended), seal it (a compromise the Keepers ultimately rejected), or use it (the path every villain in the campaign has been pursuing). The decision is the campaign's defining player choice.

    The chapter ends with Ranrok's army now visibly assembled at the Repository's surface entrance, the goblin warlord himself overseeing the assault, and the campaign's final boss confrontation imminent.

  12. 12

    12. The Final Battle — Ranrok

    The campaign's climactic boss fight. Ranrok has been steadily absorbing Ancient Magic across the campaign and is now functionally equivalent to a small wizard army on his own. The boss fight is staged in three escalating phases inside the Repository chamber: ground-based dueling, an aerial-platform sequence as the chamber's architecture begins collapsing, and a final dragon-form transformation that demands every spell the campaign has taught.

    The chapter's emotional centerpiece is Professor Fig's death during the third phase. Fig — the campaign's primary mentor figure and the only adult who has consistently believed in the protagonist's capability — sacrifices himself to weaken Ranrok at the critical moment. The chapter dwells on the loss before transitioning to the campaign's player-choice ending.

    The chapter ends with Ranrok defeated, the Repository's fate determined by the protagonist's choice, and Hogwarts emerging from the campaign's central crisis. The Ministry of Magic arrives to handle the cleanup, with the political consequences left deliberately ambiguous.

  13. 13

    13. End-of-Year Feast and Epilogue

    The campaign's epilogue is staged at the End-of-Year Feast in the Great Hall. The protagonist's House is celebrated for the year's achievement, with the House Cup awarded based on accumulated activity across the campaign. The chapter dwells on the protagonist's relationships — with Sebastian (still struggling with Anne's condition), Ominis (recovering from the Sallow family revelations), Poppy (now leading the school's magical-creature protection efforts), and Natty (returning to Senegal for the summer).

    The chapter's final scenes set up the campaign's post-game content — the summer field excursions that continue across the British countryside and the House-specific quest lines that diverge based on the player's Sorting. The credits roll over a sustained aerial shot of Hogwarts castle from the broom-flight perspective. The campaign's central question — what the protagonist will become with the Ancient Magic now in their possession — is left explicitly open for the franchise's anticipated continuation.
