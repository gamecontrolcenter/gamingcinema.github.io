About the Game

Resident Evil 2 Remake is Capcom's 2019 reimagining of the 1998 PlayStation classic, rebuilt in the RE Engine with over-the-shoulder controls and reactive zombie AI. Rookie cop Leon Kennedy and college student Claire Redfield navigate the doomed Raccoon City Police Department on his first day on the job, the same night the T-Virus outbreak transforms the city. This walkthrough captures the full playthrough on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary, focused on cinematic storytelling.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. The Gas Station — Outbreak Begins

    The campaign opens on September 29, 1998, with rookie police officer Leon S. Kennedy stopping at a deserted gas station on his way to his first day at the Raccoon City Police Department. The opening sequence is structured as a sustained survival-horror tutorial, with Leon discovering the gas station's clerk transformed into one of the franchise's signature zombies and his car stolen by a desperate truck driver.

    The chapter introduces Claire Redfield, a college student who has arrived in Raccoon City searching for her missing brother Chris. The chapter dwells on the campaign's central conceit: Leon and Claire's paths cross at the gas station, are separated by the truck driver's catastrophic accident, and re-converge throughout the campaign across the dual-scenario structure that defines the franchise's classic design.

    The chapter ends with the truck's explosion destroying the gas station, Leon and Claire separated on opposite sides of the wreckage, and the agreement to meet at the Raccoon Police Department — neither yet aware that the entire city is in catastrophic collapse.

  2. 2

    2. The Streets of Raccoon City

    The campaign's first sustained street-level chapter. Leon and Claire — playable separately in their respective scenarios — navigate the burning streets of downtown Raccoon City toward the police department. The chapter is structured as a sustained survival-horror combat sequence, with the campaign's combat fundamentals fully introduced: limited ammunition, slow-reload pistol handling, the headshot mechanic, and the campaign's signature inventory management with combinable items.

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's atmospheric foundations. Raccoon City is rendered with significantly more environmental detail than the original 1998 release — overturned police cruisers, abandoned ambulances, civilian corpses, and burning buildings establish the campaign's tonal commitment to apocalyptic scale.

    The chapter ends with Leon and Claire reaching the Raccoon Police Department through separate routes, with the chapter's final combat sequences against the city's first zombie hordes establishing the franchise's signature claustrophobic combat rhythm.

  3. 3

    3. The RPD Main Hall — Lieutenant Marvin

    The campaign's hub introduction. The Raccoon Police Department is rendered as the franchise's most acclaimed environmental design — a converted art museum that has become the city's central law-enforcement facility, with neoclassical interior architecture overlaid with mid-1990s police infrastructure. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration of the main hall and surrounding offices.

    The chapter introduces Lieutenant Marvin Branagh, the dying officer who briefs Leon and Claire on the outbreak's scope and the RPD's deteriorating situation. Marvin's voice-acted performance — across both Leon's and Claire's scenarios — has become one of the franchise's most acclaimed supporting roles. The chapter dwells on the recognition that the RPD's officers have been systematically transformed into zombies during the campaign's offscreen events.

    The chapter ends with the campaign's central key-acquisition loop established (Spade, Diamond, Heart, Club emblems hidden throughout the building) and the campaign's exploration structure fully active.

  4. 4

    4. The RPD East Wing — Spade and Diamond

    The campaign's first sustained dungeon-style exploration. The east wing of the police department contains the Library, the STARS office, and the Watchtower — each with sustained puzzle sequences that lock progress behind environmental investigation. The chapter weaves combat with the campaign's hardest sustained puzzle sequences.

    The chapter introduces the Library bookcase puzzle — the franchise's signature physical-manipulation challenge in which the player must physically push library shelves into specific configurations to reach an upper-level gangway. The chapter also stages the STARS office with significant lore implications, as Claire's investigation specifically targets evidence of her brother Chris's prior assignment.

    The chapter's central enemy introduction is the Licker — the eyeless, blade-tongued bioweapon that has become one of the franchise's most iconic enemies. The Licker's introduction is staged with deliberate menace through the east wing's tight corridors. The chapter ends with the east wing's emblems collected and the path to the west wing unlocked.

  5. 5

    5. The Parking Garage — Mr. X Arrives

    The campaign's mid-game escalation. The RPD parking garage sequence introduces Mr. X — the Tyrant bioweapon that becomes the campaign's most acclaimed pursuit antagonist. Mr. X is introduced through a sustained corridor pursuit sequence that the campaign stages with deliberate horror-film pacing.

    The Mr. X mechanic is the campaign's defining systems-design choice. The Tyrant pursues the player throughout the rest of the RPD's interior, with footstep audio that telegraphs his approach but no fixed patrol patterns. The chapter dwells on the player's resulting paranoia — every interior crossing now carries the possibility of Tyrant intervention, and the campaign's pacing permanently shifts.

    The chapter ends with Leon and Claire's separate Mr. X encounters reframing the entire RPD exploration loop, and the campaign's mid-act setup pivoting to the underground passages beneath the police department.

  6. 6

    6. The West Wing — Heart and Club

    The campaign's library-and-darkroom exploration. The west wing's chambers contain the Darkroom, the Interrogation Room, and the Records Room — each with sustained environmental investigation. The chapter weaves combat against Lickers and Zombies with the campaign's most acclaimed puzzle sequences, including the chess piece elevator puzzle that has become a fan favorite.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's character development. Leon's growing attachment to Ada Wong (in Leon's scenario) and Claire's growing attachment to Sherry Birkin (in Claire's scenario) introduce the campaign's two parallel character arcs. The chapter ends with the west wing's secrets fully recovered and the path to the underground unlocked.

  7. 7

    7. The Sewers — Beneath the City

    The campaign's mid-third-act sewer exploration. Beneath the RPD, the city's sewer system has been converted into a Umbrella experimental waste-disposal network. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-level exploration with the franchise's most acclaimed claustrophobic environments. Combat density increases significantly, with the Giant Alligator mini-boss confrontation staged as the chapter's spectacle highlight.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's parallel-scenario divergence in its sharpest form. Leon's sewer route stages his pursuit of Annette Birkin, the wife of Umbrella's lead researcher; Claire's parallel sewer route stages her growing relationship with Sherry, Annette's daughter, who has been hiding in the sewers since the outbreak began.

    The chapter ends with the sewers' central treatment-pool chamber reached, the campaign's first sustained boss confrontation against William Birkin (G-Form 2) committed, and the path to the underground NEST facility opened.

  8. 8

    8. NEST — The Underground Lab

    The campaign's third-act setting. NEST is Umbrella's classified research facility buried beneath Raccoon City, accessed via a sustained funicular ride that the chapter stages with the franchise's most acclaimed transition cinematic. The facility is rendered as a multi-level pharmaceutical-research complex with the campaign's hardest sustained combat encounters.

    The chapter introduces the G-virus in full. William Birkin's experimental research, conducted across decades inside NEST, has produced a bioweapon that has now metastasized into Birkin himself. The chapter weaves combat with sustained environmental investigation, with Birkin's audio logs detailing the moral collapse of the entire research project.

    The chapter's mid-section stages the campaign's most demanding puzzle sequences and the first sustained encounter with the Ivy enemy class — plant-based bioweapons that demand fire-based combat rather than ammunition. The chapter ends with the NEST's central chamber accessed, the G-virus sample recovered, and the campaign's final boss confrontation imminent.

  9. 9

    9. William Birkin — The G-Form Confrontations

    The campaign's climactic boss-encounter chapter. William Birkin — having undergone multiple G-virus transformations across the campaign — manifests in his final form for the campaign's central confrontation. The chapter is structured as a multi-phase boss fight across multiple NEST chambers, with the G-Form bioweapon's escalating mutations forcing the player to integrate every weapon the campaign has provided.

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's tragic-monster archetype. Birkin's slow loss of humanity, traced across audio logs and environmental detail, makes the boss confrontation the franchise's most thematically loaded sustained combat sequence. The fight ends with Birkin's defeat, the NEST's self-destruct sequence initiated, and the campaign's final escape sequence committed.

  10. 10

    10. The Final Escape — The Train

    The campaign's epilogue. Leon, Claire, and Sherry Birkin (now formally Claire's protected dependent) attempt to escape the collapsing NEST via the facility's emergency train evacuation route. The chapter is structured as a sustained pursuit sequence with the final transformed G-Form Birkin chasing the escape train.

    The chapter's final boss confrontation is staged inside the escape train itself, with the player using the franchise's signature emergency rocket launcher to deliver the killing blow. The chapter ends with Birkin destroyed, Raccoon City's destruction confirmed via the U.S. military's sterilization protocol mentioned in news radio, and Leon, Claire, and Sherry emerging from the surrounding wilderness at dawn.

    The campaign's epilogue cinematics — different for each scenario — establish the franchise's continuation. Leon's commitment to bring Umbrella down sets up Resident Evil 4. Claire's commitment to protect Sherry establishes the surrogate-family arc that Resident Evil Requiem eventually revisits through Leon's parallel adoption of similar responsibility for Grace's daughter Emily.
