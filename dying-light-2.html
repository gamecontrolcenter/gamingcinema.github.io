About the Game

Dying Light 2: Stay Human is Techland's open-world parkour-zombie sequel set 15 years after the original. Aiden Caldwell, a Pilgrim, arrives in the City — one of the last human settlements — searching for his missing sister. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary. Both day and night gameplay loops are preserved with their distinct combat tones.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. The Pilgrim — Aiden Arrives

    The campaign opens twenty-two years after the original Dying Light, with the world having fully collapsed under the Harran Virus pandemic. The protagonist Aiden Caldwell — a "Pilgrim," a member of the post-collapse traveler caste who carries messages and goods between the few surviving urban settlements — approaches the campaign's central city, Villedor, in pursuit of his missing sister Mia.

    The opening sequence is structured as a sustained tutorial-paced introduction to the campaign's reworked parkour fundamentals. Dying Light 2's movement system is significantly more refined than the original game's — wall-running, dynamic vaulting, paragliding, and the franchise's signature day-night mechanics are all reintroduced through the opening chapter's exploration of Villedor's outer ruins.

    The chapter ends with Aiden's arrival at Villedor's central gate, the campaign's central conflict (Aiden's infection, his pursuit of the man named Waltz who tortured him and his sister as children) introduced, and the city's interior districts opened to exploration.

  2. 2

    2. Old Villedor — The Bazaar

    The campaign's first sustained social-investigation chapter. Aiden enters Old Villedor, the city's eastern district, controlled by the Survivors faction — a community of post-collapse civilians organized around mutual support and democratic governance. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration of the Bazaar, the district's central marketplace, with the campaign's first sustained character-introduction sequences.

    The chapter introduces several of the campaign's primary supporting characters: Hakon, an enigmatic information broker; Aitor, the Bazaar's elected leader; Sophie, a Survivor militia commander; and Frank, Sophie's brother and Aiden's first stable contact. The chapter dwells on the franchise's central political dynamic — the Survivors faction's idealism contrasts with the Peacekeepers faction's authoritarian pragmatism, and the campaign's choice architecture is built around the player's accumulated faction preferences.

    The chapter ends with Aiden establishing his temporary base in the Bazaar, the campaign's central social network mapped, and the path to Hakon's intelligence on Waltz committed.

  3. 3

    3. The Bazaar Assault — First Major Encounter

    The campaign's first sustained combat chapter. The Bazaar comes under coordinated assault by Renegades, a faction of post-collapse raiders operating from the city's southern industrial districts. The chapter is structured as a sustained defensive combat sequence weaving combat against human enemies with the franchise's signature infected combat fundamentals.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's signature day-night dynamic at full operational intensity. Aiden's combat capability is significantly stronger during daylight hours; nighttime introduces the campaign's most dangerous infected variants — Volatiles that hunt by sound and movement — alongside the night-only stamina regeneration mechanic that allows the player to grind out infected combat encounters specifically during darkness.

    The chapter ends with the Bazaar's defense secured, the campaign's central social network reinforced, and the path to the city's central districts opened.

  4. 4

    4. The Central Loop — Crossing the Bridge

    The campaign's mid-act geography expansion. Aiden completes a multi-stage investigation that culminates in his crossing the Bridge — Villedor's signature parkour set-piece — into the Central Loop, the city's western district controlled by the rival Peacekeepers faction. The chapter is structured as a sustained vertical traversal sequence with the campaign's most acclaimed parkour combat encounters.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's central political crisis. The Central Loop's Peacekeepers, led by Colonel Williams, operate under a significantly more authoritarian framework — public executions of infected suspects, mandatory infection-status checks at district boundaries, and the franchise's most morally compromised central command structure. The chapter dwells on Aiden's choice between supporting the Peacekeepers' more functional but ethically suspect governance or the Survivors' more democratic but militarily vulnerable approach.

    The chapter ends with Aiden's first major faction-allegiance decision required, the Central Loop's exploration loop fully active, and the campaign's central political conflict committed.

  5. 5

    5. The Markers of Plague — Hakon's Betrayal

    The campaign's mid-game pivot chapter. Hakon — the information broker who has been guiding Aiden through the city's central conspiracies — is revealed as having a longer history with Waltz, Aiden's central antagonist, than he has admitted. The chapter is structured as a sustained pursuit-and-confrontation sequence across the city's rooftop network, with Hakon attempting to escape Aiden's confrontation through the franchise's most acclaimed sustained chase sequence.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central character pivot. Hakon's complicated relationship to Waltz — partner, victim, and reluctant collaborator across multiple decades — reframes the campaign's central conflict from straightforward revenge into something significantly more morally compromised.

    The chapter ends with Hakon's apparent death (the player's choice can affect this), Waltz's location confirmed in the city's central administrative complex, and the campaign's third-act pursuit committed.

  6. 6

    6. The Cathedral — Waltz's Sanctuary

    The campaign's third-act setting. The Cathedral — the city's central religious complex, converted into Waltz's personal research facility — is rendered as the campaign's most architecturally ambitious environment. The chapter is structured as a sustained infiltration sequence with the campaign's most demanding sustained combat encounters.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's central scientific subplot. Waltz has been conducting experimental research on a potential cure for the Harran Virus across decades, with the cathedral's research wings revealing both genuine scientific breakthroughs and significant ethical violations. The chapter dwells on Aiden's growing recognition that Mia — his sister, whom he has been searching for since the opening chapter — has been one of Waltz's primary research subjects across the intervening years.

    The chapter ends with Mia located in the cathedral's central chamber, Waltz's confrontation with Aiden positioned, and the campaign's emotional climax committed.

  7. 7

    7. The Waltz Confrontation

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Waltz, the man who tortured Aiden and Mia as children, confronts Aiden inside the cathedral's research wing. The chapter is structured as a sustained dialogue-and-combat sequence, with the campaign's central revelation about Waltz's actual identity and motivations delivered across multiple narrative beats.

    The chapter's central reveal is that Waltz is Aiden and Mia's biological father. The "torturer" framing across the campaign's prior chapters has been Aiden's traumatized misremembering of childhood scientific experimentation Waltz conducted on his own children — experiments that Waltz framed at the time as essential medical research, and that Mia has spent the intervening years either forgiving or refusing to forgive depending on the player's prior choices.

    The chapter ends with Waltz's defeat or escape (depending on player choices), the campaign's central family revelation processed, and the campaign's final moral decision staged.

  8. 8

    8. The Final Choice — Ending Configurations

    The campaign's climactic decision chapter. Aiden, having confronted Waltz and reunited with Mia, faces the campaign's central political decision: who controls the city after the crisis is resolved? The player's accumulated faction-allegiance choices across the campaign determine the available endings, with the chapter staged as a sustained set of dialogue-and-action decisions across the city's central infrastructure.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central thematic argument. Villedor's survival requires governance, and no governance configuration is morally clean. The chapter's possible endings include Survivors-controlled democracy (idealistic but vulnerable), Peacekeepers-controlled authoritarianism (stable but morally compromised), Renegades-controlled anarchy (visceral but unsustainable), and several hybrid configurations depending on the player's specific accumulated choices.

    The chapter ends with the player's chosen ending played out across the city's epilogue cinematics, with the campaign's central question — what kind of city can survive the post-collapse world — answered through the player's accumulated political commitments rather than through any single triumphant resolution.

  9. 9

    9. Epilogue — Aiden Departs

    The campaign's epilogue chapter is staged as a quiet sustained sequence with no combat. Aiden, Mia reunited, prepares to depart Villedor on a new Pilgrim contract — the city's broader regional network has been disrupted by Waltz's research operations, and the surviving Pilgrim community needs Aiden's continued commitment to maintain communication between the few surviving settlements across the post-collapse landscape.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's character resolutions. Mia's recovery is rendered with deliberate ambiguity — she is alive, she has reconnected with her brother, but the years of Waltz's research have left permanent psychological consequences that the campaign refuses to soften. The chapter ends with Aiden's departure from Villedor, the city's chosen governance configuration visible across the cinematic, and the campaign's central question — whether the post-collapse world is salvageable — answered with the franchise's signature qualified hope.

    The credits roll over Aiden's solitary departure into the surrounding ruins, with the post-launch expansion content (Bloody Ties, Tower Raid) explicitly seeded as the franchise's continuation.
