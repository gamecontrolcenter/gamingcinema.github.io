About the Game

Marvel's Spider-Man: Miles Morales is the standalone Insomniac follow-up to the 2018 original, following Miles Morales' first solo Spider-Man Christmas in a snow-covered Harlem. Peter Parker is away in Europe so Miles takes up the mantle alone against the Tinkerer and the Roxxon Energy corporation. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Be Yourself

    The campaign opens with a sustained highway pursuit sequence in which Miles Morales — newly trained by Peter Parker — escorts a Department of Damage Control transport carrying Rhino through Manhattan. The chapter is structured as the franchise's most spectacle-heavy opening, with Miles and Peter working in tandem across an extended high-speed chase that demonstrates Miles's distinct combat identity (bio-electric Venom Strike attacks, camouflage stealth) before introducing the campaign's central tragedy.

    The chapter ends with Peter Parker leaving New York for a vacation with MJ in Europe, formally entrusting the city's protection to Miles for the campaign's duration. The chapter's emotional pivot is Miles's growing sense that he must define himself on his own terms rather than as Peter's apprentice.

  2. 2

    2. Welcome Home — Harlem

    The campaign's hub introduction. Miles moves with his mother Rio Morales into a new apartment in Harlem, the neighborhood that will serve as the campaign's primary open-world space. The chapter weaves combat against street-level crime with the establishment of Miles's social network — his uncle Aaron Davis, his best friend Ganke Lee, and the broader Harlem community.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's central political backdrop. Rio Morales is running for City Council on an anti-Roxxon platform, with Roxxon Energy Corporation having begun a controversial energy-research facility in the neighborhood. The Roxxon Plaza tower has become the chapter's primary villain — visible from every Harlem street, casting permanent shadow over the community.

    The chapter ends with the first sightings of the Underground, a Harlem-based resistance movement whose tactics against Roxxon have escalated into open street combat.

  3. 3

    3. Roxxon Plaza — First Investigation

    The campaign's first sustained antagonist confrontation. Miles infiltrates Roxxon Plaza to investigate reports of unauthorized human experimentation. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage stealth-and-combat sequence through the tower's research wings, with the campaign's first sustained encounter with both Roxxon's private security forces and the Underground's masked operatives.

    The chapter introduces Simon Krieger, Roxxon's CEO, as the campaign's primary corporate antagonist. Krieger's public-facing reformist message — that Roxxon's experimental energy research will benefit Harlem — is set against the chapter's revealed reality of forced human testing and political bribery. The chapter ends with Miles's first sighting of the Tinkerer, the Underground's masked leader, who escapes the rooftop confrontation with Krieger's stolen prototype.

  4. 4

    4. The Underground — Phin's Connection

    The campaign's character pivot. The chapter reveals that the Tinkerer is Phin Mason — Miles's childhood best friend, who he hasn't seen since her brother Rick Mason died working for Roxxon. The chapter is structured as a series of flashback sequences interleaved with present-day combat, with Phin's grief over Rick's death establishing the campaign's central emotional conflict.

    The chapter dwells on the moral weight of the Underground's mission. Phin's faction is fighting for genuine justice — Rick's death was preventable, Roxxon's energy research is killing people, and the corporate response has been bribery and threats. Miles's role as Spider-Man is the chapter's central question: he is sworn to protect the city, but the city's actual enemy is the corporation his mother is running against.

    The chapter ends with Miles and Phin's first confrontation as Spider-Man versus the Tinkerer, with both refusing to back down from their respective causes.

  5. 5

    5. Family History — Aaron Davis Revealed

    The campaign's emotional pivot. Miles discovers that his uncle Aaron Davis is the Prowler — a high-end thief working for Roxxon's intelligence operations, including the surveillance program that tracked Rick Mason before his death. The chapter is structured as a sustained confrontation in the Davis family's Harlem apartment, with the moral weight of the revelation landing on Miles's relationship with the man who has been his most consistent adult mentor.

    The chapter dwells on Aaron's motivations. He has been working for Roxxon to support the family financially after Rio's late husband Jefferson's death; his thefts have funded Miles's tuition and Rio's political campaign. The chapter's central question — whether complicity is forgivable when the alternative is family destitution — is left explicitly open.

    The chapter ends with Aaron arrested by NYPD as Miles watches from across the street, the family's secret revealed publicly, and Rio's political campaign now compromised by the connection.

  6. 6

    6. The Tinkerer Revealed — The Underground's Plan

    The campaign's second-act pivot. Phin Mason publicly reveals her identity as the Tinkerer in a coordinated assault on Roxxon Plaza. The chapter is structured as a citywide combat sequence, with the Underground deploying weaponized technology against Roxxon's private security across multiple districts.

    The chapter's central reveal is the Nuform crisis. The Underground has stolen Krieger's experimental Nuform energy reactor, which Phin intends to detonate in Roxxon Plaza as both vengeance and a demonstration. The detonation would destroy the tower — and likely most of Harlem alongside it. The chapter dwells on Phin's emotional state: she has known about the Nuform's instability all along, and she has been willing to accept the casualties.

    The chapter ends with the Underground's planning fully exposed, Phin committed to the detonation, and Miles racing across the city to prevent the catastrophe.

  7. 7

    7. The Roxxon Plaza Assault

    The campaign's spectacle-heavy mid-third-act chapter. Miles infiltrates Roxxon Plaza during the active Underground assault, with the chapter weaving sustained combat against both factions simultaneously. The chapter introduces the campaign's most demanding combat encounters, with Krieger's Roxxon enforcers deploying anti-Spider-Man weaponry and the Underground's operatives using bio-electric counter-tech.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central moral compromise. Miles must defeat both factions — the corporate antagonist whose villainy created the crisis, and the rebellion whose response will kill thousands of innocent people. The campaign refuses to grant Miles a clean victory in either direction.

    The chapter ends with Krieger captured by Miles, the Roxxon Plaza tower partially evacuated, and Phin still committed to the Nuform detonation despite her allies' second thoughts.

  8. 8

    8. Brooklyn Bridge — The Final Chase

    The campaign's vertical action set-piece. Phin, in possession of the Nuform reactor, attempts to detonate it at maximum altitude over Brooklyn Bridge to maximize the damage radius. The chapter is structured as a sustained aerial pursuit, with Miles using his expanded Venom-Strike repertoire to chase Phin across the bridge's superstructure.

    The chapter's emotional pivot is the final confrontation. Miles catches Phin mid-flight, and the conversation that follows — staged on the bridge's central span — refuses every easy resolution. Phin is not a villain. She is a grieving sister whose city has refused justice. Miles is not a clean hero. His family has been complicit in the system that killed her brother.

    The chapter ends with Phin's sacrifice. She chooses to fly the Nuform reactor far enough into the atmosphere that its detonation will not harm Harlem, dying in the explosion to give the city the safety her brother never received. Miles's grief is the campaign's emotional climax.

  9. 9

    9. Aftermath — Penn Station

    The campaign's epilogue chapter. The detonation's shockwave has damaged Penn Station and surrounding infrastructure. The chapter is structured as a sustained civilian-rescue sequence across Harlem and Midtown, with Miles using his full Spider-Man toolkit to evacuate trapped commuters and stabilize the damaged buildings.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's quiet emotional resolution. Rio Morales wins her City Council race. Aaron Davis accepts his sentence with dignity. Ganke processes the loss of Phin alongside Miles. The chapter ends with Miles standing on a Harlem rooftop, formally accepting the Spider-Man identity as his own — not as Peter Parker's apprentice but as the protector of his own neighborhood.

  10. 10

    10. The Hero of Harlem — Epilogue

    The campaign's final chapter is staged across a sustained day in Miles's life as Harlem's Spider-Man. The chapter weaves several small open-world activities with the campaign's character resolutions — Miles's growing relationship with Hailey Cooper, a deaf Brooklyn Visions student; his deepening bond with his mother; and his commitment to the neighborhood he has now formally adopted as his domain.

    The chapter's final scene is a video call between Miles and Peter (returning from Europe), with Peter formally recognizing Miles as a full Spider-Man rather than an apprentice. The credits roll over Miles's first sustained swing through Harlem at sunset, with the campaign's central question — who Miles Morales is as a hero — answered in the only way the franchise permits: on his own terms.
