About the Game

Stellar Blade is Shift Up's action title following EVE, a member of the 7th Airborne Squad sent from a space colony to reclaim Earth from the Naytibas, mutated creatures who drove humanity off the planet. The fast, precision-based combat draws comparison to Sekiro and NieR, with parries, dodges, and Beta/Burst skill trees. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across three parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Eidos 7 — The 7th Airborne Squad

    The campaign opens with EVE — a member of the 7th Airborne Squad, an elite force of Angels descended from the orbital Mother Sphere — leading a mass airborne assault on Eidos 7, one of Earth's last surviving cities. The opening sequence is structured as a sustained spectacle set-piece: dozens of Angels falling from orbit, the city's surface combat erupting on contact, and EVE's first sustained combat encounters against the Naytibas — the biological monstrosities that have overrun Earth.

    The opening dwells on the campaign's central premise. Humanity, driven from Earth's surface generations ago by the Naytiba invasion, has retreated to the Mother Sphere orbital habitat. The 7th Airborne Squad has been dispatched to reclaim Earth one city at a time, with EVE's mission specifically targeting an Alpha Naytiba — a high-tier specimen whose elimination would significantly weaken the regional Naytiba ecosystem.

    The chapter's pivot is the annihilation of the 7th Airborne. Within the first hour, the entire squad is slaughtered by the Alpha Naytiba and its accompanying forces, with EVE's mentor Tachy dying in EVE's arms during the chapter's mid-section. EVE survives only through the intervention of Adam, a Xion scavenger who pulls her unconscious body to safety. The chapter ends with EVE awakening in Adam's care, the campaign's central mission (eliminating the Alpha Naytiba) reaffirmed, and the path through Eidos 7's ruins committed.

  2. 2

    2. The Wasteland — Approaching Xion

    The campaign's first sustained open-zone chapter. EVE and Adam traverse the Wasteland — the irradiated post-collapse landscape between Eidos 7 and the surviving human city of Xion. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence with the franchise's signature combat fundamentals fully introduced: EVE's dual-blade combat, the parry-and-counter system, the Burst energy-meter mechanic, and the campaign's most demanding sustained Naytiba combat encounters.

    The chapter weaves combat with sustained character-development sequences. Adam, initially rendered as a self-interested scavenger, gradually emerges as the campaign's most morally complex supporting character — a former Xion citizen with significantly more historical knowledge of the Mother Sphere's operations than he initially admits. The chapter dwells on Adam's quiet skepticism toward EVE's mission directives.

    The chapter ends with EVE and Adam's arrival at Xion's outer gates, the campaign's first sustained social environment opened, and the path to the survivor prophet Orcal committed.

  3. 3

    3. Xion — The Last Human City

    The campaign's central hub chapter. Xion is rendered as the most architecturally distinctive single environment in the campaign — a vertical multi-level survivor settlement built into and around the ruins of a pre-collapse skyscraper complex, protected from Naytiba incursion by a magnetic field powered by a "personal link" maintained by Orcal himself.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained social-exploration sequence with no combat. EVE meets the city's central inhabitants — Roxanne, the information broker; Kaya, the resource trader; Mann, the city's Sentinel security chief; and Orcal, the prophet whose personal link is the foundation of Xion's continued survival. The chapter dwells on Xion's slow recognition of EVE's Angel status — most citizens treat her with vocal distrust, with the Mother Sphere's intervention generations ago having actively failed to protect the surviving humans on Earth.

    The chapter introduces Lily, a survivor of an earlier airborne operation from EVE's predecessor mission to Eidos 9. Lily's reunion with EVE is staged with deliberate emotional weight, with the chapter's mid-section establishing the partnership that will anchor the campaign's middle act.

    The chapter ends with Orcal's first scan-mission committed, the path back into the Wasteland opened, and the campaign's central side-quest economy introduced through Xion's bulletin board system.

  4. 4

    4. The Wasteland Expansion — Side Quests and Combat

    The campaign's first sustained side-content-integrated chapter. Returning to the Wasteland with Orcal's intelligence on additional Naytiba threats, EVE undertakes a series of sub-missions — recovering pre-collapse data archives, hunting down rogue Naytiba variants threatening Xion's supply caravans, and gathering resources required for Orcal's expanded scanning operation.

    The chapter weaves combat with sustained environmental investigation. The Wasteland's broader geography is rendered with significantly more visual detail than the campaign's opening Eidos 7 environments, with the chapter introducing several of the campaign's most iconic enemy variants — the Barnacles (durable melee threats), the Cricket Slashers (fast ambush units), and the campaign's first sustained encounter with a Beta-tier Naytiba.

    The chapter ends with the propulsion control board recovered, the Tetrapod aerial vehicle restored to operational status, and the campaign's middle act fully committed.

  5. 5

    5. Eidos 9 — Lily's Past

    The campaign's most emotionally weighted single chapter. Eidos 9 is the city where Lily's predecessor airborne mission failed, with the chapter staged as a sustained exploration of the ruins where Lily's entire prior squad perished. The chapter is structured as a sustained character-focused sequence weaving combat against the city's remaining Naytiba forces with the franchise's most acclaimed environmental storytelling.

    The chapter dwells on Lily's character. Her quiet recognition of the spots where individual squadmates died, her measured commentary on the broader Mother Sphere mission framework that sent the squad to its deaths, and her growing distrust of the Mother Sphere command structure all anchor the chapter's emotional weight. The chapter ends with a critical data-archive recovered, the broader Mother Sphere conspiracy seeded, and the path to the campaign's third-act spectacle setting opened.

  6. 6

    6. The Great Desert — Spire 4 and Altess Levoire

    The campaign's largest sustained exploration zone. The Great Desert is rendered as the campaign's most expansive environment, with the Tetrapod aerial vehicle enabling sustained traversal across multiple sub-zones including Spire 4 (a pre-collapse research facility) and Altess Levoire (a remote ruin containing critical energy resources).

    The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-zone investigation sequence with the campaign's hardest sustained combat encounters. Each sub-zone provides distinct gameplay loops — Spire 4's interior weaves combat with sustained environmental puzzles, while Altess Levoire's exterior expanses stage the campaign's most acclaimed open-zone combat sequences.

    The chapter's central event is Orcal's energy-resource scan. The personal link's expanded operational capability, powered by the resources EVE recovers across this chapter, allows the prophet to scan the entire planet for Naytiba activity for the first time in decades. The scan reveals the Alpha Naytiba's location — and significantly more troubling, the existence of Elder Naytibas, a higher tier of organic threat the Mother Sphere has not previously acknowledged.

    The chapter ends with the Alpha Naytiba's location pinpointed, the campaign's third-act objective committed, and the path to Matrix 11 opened.

  7. 7

    7. Matrix 11 — The Pre-Collapse Truth

    The campaign's revelation chapter. Matrix 11 is a deep underground pre-collapse research facility containing the original data archives explaining how the Naytibas were created. The chapter is structured as a sustained interior exploration with significantly reduced combat density, dedicated entirely to the campaign's central narrative reveal.

    The chapter's central reveal is the origin of the Naytibas. The pre-collapse research staff, working under early Mother Sphere directives, engineered the Naytiba biology as a biological weapons program. The program escaped containment, the Naytibas evolved beyond their original design parameters, and the resulting catastrophe is the foundation of the entire campaign's present-day political situation. The Mother Sphere has spent generations actively concealing its responsibility for the apocalypse it now positions itself as the solution to.

    The chapter ends with EVE's understanding of her own role fundamentally reconfigured. Her mission — to eliminate the Alpha Naytiba — has been issued by the same Mother Sphere command structure that engineered the Naytiba threat in the first place. The campaign's central moral question — whether EVE should complete her mission or interrogate the directives that produced it — is fully active.

  8. 8

    8. The Final Confrontation — Alpha and Elder Naytiba

    The campaign's climactic combat chapter. EVE confronts the Alpha Naytiba in a sustained boss confrontation at the Naytiba's central nesting ground. The chapter is structured as a multi-phase boss fight with the campaign's most demanding sustained combat sequences, weaving the Alpha's signature attack patterns with environmental hazards specific to the boss arena.

    The chapter's mid-section pivots to the Elder Naytiba confrontation. The Elder is revealed as a significantly more powerful entity than the Alpha, with combat patterns drawing on the campaign's entire enemy roster across the runtime. The fight stages the campaign's mechanical apex, with EVE's full combat repertoire — every parry, every Burst skill, every Beta-tier ability — demanded for the encounter's resolution.

    The chapter ends with the immediate Naytiba leadership eliminated, but the campaign's broader political crisis fully escalated. The Mother Sphere's compromised legitimacy and the surviving human community's contested future are positioned for the campaign's final decision sequence.

  9. 9

    9. The Final Decision — Three Endings

    The campaign's epilogue chapter is staged as a sustained decision sequence with three possible outcomes. EVE, having confronted both the Naytiba leadership and the Mother Sphere's foundational deception, must choose between the campaign's three possible endings: the Default Ending (following Mother Sphere directives), the Doomsday Ending (rejecting the Mother Sphere but failing to provide an alternative), and the True Ending (rejecting the Mother Sphere while committing to the surviving human community's long-term protection).

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central thematic question. EVE's identity as an Angel — a Mother Sphere construct rather than a born human — is reframed across the final dialogue exchanges. Her relationships with Adam, Lily, and Xion's surviving population have given her a moral foundation independent of the Mother Sphere's command structure, and the campaign's chosen ending depends on the player's accumulated choices across the campaign's runtime.

    The credits roll over the player's chosen ending configuration, with the campaign's central question — whether engineered beings can transcend their original purposes — answered through the player's specific moral commitments across the campaign's runtime. The post-credits content (NieR: Automata and Goddess of Victory: NIKKE crossover DLCs) is explicitly seeded for the franchise's continuation.
