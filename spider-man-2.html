About the Game

Marvel's Spider-Man 2 from Insomniac Games is the PS5 sequel pairing Peter Parker and Miles Morales as dual playable heroes in an expanded Marvel's New York featuring Queens and Brooklyn. Kraven the Hunter arrives in the city hunting metahumans while Peter struggles with the alien symbiote suit. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Surge Protector — Sandman

    The campaign's opening hour is staged as the most spectacle-heavy introduction Insomniac has ever shipped. The chapter opens with Sandman — a citywide threat that has grown to the size of a Manhattan skyscraper — laying siege to Times Square. The player switches between Peter Parker and Miles Morales for the first time in the franchise, with seamless mid-traversal swaps that the campaign uses to establish both protagonists as fully co-equal leads.

    The chapter introduces the Web Wings, an aerial traversal system that elevates the franchise's already-acclaimed swinging into full open-air glide flight across the expanded map. The new map covers all of Manhattan plus the boroughs of Brooklyn and Queens, with the East River crossings handled via Web Wing thermals and rooftop launch ramps. The campaign immediately stages the expanded geography across the Sandman boss fight, which ranges from Times Square to Central Park to the East River and back.

    The chapter ends with Sandman defeated, the city's monument network catastrophically damaged, and the campaign's central thread emerging: Curt Connors — Peter's former research partner — has been spotted in Queens, and witnesses describe him as a thirty-foot reptile.

  2. 2

    2. Welcome Back — Peter and MJ

    A character-focused chapter that establishes the campaign's domestic foundation. Peter has spent the post-Miles Morales period attempting to live a normal life — he has a new apartment in Aunt May's old neighborhood, he works as a substitute biology teacher, and his relationship with Mary Jane Watson is the most stable it has been since the original campaign. The chapter dwells on the small details: the apartment furniture, the photo collection, the routine of teaching, and the quiet weight of Aunt May's absence.

    The chapter's central event is Harry Osborn's return to New York. Harry — Peter's childhood best friend, presumed dead at the end of Miles Morales — has been in private medical care in Europe under his father Norman's funding, and his recovery has been engineered by experimental medical technology Norman has been concealing from everyone. Harry's reintroduction is staged with deliberate warmth, including a sustained sequence at Brooklyn's old industrial waterfront.

    The chapter ends with Harry's invitation to Peter to join his new biotech startup — Emily-May Foundation — and the campaign's seeds of Peter's slow corruption already planted in Harry's experimental therapies.

  3. 3

    3. The Lizard Awakens — Curt Connors

    The campaign's first sustained antagonist arc. Dr. Curt Connors — Peter's former Empire State University research partner — has been working in private practice on a regenerative serum derived from reptile DNA, and the chapter's opening hour stages his transformation into the Lizard across Queens. Connors's wife and child have left him; his lab has been condemned; and his deteriorating mental state has been carefully tracked by Peter without intervention.

    The chapter is structured as a citywide manhunt across Queens and the East River industrial corridor. The Lizard's combat encounter is the campaign's most aggressive single-target boss fight to date — Connors is faster than the franchise's prior heavy bosses, climbs walls, and uses environmental destruction in ways the campaign explicitly trains the player to anticipate. The boss arena spans multiple Queens warehouse complexes.

    The chapter ends with Connors restrained, sedated, and committed to Norman Osborn's secure medical facility under the Emily-May Foundation. The capture is staged as a Peter-Harry victory, but the campaign's third-act conspiracy is now in motion behind closed doors.

  4. 4

    4. Welcome to Kraven — The Hunters Arrive

    The campaign's introduction of its central antagonist. Sergei Kravinoff — Kraven the Hunter — arrives in New York with a small army of mercenary hunters, having selected the city as his final hunting ground after decades of declining health and a terminal diagnosis. The chapter opens with a coordinated assault on a SHIELD black-site holding several of the city's most dangerous metahumans, with Kraven's forces extracting Scorpion, Tombstone, the Lizard, and several Sinister Six remnants as preferred prey.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained reactive response across Manhattan, with Spider-Man (alternating Peter and Miles) intercepting Kraven's hunting parties at multiple simultaneous locations. The Hunters introduce a new combat archetype — military-trained, equipped with anti-Spider-Man technology, and operating in coordinated teams that demand the campaign's most advanced gadget loadouts.

    The chapter ends with Kraven establishing his Manhattan command structure, multiple major villains released into the city, and Peter making contact with Black Cat — newly returned from Europe — for intelligence on Kraven's actual objective.

  5. 5

    5. The Black Suit — Harry's Therapy

    The campaign's domestic-tragedy chapter. Harry's experimental therapy — a black, fluid-like substance Norman's foundation has been administering — is revealed to Peter as the source of Harry's recovery. The chapter is structured around a sustained sequence at Harry's penthouse, with Peter helping a still-weakened Harry through a flare-up that nearly ends his life. The therapy is administered. Harry recovers. The substance is left in a containment unit Peter accidentally damages.

    The chapter ends with the symbiote bonding to Peter in his sleep. The first symbiote sequences are staged with deliberate intimacy: Peter's apartment, his bedroom, his slow waking realization that something is moving across his skin. The black suit — the franchise's most iconic alternate costume — manifests for the first time on the chapter's final morning.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the black-suit unlock mission, in which Peter, now wearing the symbiote, intercepts a Hunter assault on a Brooklyn waterfront warehouse. The new combat system — symbiote tendril attacks, surge meter generation, and the rage-management mechanics that will define Peter's mid-campaign — is fully introduced.

  6. 6

    6. Miles vs. Mister Negative

    Miles's parallel campaign chapter and one of the campaign's most acclaimed single arcs. Martin Li — Mister Negative, the man who killed Miles's father in the original campaign — has been released from prison on parole and has returned to Harlem. The chapter opens with Miles tracking Li through Brooklyn, with the player methodically uncovering Li's apparent reformation: he is working at F.E.A.S.T., he is genuinely helping his community, and his powers are visibly diminished.

    The chapter's central question is what Miles will do when he finds him. The campaign refuses easy answers. The combat encounter with Li is staged in a F.E.A.S.T. shelter at night, with Li refusing to defend himself and Miles forced to confront the reality that the man who killed his father is no longer the man who killed his father. The chapter's resolution — Miles's decision to leave Li alive, despite years of grief and rage — is one of the franchise's most emotionally mature scenes.

    The chapter ends with Miles returning home to Rio Morales, his mother, with the campaign's quietest character beat: the recognition that Miles has grown into something better than his anger.

  7. 7

    7. The Symbiote Spreads — Coney Island

    The campaign's mid-second-act spectacle chapter. Peter, increasingly affected by the symbiote, accompanies Harry on an outing to Coney Island that the campaign frames as nostalgic celebration. The chapter is structured around a sustained Hunters assault on the boardwalk, with the symbiote's full combat suite unleashed for the first time in extended open combat.

    The chapter's emotional centerpiece is the deterioration of Peter's character. The symbiote is accelerating his reactions, his power, and his rage. The chapter dwells on his treatment of MJ during the Coney Island sequence — short-tempered, dismissive, increasingly cruel — and the campaign refuses to soften the moments where Peter behaves like someone Mary Jane has every right to be afraid of.

    The chapter ends with Kraven's forces pushed back, the campaign's first major Hunters defeat, and Harry's discovery that the symbiote substance has bonded with Peter rather than remaining in containment. The implications are immediately catastrophic.

  8. 8

    8. The Hunt for Sandman — The Black Cat Heist

    A character-pivot chapter that brings Felicia Hardy back into the campaign as a sustained partner. Black Cat has returned to New York to recover the Sandman crystals — the substance Sandman's body was reduced to during the opening chapter, now scattered across the city and being collected by Kraven's forces. The chapter is structured as a stealth-heist collaboration, with Peter (in the black suit) and Felicia working through multiple parallel infiltrations across Manhattan rooftops.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's most sustained sequence of partner-coordinated takedowns, with Felicia's grapple line and Peter's symbiote tendrils combining in ways the franchise has not previously staged. The chapter dwells on the friction between Felicia's pragmatic moral framework and Peter's increasingly symbiote-driven anger.

    The chapter ends with the Sandman crystals recovered, Felicia leaving New York permanently, and Peter's slow recognition that he can no longer control the symbiote's influence on his behavior.

  9. 9

    9. The Black Suit Confrontation

    The campaign's emotional pivot. MJ — alongside Miles and a recovering Harry — stages an intervention in Peter's apartment after a violent incident at the substitute-teaching job has resulted in Peter physically assaulting a student. The chapter is structured as a sustained character confrontation, with no combat, no music, and Yuri Lowenthal's performance landing the campaign's most disturbing scene as Peter resists every attempt to remove the symbiote.

    The chapter's emotional crescendo is the church bell sequence — the franchise's canonical method of separating Peter from the symbiote, staged at a Brooklyn cathedral with Miles fighting the symbiote-controlled Peter across the bell tower. The symbiote is removed. Peter survives. The substance flees into the cathedral's understructure.

    The chapter ends with Harry, weakened by the symbiote's departure from Peter's body, returning to his Foundation containment unit — where the substance, drawn back to its original host, finds him. The campaign's third-act antagonist is born.

  10. 10

    10. Venom Awakens

    The campaign's third-act opening. Harry Osborn is now bonded to the symbiote at significantly deeper levels than Peter ever was. The chapter is structured as a citywide outbreak sequence — Harry, now calling himself Venom, has begun spreading the symbiote across Manhattan via a controlled infection vector, with the substance bonding to civilians and transforming them into symbiote-controlled drones.

    The chapter's combat highlights are the campaign's most apocalyptic. Symbiote-infected citizens swarm the streets, the East River crossings collapse under the weight of fleeing civilians, and Spider-Man (both Peter and Miles) is forced to fight through populations of people who cannot be killed but cannot be safely captured.

    The chapter ends with Harry's symbiote army established across Manhattan, the city's infrastructure collapsing, and Norman Osborn arriving on the scene with the campaign's most surprising character pivot: his recognition that his son has become a monster he himself created.

  11. 11

    11. The Symbiote Invasion

    The campaign's largest open-world sequence and one of the most ambitious single chapters Insomniac has ever staged. The entire Manhattan map is now infected with symbiote infrastructure — black tendrils growing from buildings, swarmed pedestrians, and Kraven's surviving Hunters joining the battle as a third faction. The chapter is structured as a sustained, multi-objective response across the city.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the Anti-Venom suit acquisition — the unique mid-third-act unlock that gives Peter the only weapon capable of meaningfully damaging Harry. The Anti-Venom mechanics are staged across multiple late-game encounters, with healing-while-attacking systems that redefine the franchise's combat math.

    The chapter ends with the campaign's central question fully posed: in order to save Harry, Peter and Miles will have to defeat him decisively, and the substance bonded to his body may not survive the separation.

  12. 12

    12. The Final Confrontation — Venom

    The campaign's climactic boss fight. Venom has retreated to the top of an Oscorp building in midtown, where his final transformation is reaching its apex. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage co-op battle, with Peter (in Anti-Venom suit) and Miles (in his full Venom-Strike loadout) alternating phases of the encounter.

    The fight is the largest-scale boss confrontation Insomniac has shipped. Venom uses every symbiote ability the campaign has staged at exponentially larger scale — citywide tendrils, civilian-shield manipulation, and a final transformation into a colossal symbiote leviathan that drags the rooftop arena across the Manhattan skyline. The fight ends with Peter and Miles cornering Harry, separating him from the symbiote, and the substance dispersed.

    The chapter ends with Harry comatose, the symbiote contained, and the city beginning the long recovery from the invasion.

  13. 13

    13. New Beginnings — Epilogue

    The campaign's epilogue weaves multiple character arcs into closing position. Peter retires from the Spider-Man identity — committed to caring for the comatose Harry full-time and to repairing his relationship with Mary Jane. Miles is left as New York's primary Spider-Man, a role the campaign explicitly frames as Miles's by right and by capability. Felicia, Yuri, and the returning supporting cast are positioned for the franchise's continuation.

    The chapter's post-credits scenes establish the next decade of the franchise. Mister Negative is moved to permanent isolation. Cindy Moon — a longtime supporting character — receives her own Spider-related origin in a sequence the campaign teases without resolving. And in the chapter's final shot, Norman Osborn, alone in his office, reaches for a containment vial that the audience knows contains the seed of the next campaign's central threat.
