About the Game

Until Dawn Remake is Ballistic Moon's PS5 remake of the 2015 Supermassive Games choice-based teen horror title. Eight friends gather at a remote mountain lodge one year after a tragic accident, only to face supernatural threats and a deranged killer. The walkthrough captures the full campaign on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary. The Butterfly Effect choice system means every QTE response shapes who survives Until Dawn.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    Prologue — One Year Earlier

    The campaign opens with a sustained flashback to Blackwood Pines, a mountain ski lodge owned by the wealthy Washington family. The Washington siblings — Josh and twin sisters Hannah and Beth — are hosting a winter weekend with their seven friends. The prologue dwells on the casual cruelty of teenagers: a prank engineered against Hannah, who has an obvious crush on Mike Munroe, escalates into a humiliation video that drives her from the lodge in tears. Beth, alarmed at her sister's disappearance, pursues her into the surrounding wilderness.

    The chapter's pivot is the chase across the snow. The twins, fleeing an unidentified pursuer in the dark forest, reach the edge of a cliff. The chapter ends with both Hannah and Beth falling into the gorge below, presumed dead. The campaign cuts forward by one year. The surviving seven friends have agreed — at Josh's invitation — to return to Blackwood Pines for an "anniversary getaway" to honor the missing twins. The chapter establishes the entire emotional substrate of the campaign before introducing a single core mechanic.

  2. 2

    Chapter 1 — Memento

    The chapter opens with Sam Giddings, one of the surviving seven, hiking up to the lodge at dusk. The remake stages her introduction with the new lighting engine fully foregrounded — fresh snow, dim afternoon light, and the increased facial-capture density that defines the remake's visual identity. The chapter is structured as a slow domestic reintroduction: each of the seven survivors arrives at the lodge in sequence, with the campaign's relationship axes (Mike-Jessica, Chris-Ashley, Matt-Emily) established through small interaction beats.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's signature Butterfly Effect mechanic — the player's micro-decisions across the campaign permanently lock in specific outcomes for each of the eight playable characters. The chapter ends with the group settling into the lodge for the night, with the central question of the weekend still hidden: what does Josh actually want from this reunion?

  3. 3

    Chapter 2 — Loss

    The chapter pivots into the campaign's first sustained horror elements. The power at the lodge fails. The group splits into pairs across the property to investigate. The chapter weaves between three perspectives: Mike and Jessica at a guest cabin, Chris and Ashley investigating a Ouija board in the lodge, and Sam and Josh at the lodge proper. Each pairing develops its own arc — Jessica's flirtation with Mike, Ashley's growing belief in genuine supernatural activity, and Sam's quiet observation of Josh's deteriorating mental state.

    The chapter ends with the disappearance of Jessica. Attacked at the guest cabin by an unseen pursuer, Jessica is dragged into the forest in a sustained sequence the remake stages with deliberate ambiguity. The chapter's first major player choice — Mike's pursuit of Jessica — determines whether several characters will survive the next several chapters.

  4. 4

    Chapter 3 — Loose Lips

    The chapter splits perspectives across the increasingly distressed group. Sam investigates a series of disturbing photographs found in the lodge's basement. Chris and Ashley find further evidence of someone watching them. Mike pursues Jessica into the mountain wilderness, encountering the campaign's first sustained sequence of "Don't Move" mechanics — a remake-distinctive mechanism in which the player must keep the controller still to avoid detection.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's relationship axes. Several Butterfly Effects established here lock characters into divergent ending paths. The chapter ends with Mike reaching the bottom of an elevator shaft where Jessica's bloodied body lies — though whether she is alive or dead is the chapter's first major reveal.

  5. 5

    Chapter 4 — Friendship

    The chapter pivots into the campaign's central reveal: Josh's prank. The disturbing events of the previous two chapters — the photographs, the pursuit, the deaths — are revealed as an elaborate staged psychological assault by Josh himself, attempting to traumatize the survivors who he holds responsible for his sisters' disappearance. The chapter is structured as a sustained confrontation at the lodge's saw mill, with Josh's elaborate corpse-and-pulley set-piece prepared for Ashley and Chris.

    The chapter's pivot is the realization that a genuine threat is also on the mountain. The Wendigo — the supernatural predator the lodge's deeper history is built around — has been hunting the survivors in parallel to Josh's prank. The chapter ends with Josh dragged into the mines by the actual Wendigo, his prank in shambles, and the real horror only now beginning.

  6. 6

    Chapter 5 — Darkness

    The campaign's first sustained Wendigo combat sequence. The survivors, now aware that something genuinely supernatural is hunting them, split between the lodge proper and the surrounding mountain. Chris and Ashley attempt to reach the cable car for evacuation; Mike and Sam descend into the mines in pursuit of Josh; Matt and Emily are trapped at a fire tower with the Wendigo closing in.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's deepest "Don't Move" sequences. Each character's survival depends on the player's ability to maintain stillness during Wendigo encounters — the creatures hunt by movement detection, and any controller motion will trigger immediate detection. The mechanic has become one of the franchise's signature design innovations.

    The chapter ends with the survivors scattered across the mountain, several Butterfly Effects now locking in deaths, and the campaign's tone fully pivoted from teenage drama to survival horror.

  7. 7

    Chapter 6 — Decisions

    A short, decision-dense chapter. Each surviving character faces an immediate life-or-death choice. Emily must either find shelter in a Wendigo nest or attempt to flee back to the lodge. Mike must either rescue Josh or save himself. Sam must either save Mike from a Wendigo trap or pursue her own evacuation route. The chapter's pacing accelerates significantly — the campaign's branching tree opens to its widest point here, with multiple permanent character deaths possible in a single chapter.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central thesis: that survival is not a function of heroism but of decision quality under pressure. The chapter ends with the surviving characters converging on the lodge's basement, where the campaign's central reveal about the Wendigo's origin is about to be staged.

  8. 8

    Chapter 7 — Fortitude

    The campaign's exposition chapter. The surviving characters — guided by the Stranger, an elderly man who has been on the mountain for years monitoring the Wendigo population — descend into the sanatorium beneath the lodge. The chapter is structured as a sustained exposition sequence weaving combat with documentary discovery. The Stranger reveals the Wendigo's full history: they are humans who have resorted to cannibalism in the mountain's wilderness, transformed by an ancient Native curse into the predators the chapter has been staging.

    The chapter's central reveal is Hannah Washington. The missing twin, presumed dead in the prologue, survived her fall into the gorge — and turned to cannibalism to survive, transforming into the campaign's primary Wendigo antagonist. The chapter's emotional weight lands the campaign's central tragedy: the prank that drove Hannah from the lodge a year ago has produced the predator now hunting her surviving friends.

    The chapter ends with the Stranger's death at Hannah's hand, the survivors armed with the Wendigo's vulnerabilities (fire, stillness), and the campaign's final act staged.

  9. 9

    Chapter 8 — Truth

    The campaign's most demanding combat sequence. The Wendigo population, alerted by Hannah's hunt, swarms the lodge and the surrounding mountain. Each surviving character faces sustained Wendigo encounters with the campaign's signature stillness mechanics. The chapter weaves combat across the lodge's interior — the kitchen, the basement, the upper floors — with multiple permanent character deaths possible based on the player's reactions.

    The chapter's central thread is the survivors' coordination through what remains of the lodge's communication infrastructure. Multiple Butterfly Effects from earlier chapters resolve here — characters who refused to share information are now isolated; characters who built trust are now coordinating effectively. The chapter ends with the surviving cast preparing for the final sequence at the lodge's main hall.

  10. 10

    Chapter 9 — Karma

    The campaign's climactic chapter. The Wendigo swarm reaches the lodge proper in coordinated attack. The chapter is structured as a multi-character final stand — each surviving character has their own combat sequence and survival decision, with the cumulative outcome determining the campaign's ending. The chapter's "Don't Move" sequences become the most demanding the campaign has staged, with multiple Wendigos in single rooms requiring sustained stillness from the player.

    The chapter's emotional weight lands the campaign's central question. The cumulative deaths from across the campaign — typically two to five of the eight characters — produce one of multiple ending configurations. The chapter ends with the lodge in flames, the surviving characters fleeing toward dawn, and the Wendigo population finally addressed.

  11. 11

    Chapter 10 — Dawn

    The campaign's epilogue. The surviving characters — anywhere from zero to eight of the original cast — give post-event interviews to police investigators on the mountain. The chapter is structured as a series of one-on-one interrogations, with each surviving character's testimony shaped by their specific experiences across the previous chapters. The deceased characters' testimonies are absent.

    The chapter's final moments stage the campaign's central twist for any audience unfamiliar with the franchise: the police's quiet acknowledgment that the Wendigo population on Blackwood Mountain remains active. The campaign's central question — whether to call the events a "victory" — is left explicitly open. The credits roll over the surviving cast departing the mountain, with the franchise's broader anthology directly seeded.
