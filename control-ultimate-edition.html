About the Game

Control Ultimate Edition is Remedy Entertainment's supernatural action-adventure inside The Oldest House, headquarters of the Federal Bureau of Control. Jesse Faden arrives searching for her missing brother and unwittingly becomes the Bureau's new director after the previous director's suicide. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign plus AWE and Foundation DLC across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Welcome to the Oldest House

    The campaign opens with Jesse Faden, a twenty-something woman who has spent seventeen years searching for her missing brother Dylan, arriving at the Oldest House — the headquarters of the Federal Bureau of Control in a fictionalized New York City. The opening sequence is structured as a slow-burn exploration of the building's Brutalist interior, with Jesse navigating the empty corridors, the desk of an apparently suicide-stricken director, and the Service Weapon — a shape-shifting parautilitarian firearm that is the Bureau's signature artifact.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's central conceit: the Oldest House is not a normal building. It is a Place of Power, a paranatural structure with shifting geography, telekinetic anomalies, and an extradimensional entity called the Board that communicates through reverberant whispers. The Service Weapon binds to Jesse — an act that, by Bureau tradition, makes her the new Director by inheritance. The chapter ends with Jesse formally accepting the role, her connection to Dylan's whereabouts now staged through Bureau records.

  2. 2

    2. Unknown Caller

    The campaign's first sustained combat chapter. The Hiss — a paranatural entity that has infected most of the Bureau's staff and is broadcasting a corrupting frequency throughout the Oldest House — manifests as the campaign's primary enemy faction. The chapter is structured as a sustained combat sequence in the building's Janitor's Office, with the campaign's combat fundamentals fully introduced: the Service Weapon's multiple shape-shifting forms, Jesse's growing telekinetic abilities, and the campaign's signature destructible-environment combat.

    The chapter introduces Ahti, the Oldest House's enigmatic Finnish janitor, who offers Jesse cryptic guidance in fragments of Finnish folk wisdom. Ahti's presence — and the broader Remedy Connected Universe references threaded through his dialogue — establish the campaign's metafictional foundation.

    The chapter ends with Jesse's first sustained Hiss combat encounter cleared, the Service Weapon's first form unlocked, and the Bureau's central operational mystery introduced: what has Director Trench been concealing?

  3. 3

    3. Directorial Override

    The campaign's first major dungeon. Jesse descends into the Astral Plane, an extradimensional space connected to the Oldest House through the Director's office. The chapter is structured as a sustained traversal through impossible geometries — floating platforms, vertical mazes, and the campaign's first introduction to the Astral Plane challenge maps that recur as side content.

    The chapter unlocks Jesse's Launch ability — telekinetic projection of environmental objects — which becomes the campaign's most-used combat verb. The chapter weaves combat with environmental puzzles, with the destructible Brutalist architecture providing constant ammunition for Jesse's expanded toolkit.

    The chapter ends with Jesse formally certified as the Bureau's Director by Polaris, her growing acceptance of the role increasingly visible, and the campaign's central mission — finding Dylan inside the Oldest House — fully staged.

  4. 4

    4. Threshold — Maintenance and Research

    The campaign's mid-act exploration chapter. Jesse explores the Maintenance and Research sectors of the Oldest House, each rendered with significantly different atmospheric and architectural identities. The chapter weaves combat with the campaign's most sustained environmental storytelling — the Bureau's catalog of contained paranatural artifacts (the Object of Power archive), the audio logs of researchers describing their encounters with the Astral Plane, and the recurring file references to the Altered World Events that produced the artifacts themselves.

    The chapter introduces the Polaris connection more explicitly. Polaris — the entity Jesse has carried inside her since childhood — is revealed as Jesse's connection to the Astral Plane and the source of her growing paranatural abilities. The chapter dwells on Jesse's growing comfort with the supernatural identity that has defined her since her brother's disappearance.

    The chapter ends with Jesse acquiring the Astral Plane traversal ability, the Oldest House's full map now navigable, and the central question of Dylan's location finally focused.

  5. 5

    5. My Brother's Keeper

    The campaign's emotional centerpiece. Jesse locates Dylan Faden — her missing brother — in a deep Bureau containment facility. He has been a Bureau prisoner for the past seventeen years, used as a test subject for paranatural research. The chapter dwells on the moral weight of the discovery: the Bureau Jesse has just inherited as Director has been systematically experimenting on her brother for nearly two decades.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained conversation across the containment cell's barrier, with Dylan and Jesse reconstructing the events of their childhood that led to his Bureau captivity. The conversation reveals that the Hiss that has infected the Bureau may be related to the experiments performed on Dylan, and that Jesse's connection to Polaris may be the key to resolving both crises.

    The chapter ends with Dylan possessed by the Hiss mid-conversation, the campaign's antagonist now clearly identified as Dylan's corrupted self, and Jesse's central mission reframed: saving the Bureau and saving her brother are now the same problem.

  6. 6

    6. The Face of the Enemy

    The campaign's mid-third-act combat chapter. Jesse pursues evidence of the Hiss's origin through the Oldest House's deepest containment levels, with the chapter weaving combat against increasingly transformed Hiss variants with sustained environmental exploration. The chapter introduces several of the campaign's most acclaimed Object of Power encounters, including the Refrigerator (which must be continuously observed or it begins moving toward Jesse) and the Floppy Disk (which corrupts adjacent staff in escalating patterns).

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central metafictional layer — the Bureau's research papers reference Alan Wake's Cauldron Lake, Bright Falls, and the Dark Place by name, establishing the Remedy Connected Universe as a direct continuity rather than a thematic overlap. The references seed the eventual AWE expansion's full cross-pollination.

    The chapter ends with the campaign's first sustained boss confrontation against a Hiss-corrupted Bureau commander, Jesse's full ability roster now unlocked, and the Ashtray Maze sequence positioned for the next chapter.

  7. 7

    7. The Old Boys' Club — The Ashtray Maze

    The most acclaimed single chapter in the campaign and one of the most acclaimed sequences in modern action gaming. The Ashtray Maze — a constantly reconfiguring labyrinthine corridor within the Oldest House — is staged as a sustained combat-and-traversal sequence accompanied by the Old Gods of Asgard song Take Control. The chapter is structured as a single continuous run through architecture that physically rearranges itself with every step.

    The chapter's combat highlights include every system the campaign has taught: Service Weapon form-switching, Launch chains, dash-dodging, possessed-civilian saves, and the campaign's first sustained encounter with the Former — a paranatural entity that haunts the Maze's central chambers. The chapter's pacing has become one of the most cited examples of mechanical and narrative synthesis in modern game design.

    The chapter ends with Jesse emerging from the Ashtray Maze, the path to the campaign's final mission unlocked, and the Hiss's central source — Dylan himself — now clearly the campaign's final boss.

  8. 8

    8. Polaris — The Final Confrontation

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Jesse confronts Dylan in the deepest containment level of the Oldest House. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage boss fight, with Dylan's Hiss-possessed form weaving paranatural attacks that the campaign has been carefully escalating across every prior chapter. The fight ends with Jesse's full integration of Polaris's abilities — the campaign's most spectacle-heavy single encounter — and Dylan's containment in a Bureau-managed coma.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central thematic resolution. Jesse has not "won" — the Bureau remains an institution that has experimented on her family, the Hiss remains a contained but unsolved problem, and the relationship with her brother is unresolved. The chapter ends with Jesse formally accepting the role of Director permanently and the credits rolling over a static shot of the Oldest House's exterior, with no exterior view of New York available.

  9. 9

    9. The Foundation (DLC)

    The campaign's first expansion. Marshall, the Bureau's Head of Operations, sends Jesse beneath the Oldest House into the Foundation — the paranatural underground complex on which the Oldest House was built. The chapter is structured as a sustained vertical descent into Astral Plane-adjacent geology, with new combat scenarios and several new Service Weapon forms unlocked.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's mythology. The Foundation is the original Place of Power that the Bureau's headquarters was constructed to contain. The expansion's exposition deepens the campaign's relationship to Polaris, the Astral Plane, and the broader paranatural cosmology.

    The chapter ends with the Foundation stabilized, a new Astral Plane breach contained, and the expansion's mid-campaign cliffhanger pointing toward AWE.

  10. 10

    10. AWE (DLC)

    The campaign's second expansion and the full Remedy Connected Universe pivot. The Bureau's Investigations Sector opens, revealing files on the Bright Falls AWE — the Altered World Event that produced the events of Alan Wake. The chapter is structured as a sustained investigation of Bureau files crossing directly into Alan Wake's continuity, with Wake himself appearing in voice-over and recorded video segments.

    The chapter's central confrontation is the Hartman boss fight — Doctor Emil Hartman, the antagonist of Alan Wake's original DLC, manifesting in the Oldest House through the Hiss's connection to Cauldron Lake. The fight is the expansion's mechanical apex.

    The chapter ends with the expansion's mid-credits scene setting up Alan Wake II in explicit terms: Alan Wake is alive, trapped in the Dark Place, writing the events of both Control and his own escape simultaneously. The Remedy Connected Universe is now formally a single continuity.
