About the Game

The Last of Us Part II Remastered is Naughty Dog's PS5 remaster of the 2020 sequel, adding the No Return roguelite mode, Lost Levels, and rebuilt rendering pipeline. The story follows Ellie pursuing revenge across post-apocalyptic Seattle, with the controversial mid-game switch to antagonist Abby's perspective. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across three parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. The Farm — Jackson

    The campaign opens in Jackson, Wyoming, four years after the events of Part I. Ellie, now nineteen, lives on a working ranch outside the town walls alongside her surrogate-uncle Joel and his brother Tommy. The opening sequence is built as a slow domestic immersion in a community that has spent four years stabilizing into something genuinely functional — schools, livestock, electricity, a militia, and a fragile peace with the surrounding wilderness. Ellie is no longer a child, has a girlfriend in Dina, has friends in Jesse and Jesse's pregnant ex Cat, and lives in a converted garage on Joel's property that the campaign dwells on with deliberate intimacy.

    The chapter's central event is the Patrol — Joel and his patrol partner Tommy ride out on a routine sweep of the territory north of Jackson, intercepting walkers and clearing infected nests as part of the town's standard security rotation. The patrol is staged with no music, in falling snow, and the chapter pacing deliberately mirrors the calm of the opening domestic sequences.

    The chapter ends with Joel and Tommy taking shelter from a sudden infected horde in an abandoned ski lodge on the edge of town. Inside, they find a wounded young woman named Abby and her group of WLF survivors. Joel offers help. Joel introduces himself by name. The chapter cuts to black.

  2. 2

    2. The Death of Joel

    The campaign's pivot. Abby and her group — Owen, Manny, Mel, Nora, Jordan, and Leah — recognize Joel by name immediately. The rest of the chapter is staged as a sustained, unbroken sequence in the ski lodge basement, with Joel restrained, Tommy beaten, and Ellie — who has been tracking them through the storm — arriving moments too late. Abby executes Joel with a golf club, in front of Ellie, in a sequence the campaign refuses to soften.

    The chapter's emotional staging refuses every convention the franchise's first installment built. There is no last conversation, no thematic closure, no music cue. The chapter ends with Tommy and Ellie left alive in the lodge — alive specifically so they can carry the message back to whoever else loved Joel — and Abby's group disappearing into the storm.

    The chapter cuts forward to Jackson in the days following. Joel's funeral. The town's reckoning. Ellie's decision, made over Maria's objection, to ride west to Seattle in pursuit of Abby's WLF faction. The campaign's central engine is now committed.

  3. 3

    3. The Pursuit Begins — Seattle Day 1 (Ellie)

    Ellie and Dina arrive in Seattle, Washington, the post-collapse home of the Washington Liberation Front (WLF) — a paramilitary survivor faction that controls the city's central districts from a converted football stadium. The chapter is structured as an open-zone investigation through downtown Seattle, with the WLF's territorial markers, patrol patterns, and surveillance infrastructure rendered as one of the campaign's most dense urban environments.

    The chapter introduces the infected variations that define Part II's ecosystem — the Shamblers, the new Stalkers, and the Bloater hunting parties that the original game never staged at this density. The combat encounters dwell in the slow stealth fundamentals the franchise has refined across two games, but the environments are larger, the patrol routes longer, and the cost of detection significantly higher.

    The chapter's central thread is the methodical pursuit of Abby's group through the WLF's identification systems. Ellie and Dina hunt for names, for affiliations, for the operational base where Abby is believed to be stationed. The chapter dwells in the cold-procedural mechanics of revenge — every interrogation, every body, every choice — and the campaign refuses to let the player feel righteous about any of it.

  4. 4

    4. Hostile Territory — Hillcrest

    A mid-Day-1 chapter set in the Hillcrest residential neighborhood north of downtown Seattle. The biome is rendered as an overgrown suburb, with single-family homes consumed by ivy, abandoned cars, and a WLF patrol presence that suggests Hillcrest is being actively contested. The chapter's exploration loop opens optional house infiltrations, scavenger encounters, and one of the campaign's most acclaimed quiet sequences — a music store where Ellie plays Joel's guitar.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's first sustained WLF dog handlers. The dogs introduce a new sensory tracking mechanic — they follow scent trails on the ground, forcing the player to vary their pathing in ways the franchise's prior combat designs did not demand. The dogs also force the campaign's first genuinely uncomfortable choice: the player must kill an animal that has done nothing wrong, in order for Ellie to continue.

    The chapter ends with Ellie and Dina recovering critical intelligence on Abby's location and the WLF's intelligence-gathering aquarium base.

  5. 5

    5. The Aquarium — Owen

    The transition between Days 1 and 2, structured as an extended infiltration of the Seattle Aquarium, a derelict pre-collapse facility that the WLF has repurposed as a quiet intelligence-station for its leadership. The chapter is staged in low light, with bioluminescent water tanks providing most of the illumination, and the design weaves stealth fundamentals through tight, claustrophobic corridors.

    The chapter's central confrontation is with Owen Moore, one of Abby's group from the lodge. Owen is the campaign's first sustained antagonist who is rendered as a recognizably decent person — he is visibly grieving, visibly conflicted about Joel's killing, and visibly in love with both Abby and his partner Mel. The chapter's encounter with Owen ends in his death at Ellie's hand, with the killing staged in extended close-up and the immediate, terrible discovery that Mel was pregnant.

    The chapter ends with Ellie and Dina escaping the aquarium with the location of Abby's base, the campaign's first sustained moral question landing in full force.

  6. 6

    6. Seattle Day 2 (Ellie) — Tracking Nora

    Ellie's second day in Seattle is the campaign's longest single combat day, structured as a methodical hunt through a series of WLF-controlled districts in pursuit of Nora Harris, the medical lieutenant who was at Joel's killing and one of Ellie's last remaining named targets. The chapter's exploration loop opens Seattle Hospital, one of the campaign's most acclaimed level designs — a multi-floor environment that mixes WLF patrols with the campaign's most extensive infected encounter.

    The chapter's central encounter is the basement chase. Nora flees into the hospital's flooded lower floors, drawing Ellie through an infected nest the WLF has been actively avoiding. The chase ends with Nora exposed to spores, dying of the resulting fungal infection — and Ellie torturing the dying woman for information about Abby's location. The chapter refuses to make the torture sequence palatable. The campaign explicitly stages it as the moral point of no return.

    The chapter ends with Ellie returning to the theater base she and Dina have made their forward operations point, the first Nightmare PTSD sequence forcing the player to experience what Ellie has been numbing herself through, and the campaign's central question — what does revenge cost — fully open.

  7. 7

    7. The Theater — Day 2 End

    A relatively short chapter staged inside the abandoned Capitol Theater in downtown Seattle, where Ellie, Dina, Jesse (recently arrived from Jackson), and Tommy reunite for one night between the campaign's hunting days. The chapter is structured as a series of quiet rooftop and backstage conversations: Ellie and Dina argue about the pregnancy; Ellie and Tommy argue about Abby; Jesse and Ellie reckon with how much of each of them remains.

    The chapter dwells on what the campaign has been refusing to acknowledge: that Ellie's revenge mission has put Dina's pregnancy at lethal risk, that Tommy is operating on his own private version of the same revenge mission with significantly less restraint, and that Jesse is here because someone has to bring the survivors home. The campaign's first quiet, character-only chapter in a long stretch lands with substantial emotional weight.

    The chapter ends with the group's decision to make one final raid the following day, with the goal of locating Abby herself and ending the mission once and for all.

  8. 8

    8. Ground Zero — Seattle Day 3 (Ellie)

    The campaign's most demanding combat day. Ellie's third day in Seattle is structured around the assault on the WLF stadium, a fortified complex that serves as the WLF faction's military and civilian center. The chapter weaves sustained combat against military-grade WLF forces with the campaign's first prolonged engagement with the WLF's heaviest infected response teams.

    The chapter's central confrontation is the death of Jesse. Returning from a side mission with Tommy, Jesse is shot through the head by a WLF sniper in a sequence staged with no warning, no buildup, and no music. The killing is one of the campaign's most acclaimed staging decisions — it removes a character the campaign has carefully built, in a moment where the player has been trained to expect tension rather than tragedy.

    The chapter ends with Ellie's first direct confrontation with Abby herself in the theater basement. The fight is brutal, ends with Ellie about to deliver a killing blow, and is interrupted by the campaign's most controversial structural decision — the perspective shifts.

  9. 9

    9. Seattle Day 1 (Abby) — The Stadium

    The campaign's perspective inversion. The same three days the player has just spent hunting Abby are now staged again, from her side, beginning with her arrival back at the WLF stadium the morning after Joel's killing in the ski lodge. The campaign asks the player to spend the next ten hours playing the woman who killed Joel, and to do so with the same camera angles, the same combat depth, and the same emotional intimacy the original days afforded Ellie.

    The chapter opens with Abby's domestic life at the stadium — her relationships with Owen, Mel, Manny, Jordan, and the elderly WLF medic Isaac Dixon. The stadium is rendered as a functional community with significant moral texture: schools, civilian neighborhoods, mess halls, and the obvious investment of years of effort into making a place worth defending. The campaign forces the player to recognize that the people Ellie has been killing across the previous chapters had names, families, and unconditional belonging to one another.

    The chapter ends with Abby leaving the stadium on a routine mission and discovering that her WLF patrol has been ambushed by the Seraphites — the rival religious faction the campaign has only glimpsed in the Ellie chapters.

  10. 10

    10. The Forest — Yara and Lev

    Abby's mid-game arc and the campaign's most thematically reframing chapter. While conducting a sniper rescue operation on the WLF's eastern frontier, Abby intervenes in the public execution of two Seraphite children — Yara, a teenage girl, and Lev, her younger brother, who has been condemned for cutting his own hair and rejecting the Seraphite faith's gender norms. Abby's rescue is staged with no calculation: she does it because the killings are wrong. The chapter pivots Abby from antagonist to protagonist in a single sustained sequence.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's first sustained Seraphite engagements. The Seraphites are mechanically distinct from the WLF — silent communication via whistles, bow-and-arrow primary armament, and a religious commitment to the cause that the chapter renders without satire. The chapter is the franchise's most empathetic portrait of an antagonist faction.

    The chapter ends with Yara critically wounded by a Seraphite arrow, Lev permanently bonded to Abby, and Abby's WLF loyalties beginning to fracture as her commanders demand the children's surrender.

  11. 11

    11. The Coast — Seraphite Island

    Abby's Seattle Day 3, staged as a sustained infiltration of the Seraphite island stronghold off the Seattle coast. The chapter's exploration loop is the campaign's most architecturally ambitious — a religious commune built into the island's cliff face, with shrine complexes, cult-staged civilian housing, and the campaign's most distinctive Seraphite combat encounters.

    The chapter's emotional centerpiece is Yara's amputation surgery. Performed by Abby in a candlelit warehouse with Lev watching, the sequence stages the chapter's central thematic argument: that grace under impossible circumstance is what humanity actually consists of, and that the campaign's antagonist has been carrying as much of it as its protagonist. The chapter ends with Yara walking again, the Seraphite leader Emily publicly turning on Lev, and Abby committing to leading her new family out of Seattle entirely.

    The chapter closes with the WLF's massed assault on the Seraphite island — Isaac Dixon's long-planned final solution — and Abby caught between her faction's military structure and the family she has assembled. Her decision to defect from the WLF closes the campaign's second day.

  12. 12

    12. The Confrontation — Seattle Day 3 (Abby)

    The campaign's structural climax. Abby, Yara, and Lev attempt to escape Seattle entirely, only to find the WLF has burned every passage out of the city. The chapter is structured as a desperate retreat through the Hillcrest neighborhood — the same biome Ellie hunted through on her first day in the city — with the perspective inverted: the player now flees the very ambushes Ellie set.

    The chapter's emotional crescendo is the death of Yara. Cut down by an Isaac Dixon sniper team in the same alley Jesse was shot in earlier in the campaign, Yara dies in Abby's arms as the camera holds on Lev's grief. The chapter is one of the campaign's most studied character beats — it reframes the campaign's central question of grief one final time.

    The chapter ends with Abby's return to the theater for a final confrontation: the same fight the campaign cut away from in Ellie's Day 3. This time the player is on the other side of it. Abby spares Ellie's life in the final moments, on Lev's insistence, and the campaign's two protagonists part with the war between them unfinished but interrupted.

  13. 13

    13. The Farm — Months Later

    The campaign's epilogue begins with Ellie, Dina, and their adopted child JJ living on a working farm in the Wyoming hill country. The chapter is staged with no infected, no combat, and no music for nearly its entire run — instead, the campaign dwells in the slow-rebuilding life Ellie has tried to construct in the aftermath of Seattle.

    The chapter's central event is Tommy's arrival at the farm with news that Abby has been seen in Santa Barbara, California. Tommy's request that Ellie finish what they started in Seattle is delivered as a fully realized portrait of how revenge metastasizes — Tommy is now bitter, lame from his Seattle wounds, separated from Maria, and the campaign refuses to romanticize his obsession. The chapter ends with Ellie, against Dina's protest, choosing to leave the farm and pursue Abby to the coast.

  14. 14

    14. Santa Barbara

    The campaign's final hunting chapter. Ellie arrives in Santa Barbara, California, in pursuit of the Rattlers — a coastal slaver faction that has taken Abby and Lev captive on the coastline. The chapter is structured as the campaign's most physically punishing infiltration: a fortified slaver compound on the beach, with multiple combat encounters against new enemy classes and a brutal interrogation sequence.

    The chapter's mid-section is Ellie's discovery of Abby, broken and starving on the Rattlers' execution pillar. Lev is unconscious beside her. The campaign forces the player to free both of them — and then to make Ellie pursue Abby down the beach for the final confrontation. The pursuit is staged in shallow surf, in late-afternoon sun, with no music and no dialogue.

    The chapter closes with the beach fight. Ellie and Abby, both exhausted, fight to a standstill in the surf. Ellie has the chance to kill Abby. In a sequence staged through a Joel-memory flashback, Ellie chooses to let her go. Abby and Lev disappear toward the boat. The cycle is, finally, refused.

  15. 15

    15. The Farm — Epilogue

    The campaign's final chapter is a return to the Wyoming farm. The chapter is structured as a single sustained walk through the empty house. Dina is gone. JJ is gone. The dishes are still in the sink. The chapter ends with Ellie picking up Joel's guitar — the same guitar Joel gave her at the campaign's domestic opening — and discovering that the partial fingertip she lost in the beach fight has cost her the ability to play it. She places the guitar on Joel's bed and leaves.

    The campaign's final image is Ellie walking down the farm road, alone, with everything she has spent two games building lost in the pursuit of a revenge she eventually chose not to take. The credits roll over the original cover of the franchise's signature score. The story refuses comfort, refuses redemption, and refuses to tell the audience what happens next.
