About the Game

Marvel's Spider-Man Remastered is the PS5 enhanced edition of the 2018 original, with rebuilt facial capture, improved lighting, and ray-traced reflections across Manhattan. Peter Parker has been Spider-Man for eight years and balances superheroics with his day job at Otto Octavius's lab. The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    The Fall of Fisk

    The campaign's opening hour is structured as a tutorial wrapped in one of the franchise's most confident introductions. Peter Parker — now twenty-three, eight years into his career as Spider-Man, and in the middle of an eighteen-month investigation — finally has actionable intel on crime lord Wilson Fisk's penthouse fortress at the top of Fisk Tower. The chapter opens with Spider-Man's iconic swing across the Financial District, accompanied by the John Paesano score, and immediately establishes the traversal system as one of the most physically convincing in any superhero game.

    The Fisk Tower infiltration introduces every major combat verb in sequence: melee fundamentals, web-shooter combinations, the perfect-dodge Spider-Sense system, perch takedowns, stealth web-strikes from environmental hooks, and the gadget loadout. The level design escalates floor by floor, ending in a wrecked penthouse where Fisk himself charges Spider-Man in a multi-phase brawl. The Kingpin fight is mechanically simple but thematically loaded — Fisk is positioned as a man whose only response to anything is brute force, and the fight is staged around dodging his linear charges while clearing reinforcement waves.

    The chapter closes with Fisk in custody, the NYPD on the rooftop, and Peter's voice-over warning that whatever was holding the city's underworld in check is now gone. The first Inner Demons are sighted in the closing montage, planting the antagonist seed that will dominate the rest of Act 1.

  2. 2

    The Inner Demons Rise

    The early hours of Act 1 open the city map fully and introduce the Inner Demons as the campaign's first proper enemy faction. The Demons are masked, wear ornate red-and-black armor, and wield neural-corrupted weapons that mark them as something fundamentally more dangerous than the gang remnants of Fisk's empire. Their attacks on former Fisk holdings — clearing penthouses, executing officers, taking territory — establish the chapter's tone of escalation.

    The chapter's mid-section is dedicated to the Yuri Watanabe partnership, the player's introduction to Captain Yuri at the 84th Precinct. Yuri's tutorial mission at a captured police station weaves stealth fundamentals into the open-world structure: perch takedowns from above, environmental web-strikes, and the alert-cascade mechanic that punishes loud play. The Yuri partnership becomes the campaign's primary law-enforcement thread.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's first major boss encounter outside the main story — the Shocker stakeout. Shocker's shield-shockwave pattern and the debris-throwing counter establish the design template that the campaign's later bosses will riff on. The chapter closes with the first Demon Bases unlocking on the open-world map, opening combat-arena side content for the rest of the game.

  3. 3

    The Daily Bugle

    A character-focused chapter that brings Mary Jane Watson back into the campaign's main thread. MJ, now a senior investigative journalist at the Daily Bugle, has been tracking the same Inner Demons crisis from a journalistic angle, and her return positions her as the campaign's secondary playable lead. Her introduction is staged across a Bugle newsroom sequence, a rooftop conversation with Peter, and her first stealth mission infiltrating a Demon storage facility.

    The MJ stealth missions are the campaign's most divisive design choice and one of its most thematically intentional. MJ has no combat capabilities, no super powers, and no Spider-Sense — what she has instead is a smartphone camera that marks enemies, a stash of distractions (bottles, pebbles), and a network of cover and patrol patterns to thread through. The first MJ sequence is staged inside an abandoned Demon warehouse, and the failure-restart pacing forces the player to learn the patrol logic carefully.

    The chapter ends with MJ recovering the first concrete intelligence on Mister Negative — a name, a corrupted-energy signature, and a connection to F.E.A.S.T. The thread is left dangling for Act 2 to pick up.

  4. 4

    Octavius Industries

    The chapter that anchors the campaign's emotional center. Peter's day job — working as a research assistant for Dr. Otto Octavius at Octavius Industries — is introduced in full, with the prosthetic-arm puzzle that doubles as the campaign's first lab minigame. The neural-prosthetic project that Otto and Peter are working on is positioned as an act of genuine scientific idealism — Otto wants to help amputees regain function, and the project is genuinely revolutionary.

    The chapter's central conflict is Mayor Norman Osborn's political pressure on Otto. Osborn, Otto's former research partner, has just defunded the entire project, and his on-camera explanation — broadcast in news clips throughout Manhattan — frames Otto as a failed scientist who could not deliver results. Otto's quiet response, staged in the lab's cracked-window office, is one of the campaign's most carefully written scenes.

    The chapter introduces the circuit puzzle and molecule synthesis minigames that will recur throughout the campaign's collectibles and research stations. Peter's growing trust in Otto, his sense of obligation, and his gradual realization that Otto is starting to break — all are seeded here. The chapter ends with Peter walking out of the lab and back into his Spider-Man life, with the campaign's tragedy already underway behind him.

  5. 5

    The First Negative Encounter

    The campaign's first direct confrontation with Mister Negative. The chapter is staged across a multi-block Demon assault in midtown, with corrupted-energy attacks tearing through Times Square and Spider-Man arriving to find a fully transformed Negative leading the assault personally. The level design escalates from open-world traversal to a closed-arena boss fight against Negative at the chapter's midpoint.

    The first Mister Negative encounter is mechanically designed to be unwinnable — Negative teleports, throws corrupted-energy waves, summons Demon reinforcements faster than Spider-Man can clear them, and ultimately escapes after a punishing exchange. The fight is staged to teach the player that Negative is not a normal villain. The player is expected to lose ground throughout, and the cutscene resolution explicitly frames Spider-Man as outmatched.

    The chapter's closing minutes pivot to the Black Cat stakeouts — Felicia Hardy's calling cards begin appearing on rooftops, planting the seeds of the post-game DLC arc. The chapter ends with Yuri Watanabe demanding Spider-Man take Negative seriously, and Peter walking out of the precinct with Mister Negative now on his personal hunt list.

  6. 6

    F.E.A.S.T. and Miles Morales

    A narrative-focused chapter that introduces two of the campaign's most important supporting characters. The first is Martin Li, the philanthropist who founded the F.E.A.S.T. homeless shelter where Aunt May volunteers — the player has already encountered Mister Negative, and the chapter dwells in the dramatic-irony space of knowing that the soft-spoken Li is the man Spider-Man has been hunting. The shelter scenes are staged with warmth, including a quiet conversation between Peter and May about whether she should retire from active volunteer work.

    The chapter's second introduction is Miles Morales, the teenager whose family Aunt May has been mentoring through F.E.A.S.T.'s programs. Miles's father, Officer Jefferson Davis, is introduced as a working-class NYPD cop with deep loyalty to the shelter and to Mayor Osborn's reformist policies. The Davis family is established as decent, working-class, and quietly important. The chapter does not yet kill any of them.

    A side mission inside the chapter has Spider-Man tracking down a warehouse fire that turns out to be a Demon attack on a F.E.A.S.T. supply depot. The fight is the chapter's combat centerpiece. The chapter closes with Peter introducing himself to Miles at the shelter as Aunt May's "friend Peter," and the audience knowing what Miles will eventually become.

  7. 7

    Marked Man

    The campaign's first major narrative pivot. Mister Negative launches a public, coordinated attack on a political event hosted by Mayor Norman Osborn. The chapter's central set-piece is staged across the rooftops surrounding the event and on the public stage itself. Mister Negative's grievance is finally articulated in full — a years-old Oscorp experiment ruined Li's family, and Li blames Osborn personally. The attack is intended to kill Osborn on live television.

    The chapter's centerpiece is the second Mister Negative duel, fought on an open-air rooftop against the falling debris of the event. Negative is faster, his corrupted-energy walls cut through the arena, and his charged attacks demand perfect-dodge timing the campaign has only just begun to teach. The fight ends mid-sequence as Negative breaks off to commit the chapter's defining act.

    That act is the death of Officer Jefferson Davis. Davis is killed protecting Osborn during the public attack, in a sequence staged with deliberate weight and no music. The death is the campaign's emotional pivot — it transforms Miles Morales's family from background detail into the campaign's central tragedy, and it commits the player to whatever the next act delivers. The chapter ends with Aunt May breaking the news to Miles's mother Rio at F.E.A.S.T., and the city in mourning.

  8. 8

    Devil's Breath Unleashed

    The chapter that opens Act 3. Mister Negative, his political assassination foiled, pivots to the larger plan he has been preparing the entire campaign: the release of Devil's Breath, an Oscorp-engineered bioweapon, into Manhattan's water supply. The chapter is structured as a citywide crisis sequence, with Spider-Man racing across districts trying to contain the initial release while the death toll climbs in news broadcasts.

    The chapter intercuts Peter's Spider-Man work with Otto Octavius's parallel descent. Otto, devastated by Osborn's betrayal and the failure of his life's work, has been quietly building four mechanical tentacles in his off-campus workshop. The tentacle prototypes are revealed in a sequence staged in a half-lit garage, with Otto's voice still soft, still measured, still recognizably Peter's mentor — and clearly preparing to do something terrible.

    The chapter introduces Aunt May's infection. Working at F.E.A.S.T. during the initial release, May is one of thousands exposed before the public quarantine. Her diagnosis is delivered in a hospital scene that the campaign will pay off explicitly in its final hour. The chapter closes with the city under martial law, Mister Negative still at large, and Otto's transformation complete.

  9. 9

    The Raft Breakout

    The campaign's most acclaimed mid-game sequence. Doctor Octopus — Otto Octavius now fully transformed — breaks into The Raft, the maximum-security island prison off the coast of Manhattan, and releases the Sinister Six: Electro, Vulture, Rhino, Scorpion, and Mister Negative. The chapter is structured as a continuous descent through the Raft's containment levels, with each Sinister Six member introduced via their breakout cinematic.

    The chapter's defining moment is Spider-Man's first encounter with Doctor Octopus. The fight is mechanically scripted — Doc Ock has total environmental control, can throw concrete pillars at Spider-Man, and is functionally invulnerable to direct attack. The player is forced to lose. The cinematic that follows the staged defeat — Otto's "I've known all along" identity reveal — is one of the franchise's defining moments. The reveal recontextualizes the entire campaign: every mentor scene, every lab session, every quiet conversation has been with a man who has been planning this.

    The chapter closes with the Sinister Six scattering across Manhattan, the city's largest threat in decades unleashed, and Spider-Man broken on the Raft floor with no clear plan for what comes next.

  10. 10

    The Sinister Six Hunt

    The campaign's mid-third-act open hunt. Spider-Man recovers, returns to Manhattan, and begins hunting the Sinister Six members across the city. The chapter is structured as a hub mission with multiple paths — the player can engage the villains in any order, though the campaign nudges toward a specific sequence.

    The chapter's two major boss encounters are the tag-team fights with Electro and Vulture and with Rhino and Scorpion. The Electro/Vulture duel is staged in Times Square at night, with Vulture using the city's vertical space and Electro chaining lightning across the ground. The Rhino/Scorpion fight takes place in the Financial District, with Rhino's charge attacks forcing the player to learn environmental staggers and Scorpion's hallucinogenic venom distorting the screen with each hit. Both fights are designed to feel like genuine super-villain confrontations rather than reskinned grunt encounters.

    The chapter closes with four of the six Sinister Six members in custody, Negative and Doc Ock still at large, and Aunt May's condition worsening at the hospital. The campaign begins its march toward the finale.

  11. 11

    The Heart of the Matter

    The final Mister Negative encounter. The chapter opens with Spider-Man tracking Negative through a citywide helicopter chase, with corrupted-energy attacks raining from the helicopter and the player pursuing in pure swing-traversal mode. The chase ends on a rooftop arena, where Negative makes his final stand in a three-phase boss fight.

    The fight is staged across a vertical arena, with each phase forcing the player to relearn Negative's combo strings. Phase 1 is the corrupted-energy moveset the campaign has been teaching since Act 1; phase 2 accelerates everything; phase 3 transforms Negative into a full Inner Demon form, with full-screen AOE attacks that punish anything but perfect-dodge mastery. The fight is the campaign's most pure mechanical exam outside the final battle.

    The chapter closes with Negative defeated and Martin Li, finally stripped of his powers, broken on the rooftop. The man Aunt May still believes is a hero is taken into custody, and Peter — for the first time in the campaign — fully understands what the death of Officer Davis cost, and what kind of cost the campaign is still going to demand of him.

  12. 12

    The Oscorp Tower Battle

    The campaign's climactic confrontation. Doctor Octopus has barricaded himself at the top of Oscorp Tower with the only doses of antiserum to the Devil's Breath pathogen — including the one that could save Aunt May. The chapter is structured as a vertical assault: Spider-Man fights his way up the tower against Doc Ock's tentacle traps and Sinister Six remnants, then engages Otto himself in the campaign's signature multi-phase boss fight.

    The Doc Ock final battle is one of the most acclaimed boss encounters of the past console generation. Stage 1 is staged on the upper terrace with Otto on the ground, his tentacles slamming the arena and creating windows for Spider-Man to web-strike each tentacle when it embeds. Stage 2 transitions into a vertical tower-climb sequence, with Spider-Man wall-running upward as Otto throws debris and tentacles from below. Stage 3 returns to the rooftop with Otto fully unleashed, all four tentacles attacking simultaneously, and the campaign's hardest combo strings forcing perfect-dodge sequences and gadget management.

    The chapter's emotional payoff is Otto's defeat. Stripped of his tentacle harness, broken on the rooftop, the man who taught Peter is finally just a man — and a man Peter loves. Otto's last words to Peter, staged in a quiet exchange before the police arrive, complete the campaign's most heartbreaking arc.

  13. 13

    The Sacrifice

    The campaign's epilogue and its emotional climax. Spider-Man returns to the hospital with the recovered antiserum, only to discover that there is exactly one dose left — and that dozens of patients, including Aunt May, are dying citywide. Aunt May, fully aware of the math, chooses to give the dose to the city rather than to herself. The chapter is structured as a single extended dialogue between Peter and May at her bedside.

    The conversation reveals that May has always known Peter is Spider-Man. She tells him she has been proud of him. She gives him permission to let her go. The scene is staged with no music, in long takes, with both performances — Yuri Lowenthal's Peter and Nancy Linari's May — landing among the most acclaimed in any PlayStation game.

    The chapter closes with the campaign's epilogue. Peter and Mary Jane reconcile on a Manhattan rooftop. The city begins to recover. And in Peter's apartment, in the final scene before the credits, Miles Morales reveals that he, too, was bitten — and that he, too, now has powers. The line "I think it's time we had a talk" sets up the next decade of the franchise.
