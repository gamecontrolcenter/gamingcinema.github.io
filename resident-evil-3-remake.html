About the Game

Resident Evil 3 Remake follows Jill Valentine, ex-S.T.A.R.S. operative trapped in Raccoon City as the T-Virus outbreak escalates. Throughout the campaign she is hunted by Nemesis, an Umbrella-engineered bio-weapon programmed to eliminate all S.T.A.R.S. members. The action skews more toward combat than RE2's slower horror pacing. This walkthrough is a complete cinematic playthrough on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Jill's Nightmare — The Apartment

    The campaign opens with Jill Valentine — a STARS officer who has spent the months following the Spencer Mansion incident attempting to expose Umbrella's bioweapon program to the public — alone in her Raccoon City apartment on the night of September 28, 1998. The opening sequence is structured as a sustained psychological-horror introduction, with Jill suffering from PTSD-induced nightmares about her STARS team's losses.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's tonal commitment. Where Resident Evil 2 opens with a slow-burn outbreak introduction, Resident Evil 3 opens with the outbreak already in full collapse. The chapter's pivot is the Nemesis attack — the apartment door explodes inward as the Tyrant-class bioweapon Nemesis-T Type crashes through the wall, having been deployed specifically to hunt down the surviving STARS officers.

    The chapter's escape sequence is the franchise's most demanding sustained pursuit, with Jill fleeing through the apartment building's collapsing infrastructure as Nemesis tears through walls behind her. The chapter ends with Jill on the streets of Raccoon City, Nemesis temporarily separated, and the campaign's central pursuit relationship fully established.

  2. 2

    2. The Streets — Raccoon City in Collapse

    The campaign's first sustained street-level chapter. Jill navigates downtown Raccoon City's burning streets, with the chapter's exploration loop weaving combat against zombie hordes and the franchise's signature Drain Deimos parasites with sustained environmental investigation of the city's destruction. The chapter dwells on the campaign's central distinction from Resident Evil 2 — the city is significantly more advanced in its collapse, with U.S. military intervention visible across multiple sequences.

    The chapter introduces Brad Vickers — Jill's STARS colleague — in a brief but emotionally weighted reunion sequence that ends with Brad's death at Nemesis's hands. The chapter establishes the campaign's central question: how many of the surviving STARS officers will Nemesis eliminate before the campaign concludes.

    The chapter ends with Jill reaching the subway station, the planned escape route from the city, and the introduction of the Umbrella Biohazard Countermeasure Service (UBCS) — Umbrella's private military force, deployed to extract its corporate assets ahead of the city's military sterilization.

  3. 3

    3. The Subway — Train Derailment

    The campaign's first sustained interior chapter. The Raccoon City subway station has been converted into the UBCS's primary forward operating position, with mercenaries treating the dying civilian population with significantly more brutality than the city's law enforcement ever did. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration of the subway's multiple levels, with the campaign's first sustained encounter with Carlos Oliveira, a Hispanic-Brazilian UBCS sergeant who emerges as the campaign's secondary playable protagonist.

    The chapter dwells on Jill and Carlos's character dynamic. Carlos is positioned as the franchise's most morally ambiguous supporting protagonist — UBCS-employed but personally horrified by Umbrella's operational protocols. The chapter introduces Mikhail Victor, Carlos's commanding officer, and Nicholai Ginovaef, a UBCS lieutenant whose loyalties are deliberately ambiguous from his introduction.

    The chapter's climactic event is the subway train derailment. Nemesis attacks the train mid-departure, killing several civilians and crippling Mikhail's mobility. The chapter ends with the train wrecked, Mikhail mortally wounded, and the campaign's escape plan permanently compromised.

  4. 4

    4. Downtown — Toy Store and Restaurants

    The campaign's most acclaimed street-level chapter. Jill explores Raccoon City's downtown district in pursuit of an alternative escape route. The chapter is structured as a sustained open-area exploration sequence with multiple side-zone investigations: the Toy Uncle toy store, the Charlie Doll repair shop, and the Donut Shop that serves as the chapter's central save point.

    The chapter weaves combat with sustained environmental investigation. The campaign's signature mid-section introduces several new enemy variants, including the Pale Heads — slow but extremely difficult-to-kill zombies that the chapter stages in claustrophobic interior environments — and the Hunter Gamma amphibian bioweapons in the chapter's sewer-adjacent sub-zones.

    The chapter ends with Jill recovering an emergency electrical schematic for the city's power grid, the path to the Raccoon Police Department unlocked, and the campaign's mid-act setup pivoting toward the building Resident Evil 2 spent its entire runtime exploring.

  5. 5

    5. The Sewers — Below the Streets

    The campaign's mid-game underground exploration. The chapter is structured as a sustained sewer-level survival sequence, with the campaign's hardest sustained encounter against Hunter Gamma bioweapons. The chapter weaves combat with the franchise's signature environmental puzzles, including a key-card retrieval sequence inside a flooded chamber.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's most demanding sustained Nemesis encounter — the first sequence in which Nemesis is openly invulnerable to standard firearms and must be evaded rather than engaged. The mechanic redefines the campaign's combat math.

    The chapter ends with the sewers traversed, the city's emergency power infrastructure accessed, and the path to the Raccoon Police Department's exterior approach opened.

  6. 6

    6. The Police Department — A Familiar Setting

    The campaign's brief but iconic chapter inside the Raccoon Police Department. Where Resident Evil 2's players spent the majority of that campaign exploring the RPD, Resident Evil 3's Jill traverses a significantly compressed section of the building — the lobby, central courtyard, and several offices — across approximately ninety minutes of campaign runtime.

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's cross-campaign continuity. Jill's path crosses Brad Vickers's reanimated zombie — directly referenced in Resident Evil 2 as a brief Leon-scenario encounter — and the chapter stages multiple environmental nods to Resident Evil 2's extended RPD exploration. The chapter ends with Jill recovering a critical mission item, the campaign's protagonist switching to Carlos, and the campaign's mid-third-act pivot committed.

  7. 7

    7. The Hospital — Carlos's Scenario

    The campaign's perspective-pivot chapter. Carlos Oliveira is the player character throughout this chapter, with significantly more aggressive combat repertoire than Jill's survival-horror loadout. Carlos's assault rifle, full-grenade-launcher mode, and military-grade weaponry transform the chapter into the campaign's most action-oriented sustained sequence.

    The chapter is structured as a defense-and-extraction operation. Carlos arrives at Raccoon General Hospital to recover an experimental vaccine for Jill's Nemesis-tentacle-induced infection. The chapter weaves sustained combat against zombie hordes with the campaign's most demanding defense-position sequence — a sustained shootout in the hospital's first-floor reception against waves of zombies climbing through every entry point.

    The chapter ends with the vaccine recovered, Carlos's growing personal investment in Jill's survival established, and the campaign's third-act setup positioning the NEST 2 facility as the final destination.

  8. 8

    8. The Underground Storage — Nicholai's Betrayal

    The campaign's third-act pivot. The Underground Storage facility is revealed as Umbrella's secondary research site, with Nicholai Ginovaef confirmed as a private Umbrella corporate operative whose loyalties to UBCS were always fraudulent. The chapter is structured as a sustained infiltration sequence with combat against UBCS turncoats, Umbrella's automated security systems, and a sustained Nemesis confrontation in the facility's central chamber.

    The chapter dwells on Nicholai's character. He is rendered as the franchise's most coldly pragmatic antagonist — neither a corporate true believer like William Birkin nor a sadist like Albert Wesker, but a mercenary whose only loyalty is to whichever Umbrella division pays his contract premium. The chapter's middle act stages Nicholai's betrayal in full, with Nicholai openly killing UBCS personnel to clear his exit route.

    The chapter ends with the campaign's deepest underground facility — NEST 2 — accessed, and the campaign's final boss confrontation staged.

  9. 9

    9. NEST 2 — The Final Confrontation

    The campaign's climactic chapter. NEST 2 is Umbrella's secondary Raccoon City research facility, where the Nemesis project has been refined into its final operational configuration. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-level interior exploration with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental design and the franchise's hardest sustained combat encounters.

    The chapter's mid-section stages the campaign's final encounter with Nicholai Ginovaef. The mercenary's death — staged at Jill's hand in a brief but emotionally weighted combat sequence — closes the campaign's central human-antagonist arc. The chapter's climactic confrontation is the multi-phase Nemesis boss fight, with the Tyrant manifesting across three distinct stage-by-stage transformations.

    The Nemesis final boss is the campaign's mechanical apex. The Tyrant's first form is a melee-heavy chase pursuit. The second form is a multi-tentacled stationary boss inside the NEST 2 lab. The third form is the franchise's signature rail-gun final stage, with Jill operating an experimental Umbrella anti-bioweapon railgun in a sustained final sequence.

    The chapter ends with Nemesis destroyed, the railgun's energy bolt vaporizing the bioweapon entirely, and Jill emerging from NEST 2 alongside Carlos as the U.S. military's sterilization missile detonates over Raccoon City.

  10. 10

    10. The Escape — Aftermath

    The campaign's epilogue. Jill and Carlos depart Raccoon City via UBCS extraction helicopter, watching the city's destruction from the air. The chapter is structured as a sustained quiet sequence with no combat, dedicated entirely to the campaign's emotional resolution.

    The chapter dwells on Jill's commitment to exposing Umbrella's role in the Raccoon City destruction. Carlos's quiet support — and his decision to formally leave the UBCS to assist Jill's investigation — establishes the campaign's quiet character pairing. The chapter's final scene establishes the franchise's continuation, with Jill's eventual role in Resident Evil 5 and the broader Bioterrorism Security Assessment Alliance explicitly seeded.

    The credits roll over Raccoon City's burning silhouette, with the campaign's central question — whether Umbrella will ever face genuine accountability for what they have done — answered in the negative for the foreseeable franchise future.
