About the Game

Resident Evil Requiem is the next mainline entry in Capcom's survival horror franchise, returning to a more psychological, suspense-driven style after the action-focused turn of Village. The walkthrough captures the full game across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary — every cinematic moment, every puzzle, every enemy encounter rendered with maximum visual fidelity. Capcom's RE Engine pushes detailed lighting, fluid character animation, and dense environmental detail. The cinematic playthrough lets viewers experience the horror as theater rather than gameplay.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Wrenwood — The Hotel Investigation

    The campaign opens in October 2026, twenty-eight years after the Raccoon City Destruction Incident. FBI intelligence analyst Grace Ashcroft is dispatched by her supervisor, Special Agent Nathan Dempsey, to investigate the latest in a string of mysterious deaths — the fifth victim found in the abandoned Wrenwood Hotel, the same hotel where Grace's mother, investigative journalist Alyssa Ashcroft, was murdered eight years ago. Dempsey frames the assignment as an opportunity for Grace to face the trauma she has spent the intervening years burying.

    The opening hour is structured as a sustained survival-horror sequence rendered in first-person perspective. Grace navigates the hotel's narrow alleyways, employee entrance, kitchen, and central lobby with a limited flashlight, no firearm, and an inventory tightly constrained by the franchise's classic survival-horror logic. The chapter dwells on her growing recognition that the entire crime scene has been staged to lure her — photos of herself pinned throughout the rooms, the door to her old Room 204 unlocked, and her mother's case files arranged in deliberate sequence.

    The chapter's pivot is the transformation of Officer Norman Cole. The local police officer who guided Grace into the hotel is revealed to be infected with a mutated T-Virus strain. His sudden zombification and shotgun-armed pursuit through the hotel's upper floors stages the campaign's first sustained combat sequence, with Grace defending herself through environmental kills rather than firearms. The chapter ends with Grace cornered in the upstairs lounge by a man sitting by the fireplace — Victor Gideon, the former Umbrella scientist who has been hunting her for years. Grace is choked unconscious. The chapter cuts to black.

  2. 2

    2. Wrenwood — Leon's Pursuit

    The campaign's perspective pivots to Leon S. Kennedy, now a senior DSO agent who has himself contracted Raccoon City Syndrome — the lingering T-Virus mutation that is killing former Raccoon City survivors across the United States. Leon is investigating the same string of deaths from a different angle, supported remotely by Sherry Birkin — the young girl Leon rescued from Raccoon City in 1998, now a fellow DSO agent and his current field operations support.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained third-person action sequence through Wrenwood's streets at night, with Leon's signature combat repertoire — Glock pistol, melee finishers, the franchise's iconic roundhouse kick — fully introduced. The chapter introduces the campaign's central twist on the Resident Evil 4 formula: Leon's sections are deliberately staged as breather episodes between Grace's survival-horror passages, with significantly higher ammunition availability, faster combat tempo, and the unlimited-use combat hatchet that becomes Leon's signature flashy-finisher tool.

    The chapter's pivot is Leon spotting Victor Gideon carrying an unconscious Grace across a Wrenwood street. Gideon, attempting to escape with his prize, fires a tranquilizer-dart gun at multiple civilians, infecting them with the T-Virus and triggering a small-scale outbreak. The chapter ends with Leon containing the outbreak, ordering Sherry to quarantine the city, losing Gideon in the chaos, and receiving Sherry's tip that Victor is likely heading for the Rhodes Hill Chronic Care Center — a long-term care facility formerly funded by the Spencer Foundation and now under Gideon's private ownership.

  3. 3

    3. Rhodes Hill — Grace Awakens

    The campaign's most acclaimed early-game survival-horror sequence. Grace awakens in the East Wing of the Rhodes Hill Chronic Care Center, strapped to a gurney with an IV draining her blood into a collection apparatus. Gideon has been administering drugs and testing her blood across an undefined period of captivity, confirming she is the "key" he has been hunting since her teenage years.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained escape-and-exploration sequence rendered in first-person perspective. Grace uses the IV line and broken shards from a blood container to free herself from her restraints, then navigates the care center's increasingly impossible architecture — long, dimly lit corridors, abandoned patient rooms, and the unsettling residue of decades of human experimentation. The chapter dwells on the Rhodes Hill aesthetic, frequently compared by critics to Resident Evil 2's Raccoon Police Department: institutional decay, paper files scattered across desks, and the constant suggestion that the patients were never long-term residents in any conventional medical sense.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's primary stalking antagonist: The Girl — a creature in a hospital gown with wavy white hair who pursues Grace through the East Wing's chambers. The Girl is mechanically distinct from any prior franchise antagonist, with a tracking system that responds to Grace's movement patterns and a near-invincibility that forces stealth-and-flee combat. The chapter ends with Grace lifting the East Wing's segregating bars, attempting to crawl through the resulting gap, and being thrown against a wall by the Girl before an unseen presence drags the creature away.

  4. 4

    4. Rhodes Hill — Leon Arrives

    A perspective shift back to Leon, now arriving at the Rhodes Hill Chronic Care Center in pursuit of Gideon. The chapter opens with Leon greeted at the facility's reception by a nurse who informs him that "Dr. Gideon has been expecting him" — the first overt signal that the entire pursuit has been an elaborate trap.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained third-person action sequence through the care center's West Wing Hospital Wards. Leon's exploration is interrupted by the facility's Code 6 alarm — a coordinated bioweapon release that infects the staff and patients in real time. The chapter weaves combat against the newly transformed Rhodes Hill zombies (significantly faster and more aggressive than the franchise's classic variants) with environmental investigation of Gideon's research records.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central horror conceit: many of Rhodes Hill's "patients" had been long-term human test subjects, with their final cognitive fragments occasionally manifesting in mid-attack — zombies beg Leon to kill them, apologize for their aggression, or warn him to run. The chapter ends with Leon fighting his way deeper into the facility's central hub, the campaign's two protagonist threads now positioned for their first meeting.

  5. 5

    5. Rhodes Hill — The Girl and Emily

    The chapter alternates between Grace and Leon as both protagonists navigate the care center's deeper layers. Grace, escaping from the Girl, descends through the East Wing's lower levels where she discovers the campaign's most thematically loaded character: Emily, a young blind girl who has been held in the care center's experimental wing as a long-term test subject. Emily is revealed to be a cloned experimental subject — the product of decades of Spencer Foundation human experimentation, and the campaign's emotional anchor.

    The chapter dwells on the Grace-Emily relationship. Despite Grace's panicked state and Emily's blindness, the two girls' partnership becomes the campaign's most carefully written character arc. Grace, who has spent eight years isolated from human connection since her mother's murder, finds herself instinctively protective of a child she has known for minutes. The chapter weaves combat against the Girl with sustained quiet sequences in which Grace guides Emily through corridors Emily cannot see.

    Leon, simultaneously, fights his way through the West Wing toward the source of the bioweapon release. The chapter introduces the campaign's first sustained boss-style encounter against a transformed Rhodes Hill staff member, with Leon's combat repertoire fully expanded through unlocked weapons and ammunition. The chapter ends with the two protagonist threads finally converging in the care center's central atrium.

  6. 6

    6. Rhodes Hill — Reunion and Escape

    The campaign's mid-game pivot chapter. Grace, Emily, and Leon meet for the first time in the care center's central atrium. Leon, recognizing Grace as the FBI analyst Sherry had warned him about, immediately commits to the trio's escape. The chapter is structured as a sustained coordinated retreat through the care center's collapsing infrastructure, with the campaign's combat repertoires alternating between Grace's first-person survival horror and Leon's third-person action set-pieces.

    The chapter dwells on Leon's response to Emily. The veteran agent's relationship to children — established across the franchise via his rescue of Sherry from Raccoon City in 1998 — becomes the campaign's emotional through-line. Leon's recognition that Emily is what Sherry was, that the cycle of Umbrella's child experimentation has continued for nearly three decades, lands the chapter's thematic weight.

    The chapter's pivot is Gideon's facility lockdown. The doctor, having observed the protagonists' reunion via surveillance, triggers the care center's emergency shutters, separating Leon from Grace and Emily before they can complete their escape. Leon, before the shutters close, hands Grace a weapon — Requiem — to defend herself. The chapter ends with Grace and Emily continuing alone toward the facility's exterior, Leon trapped on the other side of the lockdown.

  7. 7

    7. Rhodes Hill — Emily's Transformation

    The campaign's emotional pivot. Grace and Emily, escaping through the care center's lower exterior corridors, are intercepted by Gideon's reinforcements. Emily is wounded in the resulting combat sequence. The chapter dwells on Emily's slow transformation — her experimental modifications, dormant for the years of her captivity, activate in response to the trauma. Emily transforms into a bioweapon variant before Grace's eyes.

    The chapter is structured as the campaign's most thematically loaded combat sequence. Grace, holding Requiem, cannot bring herself to fire on the child she has spent the previous hours protecting. The transformed Emily, recognizing Grace through the fragments of her remaining consciousness, refuses to attack her. The chapter dwells on the impossibility of either of them ending the moment cleanly.

    The chapter's pivot is the arrival of Zeno, Gideon's mysterious financier and the campaign's secondary antagonist. Zeno, accompanied by Gideon, presents Grace with the campaign's central proposition: he has spent decades pursuing an artifact called Elpis, an experimental compound that he claims can reverse the effects of T-Virus mutation. Emily, in her current state, could be cured. Grace, traumatized and exhausted, willingly accompanies Gideon and Zeno toward Elpis. The chapter ends with Leon, having broken through the facility's lockdown, arriving moments too late to intervene.

  8. 8

    8. Raccoon City — Leon's Return

    The campaign's third-act opening. Leon, tracking Gideon's transport through the night, arrives at the ruins of Raccoon City — the destroyed city he escaped in 1998 and has not seen since. The chapter is structured as a sustained third-person action sequence through the wreckage of a city the franchise's veteran audience has spent decades thinking about.

    The chapter dwells on Raccoon City's twenty-eight-year decay. The streets are overgrown but recognizable. Old shopfronts still display 1998 signage. The Raccoon City Police Department building — the central setting of Resident Evil 2 — looms in the distance, slowly being consumed by vegetation. The chapter intercuts Leon's combat against newly emergent T-Virus mutations with sustained environmental storytelling, including Leon's quiet visits to landmarks he remembers from his last visit.

    The chapter's emotional centerpiece is Leon's reflection on his Raccoon City Syndrome. The disease that is slowly killing him is itself a residue of the events of 1998 — the city is killing him from twenty-eight years away. The chapter ends with Leon reaching the entrance to the ARK Laboratory, the Umbrella facility buried beneath Raccoon City's ruins where Gideon and Zeno have taken Grace and Emily.

  9. 9

    9. The ARK — Approach and Infiltration

    The campaign's fourth and final main area. The ARK Laboratory is Umbrella's most classified research facility, buried beneath Raccoon City for the explicit purpose of surviving the city's destruction in 1998. The chapter is structured as a sustained vertical descent through the facility's exterior security infrastructure, with the campaign's hardest sustained combat encounters.

    The chapter introduces several of the campaign's signature enemies in their highest density — the Spider variant that has terrified Grace's first-person sections, the upgraded Rhodes Hill zombies, and the new ARK-exclusive bioweapon variants. The combat alternates between Leon and Grace's perspectives in increasingly rapid succession, with the campaign's two protagonist threads now operating in parallel toward the same destination.

    The chapter's central reveal comes through Grace's exploration of the ARK's interior records. Victor Gideon is revealed to be more than a former Umbrella scientist — he has been pursuing the will of Oswell E. Spencer, Umbrella's deceased founder, whose final research project sought to create a perfect human host for an experimental bioweapon hybrid. Grace's mother was killed by Gideon eight years ago. Grace herself is Spencer's intended vessel.

    The chapter ends with Grace, Leon, and Emily converging in the ARK's central chamber for the final confrontation.

  10. 10

    10. The ARK — Elpis Revealed

    The campaign's central thematic chamber. Elpis is revealed in the ARK's deepest research chamber to be not the bioweapon enhancement Zeno had presented to Grace — but an antiviral compound capable of reversing the T-Virus mutations Umbrella has produced across decades of experimentation. The chapter dwells on Gideon and Zeno's true motivation: they have been pursuing Elpis not to enhance Hell's reach but to destroy it, ending their own organization's legacy through restoration.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained dialogue sequence between Grace and the two antagonists. Gideon's confession — that he has spent decades regretting the experimental program Spencer ordered him to conduct, that Grace's mother died because Alyssa had uncovered the same regret in Gideon's private correspondence — reframes the entire campaign. The chapter's central question, whether a villain seeking redemption deserves it, is left explicitly open.

    The chapter's pivot is Gideon's offer. He acknowledges that he cannot atone for his actions but proposes that Grace use Elpis to restore Emily, cure Leon's Raccoon City Syndrome, and end the lingering T-Virus crisis afflicting the survivors of 1998. The condition is that Grace's blood — the "key" he has been pursuing — must be the activation catalyst. Grace consents. The chapter ends with the Elpis activation sequence beginning.

  11. 11

    11. The Final Battle — Gideon and Zeno's Fall

    The campaign's climactic chapter. The Elpis activation triggers the ARK's emergency lockdown protocols — fail-safes installed by Umbrella to prevent exactly the kind of corporate-legacy reversal Gideon is attempting. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-phase combat sequence across the ARK's collapsing infrastructure, with the protagonists fighting both the facility's automated security and the increasingly aggressive bioweapon manifestations that emerge as the structural integrity fails.

    The chapter weaves combat highlights across both Grace and Leon's signature combat repertoires. Grace, now fully armed and increasingly comfortable with combat, sustains the campaign's hardest first-person bioweapon encounters. Leon's third-person action set-pieces stage the campaign's most acclaimed combat sequence — a sustained pursuit through the ARK's flooding lower levels, with the unlimited-use hatchet executing the franchise's most cinematic finishers.

    The chapter ends with Gideon and Zeno killed by the ARK's collapsing infrastructure as they refuse to evacuate, and the Elpis compound successfully activated. The deus ex machina arrival of Chris Redfield's BSAA team evacuates Grace, Leon, and the transformed Emily from the disintegrating facility moments before its final implosion.

  12. 12

    12. Aftermath — A New Daughter

    The campaign's epilogue. Emily, restored to her pre-bioweapon human form by the Elpis activation, is rendered as a girl Grace has spent the campaign's runtime fighting to protect. The chapter is structured as a quiet domestic sequence in the days following the ARK's destruction, with Grace formally adopting Emily as her daughter and the campaign's central question — whether the cycles of Umbrella's harm can ever be permanently broken — answered through their domestic life rather than through combat.

    Leon's Raccoon City Syndrome is in slow remission. Sherry Birkin formally departs his support role to lead the BSAA's post-Requiem cleanup operation. Chris Redfield's presence in the epilogue points toward the franchise's broader continuation.

    The chapter's final scene is staged in Grace's apartment, with Grace and Emily watching the sun rise together. The campaign refuses to call the ending a complete victory — Spencer's experimental legacy persists, the broader bioweapon crisis is unresolved, and Grace's mother is still dead. But the campaign's central thesis — that even the daughter of Spencer's pursuit can break the cycle — lands in the affirmative. The credits roll over the franchise's signature score, with the post-launch Leon Must Die Forever DLC seeded as the campaign's bonus content.
