About the Game

Uncharted: Legacy of Thieves Collection bundles Uncharted 4: A Thief's End and Uncharted: The Lost Legacy in PS5-native form, featuring the full Drake-and-Sam-Drake adventure plus the Chloe Frazer-Nadine Ross spin-off. The walkthrough captures both games across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary. Naughty Dog's cinematic set-piece direction is rendered at maximum PS5 Pro fidelity.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. The Lure of Adventure — Settled Life

    The campaign opens with Nathan Drake, three years after the events of Uncharted 3, living a settled domestic life with his wife Elena Fisher in a modest suburban home. Nathan works at a marine-salvage firm doing legal, ostensibly safe deep-water recoveries. Elena reports for a magazine and is visibly trying to bring Nathan back into investigative journalism work alongside her. The opening sequence is structured as a sustained domestic scene that grounds the entire campaign's central emotional question: what does Nathan Drake do when he is not adventuring?

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's quiet character work. Nathan and Elena's relationship is rendered with significantly more domestic detail than the prior installments — their kitchen, their bedroom, their slowly accumulated suburban routines. The chapter introduces the campaign's central tension: Nathan, presented with photo evidence that his presumed-dead brother Sam Drake is alive, is preparing to lie to Elena about resuming the kind of high-risk treasure-hunting work the marriage was supposed to leave behind.

    The chapter ends with Nathan agreeing to meet Sam in Panama, the campaign's central deception established, and the franchise's signature adventure pulse fully re-active.

  2. 2

    2. Infernal Place — The Panama Flashback

    A sustained flashback chapter set in a Panamanian prison fifteen years before the campaign's present. Sam Drake and Nathan are imprisoned together, working a long-running treasure-hunt scheme involving Spanish pirate captain Henry Avery's lost treasure. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage prison-break sequence with sustained stealth combat, environmental traversal, and the franchise's signature setpiece climbing sequences.

    The chapter introduces Hector Alcázar — a Panamanian drug lord who has invested in Sam and Nathan's research and demands the Avery treasure as his price for their release. The chapter dwells on the campaign's central character history. Sam, the elder Drake brother, has spent his life involving Nathan in increasingly dangerous schemes; Nathan, the younger, has spent his life trusting Sam past the point where it was wise.

    The chapter ends with Sam apparently shot and killed during the prison escape, Nathan barely surviving, and the campaign's central premise — that Sam returned to his life fifteen years later — committed.

  3. 3

    3. Sam Returns — The Job Offer

    The campaign's present-day setup chapter. Sam Drake arrives at Nathan's home unannounced and explains his survival. He has spent the intervening fifteen years in a Panamanian prison, only recently escaping with the assistance of an unnamed benefactor. Hector Alcázar — Sam's former captor — is now demanding the Avery treasure within three months or Sam will be killed.

    The chapter is structured as a sustained character reunion sequence. Nathan, faced with the choice between Elena's stability and Sam's life, chooses Sam. The chapter dwells on Nathan's deception — he tells Elena he has been offered a routine salvage job in Malaysia and intends to take it. The chapter ends with Nathan departing for Italy in pursuit of Avery's auction-house lead, the campaign's central deception fully committed.

  4. 4

    4. The Italian Auction

    The campaign's first major action set-piece. Nathan and Sam infiltrate a private auction in Italy where a piece of pirate captain Avery's hidden cross is being sold. The chapter is structured as a sustained stealth-and-combat infiltration of the auction house's upper galleries, with the franchise's signature traversal across rooftops, balconies, and exterior facades.

    The chapter introduces Rafe Adler — a wealthy adventurer who has spent years funding Sam's prison expenses precisely to manipulate Sam into pursuing Avery on his behalf, and Nadine Ross — the head of Shoreline, a South African private military contractor working as Rafe's enforcement arm. Both antagonists are introduced with significantly more characterization than the franchise's prior villains, with Nadine's combat capability foregrounded as genuinely dangerous.

    The chapter ends with the Avery cross fragment in Drake hands, Rafe and Nadine in active pursuit, and the campaign's central pursuit dynamic fully established.

  5. 5

    5. Scotland — Avery's Grave

    The campaign's first sustained exterior exploration chapter. Nathan and Sam travel to Scotland's island archipelago in pursuit of Henry Avery's grave, hidden inside a cathedral on a remote island. The chapter is structured as a sustained climbing and puzzle-solving sequence, with the franchise's signature aesthetic — ancient ruins overgrown with centuries of moss and ivy — rendered with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental detail.

    The chapter dwells on Sam's deteriorating ethics. Sam, increasingly desperate as the Alcázar deadline approaches, is willing to take risks Nathan would have refused a decade earlier. The chapter's central character beat is a quiet rooftop conversation between the brothers about what they have become.

    The chapter ends with the next Avery clue recovered — pointing to Madagascar — and Rafe and Nadine's Shoreline forces arriving at the cathedral moments after the Drakes' escape.

  6. 6

    6. King's Bay — Madagascar

    The campaign's largest open-zone chapter. Madagascar's King's Bay region is rendered as the franchise's most expansive single environment, with sustained vehicle traversal across the planet's grasslands, hillsides, and inland fortress complexes. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage exploration sequence with the franchise's most acclaimed driving sequences and the introduction of multiple optional environmental investigations.

    The chapter introduces Sully — Victor Sullivan, Nathan's lifelong mentor and the franchise's most consistent supporting character — for the first time in the present-day campaign. Sully's arrival in Madagascar is staged with deliberate warmth, with the chapter dwelling on his relationship with both Drake brothers across the campaign's three-generation friendship.

    The chapter ends with the Avery clue trail leading the trio toward King's Bay's hidden harbor, the campaign's mid-game open-zone exploration committed, and the franchise's signature jeep traversal sequences introduced.

  7. 7

    7. Following Avery's Trail — The Twelve Towers

    A mid-act chapter dedicated to following Avery's elaborate clue trail across the Madagascar coast. The chapter weaves the franchise's signature climbing and puzzle-solving sequences with sustained combat against Shoreline's pursuit teams. The chapter dwells on the campaign's character work — Nathan's growing concern about Sam's risk tolerance, Elena's mounting suspicions back home in suburban America, and Sully's quiet observation of the entire situation.

    The chapter's central event is Elena's arrival in Madagascar. The chapter's most acclaimed cutscene is staged at the local hotel where Elena confronts Nathan with photographic evidence of his deception. The chapter is the franchise's most emotionally weighted single dialogue sequence, with Naughty Dog's signature voice performances anchoring the campaign's central character tension.

    The chapter ends with Elena formally rejoining the expedition under her own terms, the campaign's central character resolution positioned, and the broader Avery investigation committed.

  8. 8

    8. At Sea — The Island Approach

    The campaign's nautical traversal chapter. The Drakes, Sully, and Elena cross the open ocean to Libertalia — a pirate utopia city founded by Henry Avery and other Golden Age pirates as a post-piracy sanctuary. The chapter is structured as a sustained boat-based exploration sequence with the franchise's most acclaimed open-water visual rendering and the introduction of the campaign's central archaeological location.

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's signature sense of historical wonder. Libertalia is rendered as the franchise's most architecturally ambitious environment — a multi-tier colonial city overgrown with centuries of jungle reclamation, with the pirate captains' individual mansions visible across the city's exterior. The chapter ends with the team's arrival at Libertalia, the city's exploration committed, and the campaign's third-act setting fully established.

  9. 9

    9. Libertalia — The Founder's Mansions

    The campaign's largest sustained interior-exterior exploration chapter. The team splits between Libertalia's surviving pirate-captain mansions in pursuit of the founders' individual treasure caches and the central archive that points toward Avery's hidden vault. The chapter weaves combat with sustained environmental investigation, with each pirate-captain mansion staging a distinct architectural set-piece — the Spaniard's Old World fortress, the Englishman's Tudor manor, the Frenchman's Versailles-inspired pavilion.

    The chapter dwells on Libertalia's central reveal. The colonial-era pirate utopia was not the paradise its founders advertised. The chapter's environmental detail reveals systematic exploitation of indentured labor, slow descent into open civil war among the founding captains, and Avery's eventual betrayal of his fellow pirates for the treasure's sole ownership.

    The chapter ends with the central archive recovered, Avery's vault location confirmed, and the campaign's final assault committed.

  10. 10

    10. New Devon — Avery's Manor

    The campaign's mid-third-act spectacle chapter. New Devon is Libertalia's central colonial-era settlement, abandoned for three centuries but rendered with the franchise's most acclaimed environmental detail. The chapter is structured as a sustained multi-zone exploration sequence with the campaign's hardest sustained combat encounters against Shoreline's full mercenary force.

    The chapter's central reveal is Avery's poisoning of his fellow founders. The colonial archives reveal that Avery, having grown paranoid about his fellow captains' ambitions, systematically poisoned them at a unifying banquet — an event the chapter stages through environmental detail in the abandoned dining hall.

    The chapter ends with the path to Avery's central vault opened, Rafe and Nadine's forces converging on the team's position, and the campaign's climactic confrontation imminent.

  11. 11

    11. Avery's Descent — The Vault

    The campaign's climactic spectacle chapter. Henry Avery's central vault is staged inside the New Devon harbor cave system, with the chapter weaving sustained combat against Shoreline forces with the franchise's signature treasure-vault exploration. The vault itself is rendered with the campaign's most acclaimed environmental design — vast cathedrals of stacked treasure, ancient pirate-galleon wreckage, and the franchise's signature traversal across precarious wooden walkways.

    The chapter's pivot is Rafe's confrontation in the vault's central chamber. Rafe, having pursued Avery's treasure for years as a personal obsession, has lost interest in the wealth itself — what he wants is to be the one who found it. The chapter's central reveal is that Sam has been working with Rafe across the previous several chapters, in an arrangement Rafe has used to manipulate both brothers throughout the campaign.

    The chapter ends with Sam's true situation exposed, Rafe pulling a weapon on the Drake brothers, and the campaign's final boss confrontation committed.

  12. 12

    12. A Thief's End — The Final Duel

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Rafe Adler and Nathan Drake confront each other in a sustained duel inside Avery's central vault, with the chapter's signature combat sequence staged as a sword fight inside the collapsing pirate galleon. The duel is the franchise's most acclaimed final boss encounter, with Rafe's combat capability matched against Nathan's improvisational fighting style.

    The fight ends with Rafe's death by the collapsing treasure pile he has spent his life pursuing — a thematically loaded ending the campaign refuses to soften. Nathan and Sam escape the vault as it floods, Elena and Sully extract the surviving Drakes via boat, and the chapter ends with the campaign's emotional resolution staged on the ocean as Libertalia recedes into the distance.

  13. 13

    13. The Brothers Drake — Epilogue

    The campaign's epilogue is set years after the campaign's events. Cassie Drake — Nathan and Elena's daughter, a teenager in the epilogue's framing — is exploring her family's coastal beach house and discovering the artifacts her parents have spent decades collecting. The chapter is structured as a single sustained playable sequence as Cassie, with no combat and minimal scripted dialogue.

    The chapter dwells on the franchise's quiet generational closing. Cassie's discovery of her parents' treasure-hunting past — the journals, the photographs, the recovered artifacts — is staged as the franchise's formal handoff to a new generation. The chapter's final scene shows Nathan and Elena's adult conversation with Cassie, with the campaign's central question — whether Nathan can leave adventuring behind — answered through the quiet domestic life he has built across the years since Libertalia. The credits roll over the family's beach house at sunset.

  14. 14

    1. The Insurgency — Western India

    The campaign opens with Chloe Frazer — a recurring supporting character from the Uncharted 2 and Uncharted 3 installments — operating alone in a war-torn region of western India. The opening sequence is structured as a sustained urban-infiltration tutorial, with Chloe navigating the chapter's central city under the control of Asav, a guerrilla insurgent leader whose forces have seized political power across the region.

    The chapter dwells on Chloe's character. Where the franchise's prior installments treated her as Nathan Drake's supporting partner, The Lost Legacy foregrounds her as a fully realized lead. Her Indian-South African heritage, her complicated family history (her father's death pursuing the same artifact she is hunting), and her established working relationships with both Nathan and Sully all anchor the campaign's character foundation.

    The chapter ends with Chloe's first sustained combat encounter, the central artifact — the Tusk of Ganesh — confirmed as her target, and the campaign's central premise (an artifact hunt across the conflict-torn Indian countryside) fully established.

  15. 15

    2. Infiltration — Asav's Compound

    The campaign's first sustained combat chapter. Chloe infiltrates Asav's central command compound to recover the Hoysala disc, a key fragment of the larger Tusk investigation. The chapter is structured as a sustained stealth-and-combat infiltration with the franchise's signature climbing sequences and the introduction of the campaign's reworked combat fundamentals.

    The chapter introduces Nadine Ross — the antagonist from Uncharted 4, now operating as a freelance contractor after Shoreline's dissolution in the prior campaign's events. Nadine has been hired by Chloe as her professional partner for the Tusk expedition, a working relationship established offscreen between the campaigns. The chapter dwells on their slow, prickly partnership — Chloe is improvisational and verbally aggressive; Nadine is disciplined and quietly contemptuous of Chloe's methods.

    The chapter ends with the Hoysala disc recovered, the Tusk's location pointing toward the Western Ghats mountain range, and Asav's forces in active pursuit.

  16. 16

    3. Homecoming — Chloe's Past

    A character-focused chapter set briefly in Chloe's childhood home in Mumbai. The chapter is structured as a quiet exploration sequence with no combat, dedicated entirely to establishing Chloe's relationship with her father and his death years earlier in pursuit of the same Hoysala artifacts.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's emotional foundation. Chloe's father — a respected academic obsessed with the lost Hoysala dynasty — died in a research expedition that Chloe blames herself for not preventing. The chapter's central character beat is Chloe's quiet examination of her father's preserved study, with the franchise's signature environmental storytelling carrying the emotional weight.

    The chapter ends with Chloe's resolve to complete her father's research, Nadine's quiet observation of the entire visit, and the campaign's central character thread fully committed.

  17. 17

    4. The Western Ghats — Open Hub

    The campaign's largest open-zone chapter and one of the franchise's most acclaimed environmental designs. The Western Ghats is a sprawling mountain region containing multiple Hoysala-era archaeological sites, with Chloe and Nadine conducting parallel investigations across several optional sub-zones. The chapter is structured as a multi-stage exploration sequence, with the franchise's signature jeep traversal expanded into an open-zone driving format.

    The chapter weaves combat with sustained environmental investigation. Each Hoysala site provides historical context for the Tusk's broader pre-colonial significance, with the franchise's signature signature treasure-hunting puzzles staged across multiple optional locations. The chapter dwells on Chloe and Nadine's developing partnership through quiet jeep conversations during travel.

    The chapter ends with the central Hoysala temple complex located, the path to the Great Battle site opened, and Asav's pursuit forces converging.

  18. 18

    5. The Great Battle — Hoysala Temple

    The campaign's mid-game spectacle chapter. The Great Battle of Talakad — the legendary 14th-century clash between Hoysala forces and invading enemies — is the central archaeological setting. The chapter is structured as a sustained interior exploration of the temple complex, with the franchise's signature treasure-hunting puzzles staged across the temple's multiple chambers.

    The chapter weaves combat against Asav's pursuing forces with sustained environmental investigation. The chapter's environmental detail is one of the franchise's most acclaimed achievements — the Hoysala carvings, the temple's central battle frieze, and the campaign's most detailed archaeological storytelling. The chapter ends with the next clue recovered, the path to the deeper Hoysala vaults opened, and the campaign's third-act setup committed.

  19. 19

    6. Hidden in the Mountains — The Vault System

    The campaign's most acclaimed puzzle chapter. The deeper Hoysala vaults are accessible via a multi-stage puzzle system requiring Chloe to combine recovered Hoysala discs into a central activation mechanism. The chapter is structured as a sustained vertical exploration sequence weaving combat with the campaign's hardest sustained puzzle sequences.

    The chapter dwells on Chloe's growing investment in the historical significance of what she is doing. The franchise's earlier installments treated treasure-hunting primarily as adventure-comedy with personal stakes; The Lost Legacy foregrounds the colonial-era erasure of Hoysala history and Chloe's quiet commitment to its preservation. The chapter's environmental texture is the franchise's most thematically loaded.

    The chapter ends with the deeper vault accessed, the Tusk's true location confirmed, and the campaign's pivot toward the central confrontation positioned.

  20. 20

    7. The Persistence of Memory — Sam Drake Returns

    The campaign's perspective-pivot chapter. Sam Drake — Nathan's brother from Uncharted 4 — has been pursuing Chloe across the campaign as a parallel treasure-hunter under Asav's contract. The chapter is structured as a sustained character encounter weaving sustained combat with the franchise's signature character-driven dialogue.

    The chapter dwells on Sam's character resolution. Sam, having survived the events of A Thief's End but never quite returned to the brotherly relationship with Nathan, has been operating as a freelance contractor with deteriorating ethical filters. His pursuit of Chloe is not personal — it is professional — but his methods have escalated to violence Nathan would not have approved.

    The chapter ends with Sam's growing recognition that Asav's plans for the Tusk extend beyond simple personal enrichment, Sam's defection from Asav's contract, and the campaign's central character realignment committed.

  21. 21

    8. Partners — Nadine's Training

    A character-focused chapter dedicated to Nadine Ross's training of Chloe in advanced close-quarters combat techniques. The chapter is structured as a sustained character-development sequence with reduced combat density, dedicated entirely to Chloe and Nadine's working relationship.

    The chapter dwells on Nadine's growth. Where her Uncharted 4 appearance staged her as a coldly competent antagonist, The Lost Legacy renders her as a partner with significant character interiority. Her quiet revelation of her family history, her commitment to professional ethics over financial pursuit, and her growing genuine respect for Chloe's improvisational instincts all anchor the campaign's emotional foundation.

    The chapter ends with Chloe and Nadine's partnership formally committed, the campaign's final assault on Asav's forces positioned, and the climactic train sequence staged.

  22. 22

    9. End of the Line — The Train

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Asav has loaded the Tusk of Ganesh onto a military train traveling across the Indian countryside, with the explicit intent of using the artifact's symbolic power to ignite full-scale civil war across the region. The chapter is structured as a sustained train-set-piece sequence, with the campaign's hardest sustained combat encounters and the franchise's most acclaimed mobile action design.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's most demanding traversal sequences — Chloe moving between train cars at high speed, fighting Asav's reinforcements on the train roof, and the franchise's signature improvised-physics combat. The chapter's mid-section stages Asav's final confrontation, with the antagonist's central plan exposed and the train's cargo confirmed as a weaponized improvised explosive device intended for civilian-target deployment.

    The chapter ends with Asav defeated, the Tusk recovered, the train's catastrophic explosion prevented, and Chloe's quiet decision to return the Tusk to Indian authorities for proper museum preservation rather than private sale. The campaign's emotional resolution lands the franchise's most thematically loaded single ending — the artifact has been preserved, the colonial-era exploitation of South Asian history has been resisted in a small but genuine way, and Chloe has emerged from her father's shadow into her own legacy.

    The credits roll over Chloe and Nadine's quiet departure from India, with the franchise's signature musical cue scoring the campaign's emotional closure.
