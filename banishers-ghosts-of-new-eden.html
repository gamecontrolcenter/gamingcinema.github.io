About the Game

Banishers: Ghosts of New Eden is Don't Nod's action RPG set in 17th century colonial New England. Red and Antea are Banishers — supernatural hunters tasked with exorcising the dead. After a tragic incident at the haunted village of New Eden, Antea becomes a ghost herself, and Red must choose between resurrecting her at terrible cost or letting her ascend. The walkthrough captures the full campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Arrival in New Eden

    The campaign opens in 1695, in a remote New England coastal settlement called New Eden. The protagonists — Antea Duarte, a Cuban-born Banisher, and her partner Red mac Raith, a Scottish-born Banisher — arrive at the town having been summoned by its desperate community. Banishers are professional ghost-hunters whose work consists of investigating supernatural incidents, identifying the spirits causing them, and either resolving the spirits' unfinished business (Ascending them) or destroying them outright (Banishing them).

    The opening sequence is structured as a sustained character-introduction chapter. Antea and Red's professional partnership, romantic relationship, and shared philosophy regarding the supernatural are established through sustained dialogue exchanges across the chapter's opening hours. The chapter's first sustained combat sequence introduces the campaign's reworked combat fundamentals — Red's flintlock-and-sword melee combat alongside Antea's spectral interventions, with the player rapidly switching between the two protagonists in real-time.

    The chapter ends with Antea and Red reaching New Eden's central settlement, the local pastor's brief on the community's haunting situation delivered, and the campaign's central investigation formally committed.

  2. 2

    2. The Nightmare — Antea's Death

    The campaign's inciting tragedy. The local haunting is revealed as significantly more powerful than Antea and Red anticipated. The Nightmare, a senior-tier supernatural entity that has been corrupting the broader region's spiritual ecosystem, ambushes the Banishers at the settlement's church. The chapter is structured as a sustained combat sequence in collapsing church architecture, with Antea attempting to shield Red from the entity's final attack.

    The chapter's pivot is Antea's death. The Nightmare's killing blow lands at the end of the sequence, with Antea dying in Red's arms. The campaign's central conceit emerges as Antea's spirit manifests as a permanent companion-ghost, bound to Red and to New Eden's region until either of two resolutions can be achieved.

    The chapter ends with Red and Antea's mutual recognition of their new circumstance, the campaign's central moral framework introduced (Ascension or Resurrection as the two paths forward), and the broader investigation across New Eden's settlements committed.

  3. 3

    3. The Promise — Two Paths

    The campaign's central moral pivot chapter. Antea, now a spectral companion, offers Red two paths: Ascension (her formal Banisher departure to the afterlife, requiring the resolution of the supernatural conflicts plaguing New Eden) or Resurrection (her return to physical life, requiring the deaths of those innocent townspeople responsible for unresolved supernatural incidents in the region). The chapter is structured as a sustained dialogue sequence at the Banishers' encampment.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's central choice architecture. Each subsequent case investigation across New Eden's settlements will present Red with a moral decision — does he resolve the supernatural conflict by Ascending the responsible spirit, by Banishing it, or by blaming a living person for the haunting (thereby moving Antea closer to Resurrection)? The choices accumulate across the campaign's runtime and determine its multiple endings.

    The chapter ends with Red committing to investigate New Eden's outstanding cases, the campaign's open-world exploration loop fully active, and the franchise's signature investigation-and-judgment gameplay loop committed.

  4. 4

    4. The First Settlements — Wakefield and Salem Springs

    The campaign's first sustained investigation chapter. Red and Antea visit the smaller satellite settlements around New Eden — Wakefield, a struggling colonial village; Salem Springs, a religious enclave with extreme practices — to investigate haunting reports. The chapter is structured as a sustained open-world exploration sequence weaving combat against supernatural entities with the franchise's signature case-investigation gameplay.

    The chapter introduces the campaign's signature Haunting Cases system. Each case involves the player gathering testimony from multiple townspeople, examining physical evidence at the haunting site, identifying the spirit causing the disturbance, and making the moral choice that determines the case's resolution. The cases are interconnected — choices in earlier cases affect the testimony available in later ones.

    The chapter ends with the first several Haunting Cases resolved according to the player's chosen path, the campaign's emerging moral pattern (Red's gradual commitment toward either Ascension or Resurrection) becoming visible, and the path to deeper investigations opened.

  5. 5

    5. The Curse of New Eden

    The campaign's central investigation chapter. Red and Antea's investigations reveal that a regional curse has been operating across New Eden for decades, with the haunting incidents the Banishers have been investigating as individual cases all tied to a single broader supernatural infestation. The chapter dwells on the franchise's environmental storytelling, with the curse's origins traced through colonial-era documents, surviving Indigenous testimony from the region's Native peoples, and the slow recognition that the original European settlers brought the curse with them.

    The chapter introduces the Indigenous Banisher subplot. The region's Native communities have their own spiritual traditions that include something resembling Banishing, and Red's investigation forces him to navigate cultural negotiations the colonial Banisher tradition has not previously prepared him for. The chapter's most thematically loaded sequences dwell on the campaign's central postcolonial tensions.

    The chapter ends with the curse's central source identified, the path to its resolution committed, and the campaign's third-act setup positioned.

  6. 6

    6. The Hunt for the Source — The Old Forest

    The campaign's mid-third-act setting. The curse's central source is identified as a corrupted spiritual nexus deep within the Old Forest that surrounds New Eden. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration sequence through the forest's increasingly distorted environments, with the campaign's most demanding sustained combat encounters and the franchise's most acclaimed environmental design.

    The chapter weaves combat with sustained environmental investigation. The forest is rendered with significantly more visual detail than the campaign's settlement environments — twisting trees, supernatural fog, and the campaign's most distinctive aesthetic. The chapter ends with the Old Forest's central nexus located, the curse's broader infrastructure mapped, and the path to the campaign's climactic resolution opened.

  7. 7

    7. The Final Choice — Ascension or Resurrection

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Red and Antea reach the central source of the regional curse, with the chapter staged as a sustained boss-confrontation sequence followed by the campaign's final moral decision. The chapter dwells on the campaign's central choice architecture — Red's accumulated investigation choices across the prior chapters have positioned Antea toward either Ascension (the morally clean path) or Resurrection (the morally compromised path).

    The chapter's boss confrontation is staged across the curse's central nexus, with the campaign's most demanding sustained combat sequence. The fight's pacing depends on the player's accumulated Banisher specialization — combat-focused or investigation-focused builds produce different boss confrontation difficulty curves.

    The chapter ends with Red's final commitment to one of three possible endings: Ascension (Antea formally departs to the afterlife, the curse is fully resolved, the colonial New Eden is set on a path toward genuine peace), Resurrection (Antea returns to physical life, the curse is incompletely resolved, and the campaign's moral framework is permanently fractured), or Failure (Antea is permanently lost, with the campaign's epilogue staged as Red's quiet defeat).

  8. 8

    8. Epilogue — The Final Settlement

    The campaign's epilogue chapter is staged in the days following the central confrontation. Red's accumulated choices determine the epilogue's specific configuration — which settlements survived, which families were judged, which broader supernatural ecosystem has been restored. The chapter is structured as a quiet exploration sequence with no combat, dedicated entirely to the campaign's emotional resolutions.

    The chapter dwells on the campaign's thematic resolutions. The Banisher tradition's central ethical framework — that supernatural intervention requires the practitioner to make moral judgments that cannot be unmade — is foregrounded across the epilogue's final dialogue exchanges. The chapter ends with Red's quiet departure from New Eden (alone or alongside resurrected Antea, depending on the player's chosen ending), the credits rolling over the surviving settlement's slow restoration to ordinary life.

    The campaign's central question — whether the Banisher tradition's moral burden can be honored in a colonial context that has corrupted its practitioners — is answered through the player's accumulated choices rather than through any single ending sequence.
