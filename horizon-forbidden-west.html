About the Game

Horizon Forbidden West is Guerrilla Games' sequel set six months after Zero Dawn. Aloy travels west across post-apocalyptic North America (modern California, Nevada, and Utah) to find the source of a deadly red blight killing biospheres and confront the Far Zenith rogue AI faction. The walkthrough captures the full campaign including the Burning Shores DLC across three parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. Reach for the Stars

    The campaign opens six months after the events of Horizon Zero Dawn, with Aloy working alone in the Carja capital of Meridian to restore GAIA, the broken terraforming AI that holds the only chance of stabilizing the dying biosphere. The opening hour is structured as a tense reverse-mission — Aloy has spent half a year hunting GAIA's surviving subordinate functions across the Old World ruins, and the chapter dramatizes the cost of that obsession on her relationships with Erend, Varl, and the people of the Sundom.

    The chapter's central reveal is that the Faro Plague — the unrelenting machine-virus that ended the old world — has somehow reactivated. Crops are failing, animals are dying, and entire biomes are turning to red rot. Aloy's investigation in the ruins of San Francisco confirms that the only surviving backup of GAIA is hidden somewhere in the Forbidden West, the unmapped lands beyond the Sundom's western border. The chapter's exploration loop weaves through the wreckage of a U.S. Army base and ends with the introduction of new Tenakth-marked machines.

    The chapter closes with Aloy departing Meridian alone, declining her allies' offers to accompany her. The frontier opens. The first new biome unlocks. The campaign's central tension — Aloy's drive to save the world alone versus her steadily growing dependence on the people around her — is fully seeded.

  2. 2

    2. To the Brink

    The first sustained chapter in the Daunt, the canyon-and-forest gateway region of the Forbidden West. Aloy's arrival in the Daunt introduces the campaign's expanded machine bestiary — Bristlebacks, Burrowers, and the early Tenakth scout patrols that mark the borderlands. The chapter is structured as a survival-hike through deteriorating terrain, with the red rot visibly accelerating across the canyon floor.

    The chapter's central encounter is the reunion with Varl, the Nora warrior who has been searching for Aloy since her departure from the Sundom. Varl's introduction to the campaign anchors the first of several allies who will eventually establish the Base, the campaign's hub fortress. The chapter also introduces the Daunt's machine-cauldron pursuit — a mid-tier dungeon weaving puzzle traversal and a mini-boss encounter against a Slitherfang.

    The chapter ends with Aloy and Varl reaching Chainscrape, the Daunt's frontier outpost, and beginning the politically loaded transition into Tenakth territory.

  3. 3

    3. The Embassy

    The campaign's first major political chapter. Aloy and Varl arrive in Barren Light, a Tenakth border fortress, on a diplomatic mission to negotiate passage through the desert and grasslands beyond. The Tenakth are introduced as a tri-clan warrior nation — Desert, Sky, and Lowland — with a centralized command structure under Chief Hekarro, the Sky Clan leader who serves as the unifying figure. The chapter dwells on the cultural texture of the Tenakth: their war-paint, their machine-bone armor, their veneration of pre-collapse military leaders whose names they have preserved across centuries.

    The chapter's central event is the Tenakth-Carja diplomatic crisis that nearly collapses the embassy. A faction of Lowland Clan officers, suspicious of Aloy's intentions, attempts to detain her on accusations of Eclipse-style sabotage. The chapter pivots into the campaign's first major machine ambush, with Aloy and Varl breaking out of the fortress under fire from corrupted Tenakth-loyal machines.

    The chapter ends with Hekarro arriving in person, granting Aloy a temporary diplomatic passage in exchange for her promise to investigate the corruption events plaguing the Tenakth heartland. The frontier opens further. The campaign's political dimension is now fully active.

  4. 4

    4. Death's Door

    The chapter that transforms the campaign's stakes from regional crisis to existential threat. Aloy follows a lead to Cradle of Echoes, an Old World facility buried beneath the Tenakth Memorial Grove, where she expects to find a GAIA subordinate function called DEMETER. The dungeon descent is one of the campaign's most atmospheric — the facility is a half-flooded biological research station, with bioluminescent algae lighting the corridors and the corpse of a pre-collapse scientist documenting the slow collapse of the project.

    The chapter's central reveal is Far Zenith. The facility's records expose a previously unknown faction of pre-collapse human survivors — billionaire transhumanists who escaped the Faro Plague aboard a colony ship before the apocalypse, and who have now returned to Earth a thousand years later. The Zeniths are introduced as the campaign's true antagonists. Their motivations, their technology, and their relationship to GAIA are seeded in this chapter's logs and audio recordings.

    The chapter closes with the first encounter against a Zenith Specter — a machine guardian dispatched by Far Zenith to prevent Aloy from recovering DEMETER. The fight is staged as a tutorial in the campaign's hardest enemy class, and Aloy barely survives.

  5. 5

    5. The Sea of Sands

    The chapter that opens the Tenakth Desert Clan region in full. Aloy travels south to Scalding Spear, the desert capital, in pursuit of POSEIDON, the GAIA subordinate function buried somewhere beneath the dunes. The desert biome is rendered as the campaign's most visually punishing — heat distortion, sandstorms, and machine-burial mechanics that turn the open landscape into a constantly shifting battlefield.

    The chapter's central character introduction is Kotallo, a Desert Clan marshal who has lost his arm in a recent Regalla-led attack on Tenakth loyalists. Kotallo joins Aloy's growing party as the campaign's second major Tenakth ally, and his arc — about reclaiming honor through service rather than combat — anchors the chapter's emotional core.

    The chapter's set-piece is the Cauldron MU infiltration, the largest machine-factory dungeon in the desert region. The cauldron's central chamber introduces the Slaughterspine machine class — apex predators that will reappear throughout the late campaign. The chapter closes with POSEIDON's flooded subordinate facility opened, the Tenakth Desert Clan formally allied with Aloy's mission, and the Base hub fortress now fully established.

  6. 6

    6. The Dying Lands

    A mid-campaign chapter that explores the Tenakth Lowlands and the cultural center of Plainsong. The Lowlands biome is the campaign's most temperate and densely populated region — terraced farms, riverside villages, and the Tenakth ceremonial city of Plainsong itself. The chapter dwells on the Lowland Clan's ritual practices, including the funereal songs that give the chapter its name, and on the political tensions between Hekarro's centralized rule and the Lowland Clan's separatist faction.

    The chapter introduces Regalla, the rogue Tenakth war-chief who has been raising an army against Hekarro. Regalla's motivation is more complicated than the campaign initially presents — her insurgency is rooted in genuine grievances about Tenakth machine-binding rituals and the cost of the Three Clans' unification — and the chapter's mid-section is structured as a political investigation into her movements.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the Tenakth Rebel Camps, recurring open-world strongholds that the player liberates throughout the late campaign. The chapter closes with the first sighting of Regalla's command structure and the campaign's pivot toward the Kulrut trials at Memorial Grove.

  7. 7

    7. Cradle of Echoes

    The campaign's longest and most narratively dense story chapter. Aloy returns to the Memorial Grove, this time at the center of an active Tenakth pilgrimage, to access the deepest Far Zenith archive yet uncovered. The chapter is structured as a multi-tiered descent through a pre-collapse research facility, with each level revealing more about Far Zenith's history, their relationship to the Zero Dawn project, and the specific Zeniths now on Earth.

    The chapter's central reveal is the identity of the surviving Zeniths: Tilda van der Meer, a contemporary of Elisabet Sobeck whose return to Earth is shadowed by personal history with Aloy's progenitor; Erik Visser, the faction's military commander; Gerard Beaumarchais, the diplomat; and several others. Each is rendered as a real person with a real motivation, not a faction of interchangeable antagonists. Tilda in particular is positioned as a future complicating presence.

    The chapter ends with Aloy's first direct confrontation with a Zenith strike team, the introduction of the campaign's hardest combat scenarios, and the realization that GAIA's restoration alone will not be enough — Far Zenith intends to terraform Earth into a new colony for themselves, and the campaign's stakes are now civilizational.

  8. 8

    8. The Kulrut

    The campaign's political climax. Aloy, having earned the trust of the Desert and Sky Clans, must now win the Lowland Clan's allegiance through the Kulrut — a ritual trial-by-combat staged at Memorial Grove, with Hekarro, Regalla's representatives, and the Tenakth high command all observing. The chapter is structured as a tournament of escalating duels and machine fights, culminating in a confrontation with a Lowland champion sworn to Regalla's cause.

    The chapter's central twist is the assassination attempt during the trial. Regalla's loyalists launch a coordinated strike on the Memorial Grove ceremony itself, and Aloy is forced to defend Hekarro from his throne room in the campaign's most chaotic set-piece. The fight ranges across the Grove's ceremonial chambers, with Regalla herself eventually appearing for a personal duel.

    The chapter closes with the Three Clans united under Hekarro's banner, Regalla either captured or — depending on the player's choice — granted clemency, and the Tenakth fully committed to the campaign's final assault on Far Zenith.

  9. 9

    9. Faro's Tomb

    The campaign's darkest chapter and one of its most thematically dense. Aloy follows a Far Zenith data lead to Thornmarsh, a swampy region in the southern Lowlands, where she descends into the personal bunker of Ted Faro — the man whose military-robotics corporation triggered the Faro Plague and ended the Old World. The expectation is that Faro died in the apocalypse like everyone else. The truth is significantly worse.

    The chapter's reveal is that Ted Faro survived the apocalypse by retreating into a private bunker, that he has spent the last thousand years in a self-imposed isolation, and that he has used experimental biotechnology to extend his life into a monstrous, fungal-bloated, barely-human form. The dungeon's final chamber — Faro himself, vast and inhuman, still issuing orders to long-dead assistants over a corrupted intercom — is one of the most viscerally unsettling sequences in the franchise.

    The chapter ends with Aloy choosing to leave Faro to his fate rather than grant him the death he begs for, and the campaign's moral weight reframed: the world is dying because of one man's choices, and the woman trying to save it inherits his cleanup work.

  10. 10

    10. The Wings of the Ten

    The campaign's mid-third-act traversal upgrade. Aloy completes the final subordinate-function recovery — AETHER, hidden inside a Far Zenith-occupied facility — and unlocks the Sunwing override, granting flying-mount traversal across the entire Forbidden West map. The chapter is structured around the override ritual itself, the escape from the Zenith strike team that arrives during the attempt, and the campaign's first sustained aerial combat encounters.

    The chapter's central impact is mechanical. The Forbidden West map is now traversable in three dimensions, hidden bases are now accessible, and the campaign's final assault on the Zenith stronghold becomes geographically feasible. The chapter dwells in the wonder of the first Sunwing flight — a sustained, scoreless traversal sequence that has become one of the campaign's most replayed moments.

    The chapter ends with Aloy returning to the Base with the campaign's full toolkit assembled and the Zenith assault ready for planning.

  11. 11

    11. All That Remains

    A planning-and-character-development chapter set largely at the Base, the campaign's hub fortress. The chapter dwells in conversations with each of the assembled allies — Varl, Erend, Kotallo, Zo, Alva, Beta — and forces the player to confront the cost of the journey on the people who have followed Aloy across the West. The chapter is the franchise's most extensive single-location character chapter.

    The chapter's central event is the campaign's most controversial death. Without specifying the victim, the chapter stages a sustained Zenith infiltration of the Base itself, and Aloy's inability to prevent everything despite all her preparation lands as one of the campaign's most thematically loaded sequences. The death reframes the final assault from triumphant payoff to grief-driven necessity.

    The chapter ends with Aloy launching the Sunwing assault on the Zenith base alone, with the rest of the alliance providing distraction across the West.

  12. 12

    12. Singularity

    The campaign's climactic chapter. The assault on the Far Zenith base is staged as a multi-stage infiltration, with Aloy fighting through layered Zenith defenses, recovered Old World technology, and the most intense machine combat the campaign has staged. The base's interior is rendered as a fusion of pre-collapse luxury and post-collapse warship, with the Zeniths themselves visible through transparent walls overseeing the chaos.

    The chapter's central combat sequence is the duel with Erik Visser, the Zenith military commander, in a multi-phase fight that demands mastery of every weapon and override the campaign has taught. The fight ends with Erik's defeat, but his death is staged as the campaign's most thematically loaded — Aloy is forced to confront the question of whether killing the Zeniths is itself justifiable, given that they too are survivors of the apocalypse.

    The chapter closes with the campaign's central reveal: a second, far larger Far Zenith ship is approaching Earth. The Zeniths Aloy has been fighting are only the advance party. The campaign sets up Burning Shores and the broader franchise's continuation with one of the most decisive sequel hooks in modern PlayStation gaming.
