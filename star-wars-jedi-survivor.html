About the Game

Star Wars Jedi: Survivor is Respawn's sequel to Fallen Order, set five years after the original. Cal Kestis is now a battle-hardened Jedi fighting the Empire as it tightens its grip on the galaxy. New abilities include dual-stance lightsaber styles (Single, Double-Bladed, Dual Wield, Blaster, Crossguard). The walkthrough captures the full main campaign across two parts on PS5 Pro at 4K HDR 60FPS with PSSR upscaling, no commentary.

Walkthrough — Chapter Breakdown

A complete chapter-by-chapter overview of the campaign. Use this guide to track story progress, locate specific moments in the videos above, or plan a multi-session viewing.

  1. 1

    1. The Coruscant Infiltration

    The campaign opens five years after the events of Fallen Order, with Cal Kestis infiltrating the upper floors of Coruscant in pursuit of Senator Sejan, a corrupt Imperial collaborator. The opening hour is structured as a sustained vertical infiltration of the Coruscanti skyline — Cal's traversal repertoire is fully expanded with the dual-blade, double-bladed, and reworked crossguard lightsaber stances introduced in the campaign's tutorial sequence.

    The chapter's central event is the Coruscant crash. Cal's stolen Imperial shuttle is attacked by Imperial Inquisitor forces during the escape, with Cal's allies Greez Dritus, Nightsister Merrin, and Cere Junda scattered across the galaxy when their ship goes down. The chapter ends with Cal alone in the crashed Mantis, deep in the frontier moon of Koboh, with no operational support network and a galactic-scale Imperial bounty on his head.

  2. 2

    2. Koboh — Greez's Cantina

    The campaign's central hub introduction. Cal awakens on Koboh in the care of Greez Dritus, the four-armed Mantis pilot from Fallen Order, who has spent the years between games operating a cantina in the frontier settlement of Rambler's Reach. The chapter is structured as a sustained exploration of Koboh's open-world territories, with Greez's cantina serving as the campaign's primary hub for the rest of the game.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's first sustained encounter with Bedlam Raiders — a faction of mercenary scavengers led by the Imperial-aligned warlord Rayvis, whose army has been excavating Koboh's surface for High Republic-era technology. The Raiders introduce the campaign's most varied enemy class structure.

    The chapter ends with Cal reuniting with Bode Akuna, a mercenary contact and family man who becomes the campaign's primary companion across its entire runtime.

  3. 3

    3. Jedha — The Hidden Path

    A mid-act chapter that takes Cal off Koboh and onto Jedha, the desert pilgrimage moon glimpsed in Rogue One. The chapter opens with Cal arriving at the Crypt of Uhrma — a hidden Jedi sanctuary that Cere Junda has spent the years between games turning into a refugee center for force-sensitives and former Jedi. Cere's introduction is the campaign's first emotional anchor.

    The chapter's central reveal is Tanalorr — a Jedi sanctuary world hidden in the Abyss between systems, accessible only via High Republic compass technology. The Hidden Path's purpose is to evacuate force-sensitives to Tanalorr before the Empire's purge reaches them. The chapter's exploration loop opens Jedha's pilgrim districts, the ruins of the former Imperial outpost, and the High Republic-era Crypt of Uhrma's deeper chambers.

    The chapter ends with Cal in possession of the High Republic compass fragment, the campaign's central mission established, and the Hidden Path's broader network introduced.

  4. 4

    4. The Mantis Hangar — Bode's Family

    A character-focused chapter that establishes the campaign's emotional core. Bode Akuna's daughter Kata is introduced through Cal's visit to the family's hidden domestic life — a quiet bunker beneath Koboh's surface. The chapter dwells on Bode's relationship with his daughter, his deceased wife's connection to Cere's order, and the family's reasons for choosing to escape the Empire entirely rather than fight it.

    The chapter's tone is deliberately gentler than the campaign's surrounding action sequences. The combat density drops; the dialogue depth increases. The chapter establishes the audience's investment in Bode's character before the campaign's third-act reversal will demand it.

    The chapter ends with Cal committed to helping Bode and Kata reach Tanalorr alongside the rest of the Hidden Path.

  5. 5

    5. The Shattered Moon — High Republic Excavation

    A mid-game vertical-traversal chapter set on the Shattered Moon, a remnant of a destroyed planet in the Koboh system. The chapter is structured as a sustained climbing-and-platforming sequence across the moon's broken surfaces, with new traversal abilities introduced — air-dashing, wall-clinging, and the BD-1 grappling line extension.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the Forest Array sequence — a vertical Imperial transmitter complex that Cal traverses from base to summit while engaging Imperial troopers, droids, and the campaign's first sustained encounter with Raider Lieutenants. The traversal density and combat intensity escalate the campaign's mid-section significantly.

    The chapter ends with Cal recovering a fragment of the High Republic excavation records, the existence of Tanalorr's defenses confirmed, and the campaign's mid-act open expansion of the Koboh open world.

  6. 6

    6. The Crypt of Uhrma — Dagan Gera

    The campaign's first sustained antagonist introduction. Dagan Gera, a High Republic-era Jedi locked in bacta stasis beneath the Crypt of Uhrma since the fall of the Republic, is revived during Cal's exploration of the deep sanctuary. Dagan's introduction is initially staged as a possible ally — a Jedi master from the era of the High Republic, fluent in Force techniques the Empire's purge erased.

    The chapter dwells on Dagan's slow revelation. The High Republic was not the idealistic golden age Cal has been taught; Dagan's order had its own crimes, its own betrayals, and Dagan himself was condemned by the Council for breaking the Jedi code. His freedom is the chapter's gift to him; his rage at his own order is the campaign's mid-act antagonist.

    The chapter ends with Dagan revealed as the campaign's secondary antagonist, declaring his intention to seize Tanalorr for himself and his Raider army, and committing to defeating Cal across the rest of the campaign.

  7. 7

    7. Nova Garon — Imperial Fortress

    The campaign's most demanding combat chapter. Nova Garon is an Imperial fortress moon serving as the headquarters of the Imperial Inquisitor's pursuit of Cal. The chapter is structured as a sustained infiltration of the fortress, with multiple Inquisitor confrontations, Stormtrooper platoons, and the campaign's first sustained AT-ST and TIE pilot encounters.

    The chapter's central boss confrontation is the duel with Inquisitor Drya Thornir in the fortress's central command chamber. Thornir's combat style is the most demanding the campaign has staged, with multiple lightsaber stance switches and Force-based environmental attacks demanding mastery of the campaign's full repertoire.

    The chapter ends with Thornir defeated, the Imperial pursuit of Cal significantly disrupted, and the campaign's mid-third-act assault on Tanalorr now positioning itself.

  8. 8

    8. The Mountain Ascent — Bode and Cal

    A character-focused mid-third-act chapter staged across Koboh's central mountain range. Cal and Bode travel together on what the campaign frames as a routine reconnaissance mission, with the chapter dwelling in their friendship — Bode's stories about Kata's childhood, Cal's reflections on his master Cere, and the slow buildup of the campaign's central trust before its central betrayal.

    The chapter's exploration loop opens the campaign's hardest puzzle dungeon — the Lucrehulk wreckage in the mountain's caldera, a Trade Federation-era warship that crashed during the Clone Wars and has been a Bedlam Raider stronghold ever since. The dungeon weaves traversal-based puzzles with the campaign's deepest single combat encounter.

    The chapter ends with the wreckage cleared, the Tanalorr compass fully recovered, and the campaign's final reveal positioned for the next chapter.

  9. 9

    9. The Bode Betrayal

    The campaign's central twist. Bode Akuna is revealed to be an Imperial deep-cover agent, planted in the Hidden Path under false pretenses with explicit instructions to recover the Tanalorr compass for Imperial use. The chapter is structured as a single sustained sequence: the revelation, the duel, Cere's death at Bode's hand, and Cal's emotional collapse.

    The Bode-versus-Cal duel is the campaign's most acclaimed single confrontation. Bode is a Force-sensitive — kept hidden from Cal across the entire campaign — and his combat ability matches or exceeds Cal's. The duel ends with Bode escaping in the Mantis, Cere dying in the Crypt of Uhrma at Cal's side, and Tanalorr's location now in Imperial hands.

    The chapter ends with the campaign's central question fully reframed: not whether Cal can save the Hidden Path, but whether he can become the man capable of stopping Bode before the Empire reaches Tanalorr.

  10. 10

    10. The Abyss — The Run to Tanalorr

    The campaign's climactic act. Cal, Merrin (returned to active partnership), and the surviving Hidden Path forces stage a desperate run to Tanalorr through the Abyss — a hyperspace anomaly that destroys any ship not equipped with the High Republic compass. The chapter is structured as a sustained pursuit through hyperspace, with multiple Imperial intercepts and the campaign's most spectacle-heavy aerial sequences.

    The chapter's combat highlights include the campaign's first sustained spaceship combat sequence, with Cal piloting a stripped-down Mantis through the Abyss's gravitational anomalies. The chapter's central tension is whether Bode's Imperial fleet or Cal's Hidden Path will reach Tanalorr first.

    The chapter ends with Cal arriving at Tanalorr seconds ahead of the Empire, the Hidden Path's refugees secured on the surface, and Bode arriving alone on the planet for the campaign's climactic duel.

  11. 11

    11. Tanalorr — The Final Duel

    The campaign's climactic chapter. Tanalorr is rendered as the campaign's most visually distinct location — a verdant Jedi sanctuary world untouched by the Empire's purge, with ancient High Republic architecture integrated into a thriving biosphere. The chapter is structured as a sustained pursuit-and-duel across the planet's central temple complex.

    The final duel with Bode Akuna is one of the franchise's most acclaimed boss fights. Bode's combat style mirrors Cal's — both trained in similar Jedi traditions, both Force-sensitive, both emotionally invested in their separate causes — with the duel's emotional weight equal to its mechanical demands. The fight ends with Bode defeated and Cal forced to make a choice the campaign has been building toward: kill Bode, or attempt redemption.

    The chapter ends with the player's chosen outcome played out in full, Kata Akuna processing the death of her father in front of Cal, and the Hidden Path's refugees beginning their new lives on Tanalorr.

  12. 12

    12. Epilogue — The Mantis Crew

    The campaign's epilogue is staged on Tanalorr in the days following the final duel. The surviving cast — Cal, Merrin, Greez, Kata, and the Hidden Path's leadership — settle into the planet's new reality. The chapter dwells on the cost of the campaign on each character, with quiet conversations between Cal and Merrin about the future of the Jedi tradition, between Cal and Kata about Bode's legacy, and between Cal and Greez about what comes next.

    The chapter's final scene establishes the franchise's third installment, with Cal departing Tanalorr on a mission the campaign deliberately leaves unspecified. The credits roll over the Mantis's departure into hyperspace. The campaign's central question — whether a Jedi can survive in an Empire that has spent two decades hunting his order — is answered, at least for now, in the affirmative.
